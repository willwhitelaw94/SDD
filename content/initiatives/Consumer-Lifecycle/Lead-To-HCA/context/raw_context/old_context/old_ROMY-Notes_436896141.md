Executive Summary 

This review presents a comprehensive assessment of Trilogy Care's client onboarding processes based on three cross-functional workshops conducted on 9 and 15 July 2025. As a Category 5 approved home care provider specialising in self-management and technology-enhanced care, Trilogy Care operates multiple complex onboarding pathways that require strategic attention to ensure successful transition to Support at Home on 1 November 2025. 

 

**Key Findings:** 

- Five distinct onboarding pipelines with significant process variations and handoffs 

<!-- -->

- Extensive manual processes creating operational bottlenecks across all departments 

<!-- -->

- Technology integration failures affecting client experience 

<!-- -->

- Compliance gaps regarding Strengthened Quality Standards requirements 

<!-- -->

- Inconsistent documentation and assessment practices 

 

**Strategic Opportunity Areas:** 

1.  **Process Standardisation and Integration** 

<!-- -->

2.  **Technology Platform Optimisation** 

<!-- -->

3.  **Compliance Framework Enhancement** 

<!-- -->

4.  **Client Communication and Experience** 

<!-- -->

5.  **Assessment Consistency** 

 

Current Onboarding Pathways 

*Sales Standard Pathway *

 

**Step 1: Consumer Created** 

- **Stage**: Home Care Approved 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: Sales, BD, APM 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Growth 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Missing or incorrect referral codes 

<!-- -->

- Re-activating creates duplicate records 

<!-- -->

- Extensive manual data entry across MAC, CRM, Portal for TCID 

<!-- -->

- Risk-based questions add little to no value 

<!-- -->

- Teams can enter commencement dates post referral expiry (system should prevent this) 

 

**Step 2: Questionnaire Sent** 

- **Stage**: Investigating Suitability 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: Sales and APMs 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Sales 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- 10 clients per week lack email addresses - sales completing forms manually 

<!-- -->

- Uncertainty over most appropriate recipient for questionnaire 

<!-- -->

- Coordinators inappropriately completing client questionnaires 

<!-- -->

- Verbal consent disputes 

<!-- -->

- Email domains sometimes reject Zoho emails 

<!-- -->

- Wrong questionnaire templates sent, impacting agreement types 

**Step 2.5: Completing Questionnaire (SM+ Only)** 

- **Process**: Internal coordination team receives requests on dashboard when coordinator managed field is missing 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- No care partner can be assigned until coordinator identified 

<!-- -->

- Save and complete links may end up in client's junk mail 

<!-- -->

- When internal coordination declines, triggers 4-5 day delay to find external coordinator 

<!-- -->

- Where questionnaire remains incomplete despite follow-up, lead becomes 'lost client before onboarding' 

 

**Step 3: Questionnaire Completed → Manually Moved to Signed** 

- **Stage**: Onboarding → Auto creates care plan in CRM 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: SAS and Sales 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Sales leaves notes in lead; SAS onboarding notes in CRM care plan (dual documentation) 

<!-- -->

- Mistakes in questionnaire form create issues generating CRM care plan 

<!-- -->

- HCA completion issues (signature and commencement date problems) 

<!-- -->

- All coordinators require quarterly HCA templating updates for indexation 

<!-- -->

- Running out of Zoho mailmerge credits 

<!-- -->

- 10-day pre-commencement visibility creates communication black hole for clients 

<!-- -->

- Moving to signed stage can trigger ACER lodgement before final commencement confirmation 

 

**Step 4: Care Plan Booking Link Sent** 

- **Stage**: New 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: SAS and Ops/Care/Assessment 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Other departments have no visibility over bookings 

<!-- -->

- SAS must manually provide times/dates to other teams 

<!-- -->

- TCID connectivity issues 

<!-- -->

- Couple meetings cannot be booked through Calendly 

<!-- -->

- **Calendly System Issues**: 

<!-- -->

- Set number of "seats" for assessment partners requiring payment for additional capacity 

<!-- -->

- Random 30-minute increment meeting slots offered 

<!-- -->

- TCIDs not connecting to care plans or moving stages incorrectly 

<!-- -->

- Incorrect TCID input creates multiple meetings with same identifier 

<!-- -->

- Two-letter TCIDs automatically moving all clients with matching initials 

<!-- -->

- Calendly bookings not matching Outlook calendar availability 

<!-- -->

- Double bookings occurring 

<!-- -->

- No SMS mobile message reminders 

<!-- -->

- Outlook-CRM-Calendly integration failures 

<!-- -->

- Random delays updating Calendly availability when removing blocks 

<!-- -->

- After-hours scheduling occurring randomly 

<!-- -->

- Meeting slots intermittently unavailable 

<!-- -->

- No calendar view available - only list format 

<!-- -->

- Unable to add surge availability for regular assessment partners 

 

**Step 5: Care Plan Booked** 

- **Stage**: Signed/Booked 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Process**: If no booking made, SAS follows up within 2 days; if non-responsive, consumer moved to Lost/Rejected and offboarded 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- TIS booking determination based on 'preferred language' field, which doesn't always indicate interpreter need 

 

**Step 6: SAS Meeting Preparation** 

- **Teams Involved**: SAS and Assessment 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Detailed Missed Meeting Workflow**: 

<!-- -->

- Day 1: Booking link email sent 

<!-- -->

- Day 4: First outbound call if not booked 

<!-- -->

- Day 6: Second outbound call 

<!-- -->

- Day 8: Third outbound call, potentially involving sales representative 

<!-- -->

- Day 10: Offboarding email sent citing legal requirement for care plan meeting 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Highly manual process 

<!-- -->

- Manual entry into MAC attaching NSAF and support plan 

<!-- -->

- Manually typing all medications and diagnoses into portal 

<!-- -->

- Chasing GP health summary (maximum two attempts) 

<!-- -->

- Issues with clients understanding SDM requirements and documentation 

<!-- -->

- Last-minute meetings create time constraints (SAS aims for 3-day advance preparation) 

<!-- -->

- Assessment team not notified when ACAT or IAT unavailable 

<!-- -->

- Twice-weekly reports needed to identify duplicate bookings or TCID issues 

 

**Step 7: Assessment Team Preparation** 

- **Teams Involved**: Assessment team and SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Assessment 

<!-- -->

- **Communication**: Teams chat via SAS and Intake Communications channel 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Assessment partners only become aware of missing documents on booking day 

<!-- -->

- Bottleneck when assessment partners haven't checked calendars in advance 

<!-- -->

- Assessment partners identify conflicts of interest (coordinator email or P1 provider email set as client email) 

<!-- -->

- Varying knowledge and qualification levels across assessment partners 

<!-- -->

- Manual copying and pasting from questionnaire, CRM, ACAT/IAT, and GP health summary into portal 

<!-- -->

- Differing processes and templating variations within assessment team 

<!-- -->

- Multiple clinicians reviewing risk profile tags and writing notes 

 

**Step 7.5: Risk Review Process** 

- **Trigger**: Assessment partner flags risk during prep, assessment, or post-assessment 

<!-- -->

- **Process**: Risk profile tag triggers senior care review → multiple senior care persons tagged → risk outcome provided → rare cases may result in client deemed unsuitable for TC model 

 

**Step 8: Assessment Meeting Conducted** 

- **Stage**: Meeting Held 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: Assessment team and SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Assessment 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Care plan meetings scheduled after start date or beyond 14 days of formal commencement 

<!-- -->

- Non-attendance issues 

<!-- -->

- Clients requiring services before commencement or meeting completion 

<!-- -->

- Self-managed clients often unprepared, requiring extensive support to understand package management 

 

**Step 9: Care Plan Written** 

- **Stage**: Care Plan Written 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: Assessment team 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Assessment team 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Assessment partners using different word template variations for portal copy-pasting 

<!-- -->

- Varying knowledge, qualifications, and experience levels 

<!-- -->

- Not capturing all required information for Strengthened Quality Standards compliance 

<!-- -->

- Unable to select which needs/risks should not display on care plan (all entries appear) 

<!-- -->

- Duplicate data entry across needs, risks, and budget sections 

 

**Step 10: Care Plan Review** 

- **Stage**: To be Reviewed 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: Assessment Team 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Assessment Team 

 

**Step 11: Care Plan Sent** 

- **Stage**: Care Plan Sent 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: Care Team 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Care partner delays in sending 

<!-- -->

- Physical care plan posting required when requested by client 

 

**Step 12: Care Partner First Contact** 

- **Stage**: Care Plan Sent 

<!-- -->

- **Teams Involved**: Care team 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Care team 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**: 

<!-- -->

- Relying on email rather than phone introductions 

<!-- -->

- Missed opportunity to support initial setup and answer client queries 

<!-- -->

- Concerns that introduction calls not always occurring, particularly for self-managed PLUS clients 

 

*Priority One Partner Pathways *

 

**Lead Referral Form Process** 

- **Form**: Lead Referral Form (basic contact information collection) 

<!-- -->

- **Process Owner**: Partnerships 

<!-- -->

- **Pain Points**:

<!-- -->

- Gravity forms creating CRM lead issues 

<!-- -->

- Inaccurate contact information provided 

<!-- -->

- Essential pathway leads not converted 

<!-- -->

- Large volume of ineligible referrals (CHSP approval only) 

<!-- -->

- Poor analytics capability 

 

**Priority One Referral Form Process** 

- **Form**: Priority One Referral Form (includes referral code and expiry date) 

<!-- -->

- **Critical Pain Point**: 80% of forms completed with P1 provider's personal details rather than care recipient information 

<!-- -->

- **Compliance Issues**: 

<!-- -->

- SAS needing to contact BD to obtain actual care recipient details 

<!-- -->

- Complaints regarding consent to review referral in MAC 

<!-- -->

- TC reviewing MAC referrals without prior client consent 

<!-- -->

- **ACQSC Complaint Example March 25**: Client transferred to Trilogy without consent or knowledge, E1 provider pressured client to sign agreement, HCP transferred without permission (client exited within 7 days) 

 

*E3 Partner Pathway (45-55% of new TC clients) *

- Community Managed 12% and 14% categories 

<!-- -->

- Coordinator assists client with questionnaire completion 

<!-- -->

- No LEAD generated – creates CONSUMER directly, bypassing lead tracking 

<!-- -->

- ~98% of E3 coordinators use physical HCA forms creating version control risks

Strategic Improvement Opportunities 

 

1.  **Process Standardisation and Integration** 

**Current State**: Five distinct onboarding pathways with significant process variations, multiple handoffs, and inconsistent ownership models. 

 

**Opportunity Areas**: 

- **Unified Workflow Design**: Standardise core onboarding steps across all pipelines while maintaining necessary pathway-specific variations 

<!-- -->

- **Handoff Elimination**: Reduce the number of team transitions and create seamless process flow 

<!-- -->

- **Single Source of Truth**: Establish unified client data management across all systems 

<!-- -->

- **Clear Accountability**: Define single process owners for each step rather than shared ownership models 

 

2.  **Technology Platform Optimisation** 

**Current State**: Multiple system integration failures, manual workarounds, and technology limitations hampering operational efficiency. 

 

**Areas Requiring Attention**: 

**CRM and Portal Integration** 

- **Current Gap**: Limited data mapping from CRM to Portal requiring manual copying 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Automated data synchronisation and single data entry point 

**Booking and Calendar Management** 

- **Current Failures**: Extensive Calendly integration issues affecting client experience and significant resourcing from SAS 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Reliable, flexible booking system supporting various meeting types and provider schedules 

**Communication Systems** 

- **Current Limitations**: Email deliverability issues, SMS capability, limited automation 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Multi-channel communication platform with automated workflows 

**Document Management** 

- **Current Process**: Manual document attachment and management across systems 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Automated document workflow and centralised repository 

 

3.  **Compliance Framework Enhancement** 

**Current State**: Insufficient data capture for Strengthened Quality Standards requirements and inconsistent documentation practices. 

 

**Compliance Gaps**: 

**Strengthened Quality Standards Readiness** 

- **Gap**: Current data collection not meeting SQS requirements 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Comprehensive compliance framework ensuring all mandatory data points captured 

**Consent Management** 

- **Current Issues**: Verbal consent disputes, unclear consent processes for partner referrals 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Digital consent management with clear audit trails 

**Documentation Standards** 

- **Current Variation**: Inconsistent templates and processes across assessment team 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Standardised, compliant documentation framework 

**Risk Assessment and Management** 

- **Current Process**: Multiple clinicians reviewing same risk profiles, inconsistent flagging 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Streamlined risk assessment with clear escalation pathways 

 

4.  **Client Communication and Experience** 

**Current State**: Communication gaps, limited client visibility, and suboptimal client engagement throughout onboarding journey. 

 

**Key Opportunity Areas**: 

**Client Visibility and Control** 

- **Current Gap**: up to 10-day communication black hole, limited client insight into process progress 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Real-time progress tracking and proactive client communication 

**Communication Channels** 

- **Current Limitation**: Email-dependent communication with deliverability issues 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Multi-channel approach including SMS, phone, and digital platforms 

**Self-Service Capabilities** 

- **Current State**: High-touch, staff-dependent processes 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Client-enabled self-service options for appropriate process steps 

**Personalised Experience** 

- **Current Approach**: One-size-fits-all process regardless of client capability or preference 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Adaptive onboarding based on client needs and management choices 

 

5.  **Clinical Assessment Consistency** 

**Current State**: Varying competency levels, inconsistent processes, and quality control gaps across assessment team. 

 

**Improvement Areas**: 

**Competency Framework** 

- **Current Challenge**: Assessment partners with different knowledge and qualification levels 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Standardised competency requirements and ongoing development 

**Assessment Tools and Templates** 

- **Current Issue**: Multiple template variations and copy-paste processes 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Assessment templating with consistent formatting and data capture 

**Quality Assurance** 

- **Current Gap**: Limited quality review processes and outcome measurement 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Systematic quality assurance with peer review and outcome tracking 

**Clinical Decision Support** 

- **Current State**: Manual risk assessment and clinical decision-making 

<!-- -->

- **Opportunity**: Evidence-based decision support tools and standardised protocols 

 

6.  **Data and Analytics Capabilities** 

**Current State**: Limited analytics, poor reporting capabilities, and insufficient data-driven decision making. 

 

**Opportunity Areas**: 

- **Process Performance Metrics**: Real-time visibility into onboarding timeframes, bottlenecks, and completion rates 

<!-- -->

- **Client Satisfaction Measurement**: Systematic feedback collection and analysis 

<!-- -->

- **Predictive Analytics**: Early identification of at-risk clients and process issues 

<!-- -->

- **Compliance Reporting**: Automated compliance monitoring and reporting 

 

7.  **Lead Management and Capacity Planning** 

**Current State:** Inconsistent lead capture across pipelines, with E3 clients (45-55% of volume) bypassing lead stage entirely, making accurate capacity planning impossible. 

**Gap:** 

- E3 pipeline (45-55% of volume) bypasses lead stage entirely, making majority of demand invisible 

<!-- -->

- Not all new clients counted as leads in CRM 

<!-- -->

- No systematic way to project assessment team capacity requirements 

<!-- -->

- Risk of bottlenecks when demand exceeds available appointments 

**Opportunity: Unified Lead Tracking and Capacity Management** 

Implement comprehensive lead capture across all pipelines to enable proactive capacity planning through: 

**Assessment Appointment Availability Ratio (AAAR)** 

- **Formula:** Available appointments (next 14 days) ÷ Expected converting leads 

<!-- -->

- **Where:** Expected converting leads = Current "home care approved" leads × Historical conversion rate 

*Note: Need to confirm "Home Care Approved" is optimal CRM stage for this metric* 

This metric would enable: 

- Proactive identification of capacity constraints 

<!-- -->

- Data-driven assessment team rostering 

<!-- -->

- Early warning of potential bottlenecks 

<!-- -->

- Improved client experience through timely appointment availability 

 

 

Conclusion 

Trilogy Care's current onboarding processes represent a complex ecosystem with significant opportunities for improvement across multiple dimensions. The five distinct pathways, while serving different market segments, share common challenges around manual processes, technology limitations, communication and compliance gaps. Beyond compliance, our ability to scale beyond current volumes without significant manual resource increases risks constraining our growth ambitions and market competitiveness in an increasingly digital aged care landscape. 

 

**Highest Return Opportunity Areas**: 

1.  **Technology Integration**: Resolving system connectivity issues and eliminating manual workarounds 

<!-- -->

2.  **Process Standardisation**: Creating consistent, efficient workflows across all pathways 

<!-- -->

3.  **Compliance Enhancement**: Ensuring full readiness for Strengthened Quality Standards and Support at Home requirements 

<!-- -->

4.  **Client Experience**: Transforming the onboarding journey from provider-centric to client-centric 

 

The approaching Support at Home transition on 1 November 2025 creates both urgency and opportunity for comprehensive improvement. Addressing these opportunity areas will position Trilogy Care for successful transition while enhancing competitive advantage in the evolving aged care landscape. 

 

**Immediate Focus Areas**: 

- Calendly integration resolution 

<!-- -->

- Priority One consent process improvement 

<!-- -->

- Assessment team standardisation 

<!-- -->

- Compliance framework development 

<!-- -->

- Unified lead capture across all pipelines for capacity planning 

 

The detailed process mapping and pain point identification provide a foundation for strategic improvement planning, ensuring that transformation efforts address root causes rather than symptoms of current operational challenges.
