## Problem Statement (What)

Currently, the recipient portal is not optimised as a user-friendly website. For example, when a recipient logs in, their data is contained on one long, scrollable page and the most important package information is not immediately available at a glance.

## Benefits (Why)

**For Recipients:** **Improved Usability:** Simplified menu structure and intuitive navigation for all device sizes, especially mobile (~40% of use). **Faster Access to Key Actions:** Dashboard "window" to essential pages with direct links to tasks such as viewing budgets, signing agreements, and managing bills. **Increased Self-Service:** Profile updates, invoice comments, budget requests, and access to unified bills from one place. **Greater Transparency:** Clearer visibility of pending payments, care activity, and package utilisation. **For Trilogy Care:** **Higher Engagement & Retention:** More frequent logins and self-resolution of tasks reduce churn and frustration. **Operational Efficiency:** Fewer support calls for routine queries like "who to contact," payment status, or navigation help. **Alignment with SAH Compliance:** Seamless integration of Home Care Agreement signing and other SAH-mandated recipient interactions.

## Assumptions & Dependencies, Risks

### Assumptions
**Assumptions:** SAH requirements for recipient interactions (e.g., agreement signing, contribution display) are finalised and can be integrated. Core portal infrastructure supports responsive design changes without major re-platforming. **Dependencies:** Completion of Supplier Onboarding and Budget V2 features. Updated contact data accuracy to ensure communications and agreements reach the correct recipient/representative.

### Risks
Design complexity could push development timelines past SAH go-live. Significant UX changes may require additional recipient onboarding/training to avoid confusion.
