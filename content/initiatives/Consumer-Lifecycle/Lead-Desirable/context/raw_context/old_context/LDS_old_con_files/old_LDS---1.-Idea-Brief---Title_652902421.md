### **Idea Brief – LDS (Lead Desirable)**

<div class="table-wrap">

<table class="confluenceTable" data-table-width="760" data-layout="default" data-local-id="f73432c3-aeab-4b85-b472-9cd88f4fc2f5">

</tr>
&#10;<tr>
<td class="confluenceTd"><p><strong>Problem Statement (What)</strong></p></td>
<td class="confluenceTd"><p>The current lead management system (LES and LTH) handles core sales and conversion workflows but lacks the deeper visibility and CRM-style insights that enable sales and coordination staff to understand client context. Critical data — such as a lead’s history, linked relationships, attribution breakdowns, traits, and service preferences — is either missing or scattered. Without this, sales interactions are transactional rather than informed, and staff have limited ability to personalize or qualify discussions effectively.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="confluenceTd"><p><strong>Possible Solution (How)</strong></p></td>
<td class="confluenceTd"><p>Extend the existing <strong>Lead Profile</strong> to include advanced contextual and analytical layers through new tabs and data models:</p>
<p>• <strong>Linked Leads Management</strong> — Create two-way, navigable relationships between related leads (e.g., spouses or carers).</p>
<p>• <strong>Lead Timeline</strong> — Provide a complete audit trail of all updates, capturing before/after values, timestamps, and authorship for compliance and transparency.</p>
<p>• <strong>Attribution Tab</strong> — Combine <em>Sales Defined Attribution</em> (self-reported sources) with <em>Lead Generation Attribution</em> (UTM and referral data) for unified origin tracking.</p>
<p>• <strong>Options &amp; Traits Tab</strong> — Capture descriptive traits such as language, culture, lifestyle, mobility, and service requirements, enabling better service alignment.</p>
<p>• <strong>Package Tab Enhancements</strong> — Display MAC progress, approval milestones, and contribution details for a more complete lead-to-client picture.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="confluenceTd"><p><strong>Benefits (Why)</strong></p></td>
<td class="confluenceTd"><ul>
<li><p><strong>Closes the CRM gap</strong> — Enables staff to manage relationships, not just transactions.• <strong>Improves transparency</strong> — Every update and milestone is visible in the timeline.• <strong>Strengthens attribution analysis</strong> — Combines manual and automated sources for end-to-end visibility.• <strong>Enhances client matching and personalization</strong> — Traits and preferences help coordinators and intake teams tailor engagement.• <strong>Supports regulatory readiness</strong> — Complete lead history and MAC linkage aid in audits and data integrity.</p></li>
</ul></td>
</tr>

<td class="confluenceTd"><p><strong>Dependencies:</strong>• Depends on <strong>LES</strong> for lead schema, tab framework, and persistent components.• Depends on <strong>LGI</strong> for attribution data sources and standardization.• Depends on <strong>LTH</strong> for onboarding data and package creation fields.<strong>Assumptions:</strong>• Staff have workflow access to full lead profiles.• MAC data is retrievable for existing and new clients.<strong>Risks:</strong>• Increased data complexity could affect performance if timeline logging isn’t optimized.• Poorly defined traits schema could lead to inconsistent entry and reporting.</p></td>
