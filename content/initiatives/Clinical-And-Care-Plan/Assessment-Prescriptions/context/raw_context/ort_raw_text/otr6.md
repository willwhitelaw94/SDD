Summary
An initial assessment was completed with Ms and her husband at their
home on the 7th March 2024. Ms is a 72 year old woman who has a primary
medical condition is end stage renal failure as well as chronic lower back pain which limits
her standing tolerance and causes difficulties with mobilising and completing her ADL’s. Ms
 has also had a previous stroke in 2009 which has impacted her functional capacity.
Ms receives formal home domestic support to maintain the interior and exterior
of the home, however her husband provides the majority of domestic and personal care
support.
Ms is currently using a shower stool, however due to her reduced functional
capacity this does not provide sufficient back support which could increase her risk of falls
and injury when completing her personal care tasks.
Ms reported having limited static and dynamic standing tolerance due to low back
pain and muscle weakness and is only able to stand for several minutes before requiring a
seated rest break. Ms reported that she currently prefers to mobilise in her
wheelchair by self-propelling using her bilateral feet (footplates removed), however the
condition of this wheelchair is not suitable as a long term mobility aid as well as requiring
increased pressure care needs.
Ms main concern is being able to access the home in a safe manner. Ms
currently uses the rear access as this has minimal steps, however due to her
limited strength and standing ability, this is a high falls risk for Ms and also poses
an injury risk to Mr as he is required to physically assist her in getting up and down
the stairs. There is also a small step and a lip step when requiring to access the rear doors
which causes difficulties when mobilising in a wheelchair.
Ms also has concerns within the home and her ability to manouver between
rooms as there are small (1.8cm) lip steps to access the living room, bedrooms, bathroom
and also the side/rear sliding doors. Ms was observed to have increased
difficulties in manouvering her wheelchair through these access points. These lip steps can
also be identified as a trip hazard once Ms is provided with her 4WW.

care tasks.
