Occupational Performance Issues, Recommendations and Clinical Justification
Mobility aid: The client is currently using a borrowed 4WW for outdoor use; increase safety concern. The client is
currently exhibiting signs of instability while walking, and is having difficulty walking longer distances, leading to
increased reliance on a borrowed 4WW for stability. The client is currently navigating uneven surfaces that may
increase the risk of falls when ambulating at the local reserve, making a walking aid necessary for navigating
safely. The client would benefit for exploring new mobility aids in helping to improve balance and reducing the risk
of falls, enhance the client's ability to move around safely, promote physical activity and improve confidence and
well-being by allowing the client to remain engaged in daily activities.
Physiotherapy: The client is currently exhibiting signs of instability while walking, and is having difficulty in
walking longer distances, and experiences has pain in back and left knee that contributes to mobility limitations.
OT anticipates that client would benefit from PT input to support client to improve strength in the legs, core
muscles, and back to support better stability and endurance while walking, enhance balance and proprioception,
pain management and provide education on proper body mechanics and fall prevention strategies.
Dressing and Grooming: The client requires assistance from his wife in selecting appropriate clothing items each
day and needs reminders and assistance to recognize when clothes require laundering due to dementia-related
challenges in decision-making and memory. The client's NOK provides verbal prompting and reminders to assist
client with clothing choices and laundry tasks. The client may benefit from techniques and/or strategies such as
visual aids or cue cards to assist the client in choosing clothing items and daily routine for dressing and laundry
tasks to enhance familiarity and reduce confusion. Client and NOK to consider.
Seating:
12
The client was observed:
Using one hand on the couch armrest and the other hand on the couch seat to push through into a
standing position. This was due to the low height of the couch and the softness of seat cushioning; this
technique may lead to strain muscles and joints in the arms, shoulders, and back due to uneven
distribution of weight and force.
Slouching in the couch due to increase seat depth. This can lead to poor posture and back and neck pain.
Pushing through his legs and upper body to use the leg rest and recliner function on the couch. This may
lead to strain muscles and joints due to reduce strength in overall body and using excessive effort to
manipulate the recliner function can lead to increased fatigue, which may affect the client's overall
comfort and well-being.
OT anticipates that the client may benefit from an alternative seating option to assist him in improved
seating posture and standing up from a seated position.
Dietitian: The NOK reported that the client has experienced loss of appetite and weight loss in recent months.
Following this report, OT recommended a referral to a dietitian for further investigation and intervention.
