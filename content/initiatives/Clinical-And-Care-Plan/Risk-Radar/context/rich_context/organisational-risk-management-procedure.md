# Organisational Risk Management Procedure

**Source**: Risk Management Trilogy Care FINAL DRAFT.docx (Maryanne Humphries, Feb 2026)
**Type**: Organisational governance procedure — covers enterprise-wide risk, NOT client-level clinical risk
**Relationship to Risk Radar**: Complementary. This procedure governs corporate/strategic/operational risks. Risk Radar (RRA) governs per-client clinical risks. The two may integrate in future (client risk patterns escalating to org register).

---

## Objective and Purpose

Trilogy Care is committed to managing risks through effective planning, identification, assessment and avoidance or minimisation of risks to clients, employees and the organisation.

This procedure supports the implementation of an organisational-wide risk management system and processes that align risk identification, analysis, evaluation, treatment, monitoring and review.

Alignment with:
- Aged Care Act 2024 (esp. s.150, s.152, s.164, s.165)
- Strengthened Aged Care Quality Standards (esp. Outcome 2.4 Risk management)
- ISO 31000:2018 Risk management — Principles and Guidelines
- Organisational Prudential Compliance requirements

## Applicability

- All employees (clinical, non-clinical, permanent, temporary, casual)
- All third-party contractors and subcontractors
- All services nationally
- Compliance is mandatory

---

## Risk vs Issue

- **Risk**: A potential future event affecting organizational objectives, requiring anticipation and management through avoidance, minimization, transfer, or acceptance within tolerance levels.
- **Issue**: A current problem or incident impacting objectives that has already occurred. Risks can evolve into issues if not managed properly.

## Risk Categories

| Category | Definition |
|---|---|
| Clinical | Risks that relate to the delivery of clinical care |
| Operational | Risks relating to achieving operational plans |
| Strategic | Risks that may impact the organisation's strategic objectives |
| Workforce | Risks that impact the workforce within Trilogy Care |
| Financial | Risks that may have a financial impact to the organisation |
| Information Technology | Information technology related risks |
| Infrastructure | Risks relating to building and equipment |
| Project | Project-related risks (discuss with Risk Coordinator and Project Sponsor) |
| Other | All other risks not captured by the categories above |

Risk types: Strategic, Organisational, Operational, Project, Compliance.

---

## Risk Description Structure

> (Primary cause may occur) that may lead to (risk event) potentially resulting in (primary consequence or negative outcome that may impact on objectives)

Example: "Insufficient funding (cause) may lead to the project not being undertaken (risk event), potentially resulting in reduced long-term delivery of the end-of-life pathway (consequence)."

## Risk Analysis Matrix

### Part 1 — Consequence Table

5 levels: **Negligible → Minor → Moderate → Major → Extreme**

Assessed across domains:
- Trilogy Care Delivery (clinical): No harm → Preventable loss of life
- Trilogy Care Delivery (operations): Single service 1-day disruption → Multi-location service failure >1 week
- Strategic Planning: Nearly realised → Fails to meet statutory requirements
- Work Health and Safety: First aid only → Reportable fatality
- Business Operation: Manageable delays → Widespread cascading failures
- Financial: <0.5% budget / $100K → >10% budget / $20M
- Legal & Regulatory: No long-term consequences → Criminal conviction with imprisonment
- Project/Program: <2% delay → >20% delay, objectives unreachable
- Data Breach: (TBD)
- Reputation: Isolated local complaints → Sustained national/international media

### Part 2 — Likelihood Table

| Likelihood | Probability | Non-clinical Frequency | Clinical Frequency | Per Client |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Almost Certain (>90%) | Will likely occur in most circumstances | Daily | Weekly/monthly | 1+ in 10 |
| Likely (60-90%) | Will probably occur at least once | Weekly/quarterly | Every 1-2 years | 1 in 100 |
| Possible (30-60%) | Could be expected to occur | Every 1-2 years | Every 5 years | 1 in 1,000 |
| Unlikely (5-30%) | Could occur but not expected | Every 5 years | Every 10 years | 1 in 10,000 |
| Rare (<5%) | Only in exceptional circumstances | Every 10 years | >10 years | 1 in 100,000 |

### Part 3 — Risk Rating Matrix (Ordinal Ranking 1-25)

|  | Negligible | Minor | Moderate | Major | Extreme |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Almost Certain** | Medium (7) | Medium (11) | High (17) | Very High (23) | Very High (25) |
| **Likely** | Medium (6) | Medium (10) | High (16) | High (20) | Very High (24) |
| **Possible** | Low (3) | Medium (9) | High (15) | High (18) | High (22) |
| **Unlikely** | Low (2) | Medium (8) | Medium (12) | Medium (14) | High (21) |
| **Rare** | Low (1) | Low (4) | Low (5) | Medium (13) | High (19) |

**Note**: This is an ordinal ranking (1-25 by priority), NOT simple Likelihood × Consequence multiplication. This differs from Risk Radar's P × I arithmetic scoring.

### Part 4 — Risk Response Protocol

| Tolerance | Response | Definition | Register? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tolerable | **Tolerate** | Management comfortable with current level, no additional treatment. Monitor through standard measures. | No |
| Intolerable | **Treat** | Identify, prioritise, implement treatment actions to reduce to tolerable level. | Yes |
| Intolerable | **Transfer** | Transfer risk to another party (insurance, contract, etc.) | Yes |
| Intolerable | **Terminate** | Discontinue the activities leading to the risk. | Yes |

### Part 5 — Actions and Ownership by Risk Score

| | Low (1-5) | Medium (6-14) | High (15-22) | Very High (23-25) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Risk Owner** | HoD / Chief | Chief | Director | CEO |
| **Reporting timeline** | Within 1 month | Within 5 business days | Within 48 hours | Within 24 hours |
| **Treatment plan start** | Maintain current controls | Within 2 months | Within 1 month | Immediately |
| **Treatment approval** | HoD / Chief | Chief | Director | CEO |
| **Treatment monitoring** | Biannually to HoD | Quarterly to HoD | Monthly to Exec, Quarterly to Board | Monthly to CEO + Exec, Quarterly to Board |
| **Risk review frequency** | Biannually | Quarterly | Monthly | Fortnightly |

## Treatment Status Definitions

- **Not Started**: Defined but awaiting approval
- **In Progress**: Being implemented
- **Completed**: Successfully implemented, transferred to control monitoring
- **Stopped**: Ceased due to a request
- **Delayed**: Being implemented but not on track within expected timeframes

---

## Risk Closure

- Only closed with Risk Owner authority
- Risk deemed no longer a threat or relevant
- Optional 3-month monitoring phase before closure
- Good governance: submit closures to Executive-level governance meetings
- **Once closed, risks cannot be edited, reopened, or deleted**

---

## ALARP Principle

Risk is tolerable "As Low As Reasonably Practicable" when:
- Further reduction is impractical
- Cost of further reduction exceeds improvements gained
- No further treatments or controls are available
- Aligned with organisational risk appetite

## Comparison with Risk Radar (RRA) Epic

| Closure | Formal, immutable once closed | Not in scope |
