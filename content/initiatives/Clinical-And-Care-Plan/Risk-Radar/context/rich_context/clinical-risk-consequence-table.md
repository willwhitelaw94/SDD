# Clinical Risk Consequence Table

**Source**: Clinical Risk Consequence Table.docx (Maryanne Humphries, Feb 2026)
**Type**: Client questionnaire-based consequence assessment tool
**Relationship to Risk Radar**: This defines the consequence scoring inputs for each clinical risk area. Client responses to plain-language questions map directly to consequence levels (0-4), replacing the manual Prevalence × Impact approach from the initial spec.

---

## How to Use This Table

1. **Ask the questionnaire** as written to capture the client's reported experience and functional impact
2. **Select the best-fit response** for each clinical risk area based on client report, observation, and available information
3. **Assign the corresponding consequence level** (Negligible to Extreme) directly mapped to the selected response
4. **Apply clinical judgement** and select the highest relevant consequence level where descriptors overlap
5. **Document the outcome**, including the consequence rating and supporting evidence
6. **Use the rating** to inform care planning, monitoring, and escalation, alongside likelihood and multidisciplinary input

---

## Consequence Levels

| Level | Score | General Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Negligible** | 0 | No issue / no impact |
| **Minor** | 1 | Mild, managed, minimal impact |
| **Moderate** | 2 | Recurring or requiring active management |
| **Major** | 3 | Hospital care or significant harm |
| **Extreme** | 4 | Ongoing hospital/full dependency/life-threatening |

## High Impact and High Prevalence Clinical Care Risks (Aged Care Standards)

### Choking and Swallowing

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Are you able to eat and drink without any trouble swallowing or choking? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Have you had one choking or swallowing problem in the last six months that got better? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Have you had one or two swallowing problems that you manage by avoiding certain foods or using a special diet? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you had several swallowing or choking problems but haven't seen a specialist or made changes to help? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Have you gone to hospital or emergency because of choking or food going down the wrong way? |

### Continence

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Do you have any problems controlling your bladder or bowels? No |
| **Minor (1)** | Occasional accidents with bladder and/or bowel control |
| **Moderate (2)** | Frequent accidents requiring use of incontinence aids |
| **Major (3)** | Accidents have resulted in skin irritation, or wounds or infections such as urinary tract infections |
| **Extreme (4)** | Accidents have resulted in the need to be admitted to hospital due to severe infection such as bladder or kidney infection |

### Falls

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Have you stayed steady and not had any falls in the last 6 months? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Have you had one or two falls but didn't get hurt? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Have you had a few falls that caused bruises or small injuries? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you had a fall that broke a bone and needed hospital care? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Have you had a serious fall that broke your hip, leg or head and needed hospital admission? |

### Mental Health

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Are you feeling okay and able to manage your thoughts and feelings day to day? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Have you recently felt more worried, sad or confused than usual? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Have you had thoughts of hurting yourself or needed to talk to a doctor or nurse about your thoughts or feelings? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you been in hospital because your mental health got much worse or you tried to hurt yourself? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Do you need help to feel safe, calm, or supported with your mental health? |

### Mobility

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Can you walk safely without needing help or equipment inside or outside your home? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you use a walking stick or cane to help you move around your home or when outside? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Do you use a walker or need someone to help you walk inside your home or when outside? |
| **Major (3)** | Do you use a wheelchair or need two people to help you move either within the home or outside? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Are you mostly in bed and need full help to move or change position? |

### Nutrition and Hydration

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Has your weight stayed the same and are you eating and drinking well? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Have you lost 5 to 10 kilos without trying and without a known health reason? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Have you lost weight because of a new health problem? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you lost more than 5 kilos due to a serious or long-term illness? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Have you lost over 10 kilos and now find it hard to care for yourself or eat properly? |

### Oral Health

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Is your mouth feeling healthy, with no pain or problems? |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you have tooth decay or gum disease that needs care? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Do you feel pain when eating or get mouth infections often? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you needed a doctor or dentist for serious mouth problems? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Do you have many mouth problems that make eating, chewing or cleaning your teeth hard every day? |

### Pain — Chronic

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Do you experience any chronic pain? No |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you have mild pain that gets better with regular medicine like Panadol? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Do you have pain that doesn't go away with Panadol but gets better with stronger medicine from your doctor? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you seen a doctor or gone to the emergency room because your pain was very bad? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Have you been in hospital in the last six months to help control your pain? |

### Pressure Injuries and Wounds

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Is your skin healthy with no sores, wounds or pressure areas? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you spend most of your time sitting or lying down? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Do you have a wound that needed care from a doctor or nurse for more than two weeks in the last 12 months? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you been in hospital because of a serious wound or pressure sore? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Do you need help every day to manage wounds or keep your skin comfortable? |

### Sensory Impairment

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Can you see and hear without glasses or hearing aids? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you use glasses or a hearing aid to help you? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Do you still have trouble seeing or hearing even with the aids? |
| **Major (3)** | Do you need someone to help you because of poor vision or hearing? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Have you lost most or all your sight or hearing? |

## Trilogy Care Clinical Risk Indicators

### Chronic Disease Complications

*Reference: National Strategic Framework for Chronic Conditions*

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Do you feel healthy and not have any long-term illnesses? Yes / Do you have one long-term illness that's well managed and doesn't affect your daily life? |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you have two long-term illnesses that sometimes make daily tasks harder? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Do you have several long-term illnesses that often get worse or flare up? |
| **Major (3)** | Do you have serious problems with more than one body system, like your heart, kidneys, or brain? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Do you need help every day because your long-term illnesses have become very serious? |

### Cognitive Decline

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | No decline |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you sometimes feel a bit slower to think or forget small things now and then? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Are you noticing frequent memory slips or trouble focusing, even if you can still do your daily tasks? |
| **Major (3)** | Is your memory or thinking making it harder to manage your day, even though you're still independent? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Are you needing help more often with planning, remembering, or making decisions? |

### Infection

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Have you stayed free from infections over the past 6 months? Yes |
| **Minor (1)** | Have you had a small infection that got better without going to hospital? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Did you need to go to hospital for an infection in the past 6 months? |
| **Major (3)** | Have you had two infections with one needing hospital care? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Have you had several infections that needed hospital or emergency visits in the past 6 months? |

### Medication Mismanagement (Polypharmacy)

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Are you currently taking any regular medications? No |
| **Minor (1)** | Are you taking one or two medications without any problems? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Are you taking a few medicines and managing them well? |
| **Major (3)** | Are you taking two strong or high-risk medicines that need careful watching? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Are you taking many medicines, including strong or high-risk ones and need help to take them safely? |

### Dementia

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Do you sometimes feel a bit forgetful or notice small changes in mood or sleep? |
| **Minor (1)** | Are you having trouble remembering things or feeling more confused, anxious or withdrawn? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Is it getting harder to talk, remember where you are, or manage your day, and are you sometimes upset or restless? |
| **Major (3)** | Do you need help with walking, eating, or speaking and have you shown strong emotions or unusual behaviours? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Do you need full-time care to stay comfortable and safe, with support for your body and mind? |

**Dementia Stages Mapping**:

| Stage | Behaviours |
|---|---|
| Very Early | *(Negligible)* |
| Early (Mild) | Mild depression, sleep changes, repetitive questions or actions |
| Middle (Moderate) | Agitation or aggression, wandering, mood swings, suspicious thoughts or mild delusions |
| Late (Severe) | Hallucinations, severe aggression or resistance to care, sleep-wake cycle disruption, repetitive vocalisations |
| End-of-life | Withdrawal from surroundings, minimal communication, comfort-focused care needs |

### End of Life

| Level | Client Question |
|---|---|
| **Negligible (0)** | Do you have a life-limiting condition? |
| **Minor (1)** | Do you have a serious illness but feel stable and your symptoms are under control? |
| **Moderate (2)** | Have your symptoms changed quickly or gotten worse recently? |
| **Major (3)** | Are you slowly getting weaker and needing more help with daily tasks? |
| **Extreme (4)** | Are you or your loved ones preparing for end-of-life care in the coming days? |

## Worked Example — Falls

**Client questionnaire response**: The client reports having had two falls in the past six months, resulting in bruising to their arms and legs, but no fractures or hospital admission.

**Assessment and clinical observations**:
- Client remains independently mobile
- Bruising was noted following the falls
- No loss of consciousness or significant injury reported
- No hospital or emergency department attendance related to the falls

**Matching to consequence table**: Response most closely aligns with "Have you had a few falls that caused bruises or small injuries?" = **Moderate (2)**

**Clinical judgement applied**: Although the client remains independent, the presence of repeated falls with injury indicates a higher level of impact than minor falls without injury. The highest relevant consequence level is selected to avoid underestimating risk.

**Use of the consequence rating**:
- Inform overall clinical risk rating when combined with likelihood
- Trigger appropriate care planning (e.g., falls prevention strategies)
- Guide monitoring frequency and review requirements
- Support escalation to allied health or clinical review if indicated
