**Type**: High-level overview of the whole-of-client clinical risk framework
**Relationship to Risk Radar**: This email describes the vision and rationale for the residual-risk model that the Clinical Domains and Consequence Table documents detail.

---

## Framework Overview (Maryanne's words)

> This is the whole-of-client clinical risk framework that I mentioned at our meeting. It allows us to consistently identify, monitor and escalate risks across our client base while remaining person-centred, proportionate and scalable.

### How the process works

Clients are assessed across several clinical risk domains, including functional ability, clinical health status, mental health, nutrition, and safety. Within each domain, clinicians consider both the level of risk and the effectiveness of the mitigation already in place, such as care plans, equipment, supervision, clinical oversight and client adherence.

Instead of treating a single high risk in isolation, the framework determines the client's **residual risk** — what remains after mitigation is considered. These residual risks across domains are then synthesised into a **single overall risk status**, displayed as a **traffic-light indicator (Green / Amber / Red)**.

This status is visible on dashboards for care partners and leaders, with clear drivers of risk and escalation pathways where required. Clinical judgement is preserved through structured prompts and documented overrides, ensuring the system supports — rather than replaces — professional decision-making.

---

## Why this approach is better

1. **Whole-of-client, not single-issue driven**: A client with a high falls risk but strong mitigation and stability across other domains is not automatically treated as "high risk", avoiding unnecessary escalation while still protecting safety.

2. **Consistent and defensible at scale**: The same logic is applied across all clients, reducing variability and supporting consistent decision-making for a large and growing client base.

3. **Clear visibility for care partners**: Care partners see a simple traffic-light status that tells them who needs attention now, who needs monitoring, and who is stable, without needing to interpret complex clinical data.

4. **Earlier intervention and better outcomes**: Clients move to Amber or Red based on residual risk, allowing earlier, proportionate intervention before incidents occur.

5. **Strong governance and audit readiness**: Risk ratings are transparent, explainable, and linked to documented mitigation and care planning, supporting regulatory scrutiny and clinical governance requirements.

6. **Operational efficiency**: Resources, clinical oversight, and escalation forums (e.g. HRVC) are focused on clients with genuinely unmitigated or compounding risks, rather than those who are stable but complex on paper.

7. **Supports sustainable growth**: As the organisation scales, this model provides a structured, repeatable way to manage risk without relying on informal judgement or reactive responses.

---

## System-Driven Design

> This framework is designed to be system-driven rather than reliant on manual review of individual records.

By structuring risk assessment into standardised domains, defined mitigation expectations, and clear rules for determining residual risk, the process can be **automated within our CRM**. The system continuously calculates each client's overall risk status based on current assessments and documented mitigation, automatically surfacing clients who require attention through a traffic-light dashboard.

This removes the need for clinicians or care partners to periodically review thousands of individual files to identify risk, allowing clinical expertise to be focused where it adds the most value — on clients with genuinely unmitigated or compounding risks — while still maintaining oversight of the entire client cohort in real time.

---

## Key Implications for Risk Radar Development

1. **Residual risk model** replaces simple Prevalence × Impact scoring
2. **Mitigation effectiveness** (Strong/Partial/None) is a first-class input to scoring
3. **Traffic-light output** (Green/Amber/Red) is the primary user-facing indicator
4. **System-driven calculation** — auto-calculates from assessments + documented mitigation, not manual coordinator input
5. **Clinical judgement override** — structured prompts with documented rationale, not blind automation
6. **Dashboard-first** — traffic-light across entire client cohort for caseload triage
7. **Escalation pathways** — clear rules for when Amber/Red triggers review (HRVC, clinical governance)
