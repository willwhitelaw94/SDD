# Incident Reporting Documentation Review — September 2025

**Author**: Head of Clinical Governance (Marianne)
**Date**: September 2025
**Source**: `Incident Reporting documentation review September 2025.docx`

## Summary

Comprehensive review of the current Incident Reporting Form to ensure alignment with the Aged Care Act 2024, Strengthened Aged Care Quality Standards, and Support At Home Package. Proposes a revised form addressing gaps in SIRS compliance, clinical governance, and escalation tracking.

## Key Findings — Current Form Gaps

### Clinical Governance Gaps
- No risk assessment matrix or escalation guidance for reporters
- No section for notifications made (provider, Commission, police, family) or timeframes
- No system learning section (recurrence, trend monitoring, corrective actions)

### SIRS & Quality Standards Gaps
- SIRS categories not explicitly listed — embedded in generic outcome list
- No priority classification (Priority 1 within 24h; Priority 2 within 30d) — managed manually post-lodgement
- No field for police notification (required for unlawful conduct)
- No open disclosure or consumer/representative notification section

### Change, Deterioration & Escalation Gaps
- No "Has this happened before?" field for trend analysis
- No structured actions-taken checklist (first aid, called 000, notified provider, contacted family)
- No link to timeframes for action
- No escalation trail (who escalated to whom and when)

## Revised Form Improvements

### New Sections
- **Client Identification**: Full name, DOB, Support at Home ID/address
- **Trend Tracking**: "Has this happened before?" and "Part of a trend?" fields
- **Harm Classification Scale**: 4-tier system with timeframes for action
  - 🔴 Severe Harm → Call 000, notify within 24h
  - 🟠 Moderate Harm → Notify within 1 business day
  - 🟡 Low Harm → Notify within 2 business days
  - ⚪ Clinical Notification Only → Notify within 5 business days
- **Structured Actions Checklist**: First aid, called 000, contacted registered supporter, removed hazard, etc.
- **Escalation Details**: Who contacted, date/time, outcome
- **Follow-up Plan**: GP appointment, care plan update, monitoring requirements
- **Client Notification**: Was client/rep informed, date/method, feedback

### Remaining Gaps (to be handled in IMS)
- Root cause analysis and closure sign-off → managed in Incident Management System
- Clinical governance committee review → monthly governance meetings
- SIRS category identification → internal operational process (see Appendix 3)
- Priority 1 vs Priority 2 classification → policy with quick reference guide
- Police notification tracking → IMS documentation requirement

## SIRS Category Identification (Appendix 3)

### Operational Requirements
- Incident reviewer must check all forms for SIRS alignment before closure
- Priority 1 (24h): sexual assault, unexpected death, suicide attempt, life-threatening injury
- Priority 2 (30d): all other SIRS reportable incidents
- Line manager/clinical lead assigns priority within 24h of report receipt
- IMS sets automatic reminders and escalation alerts

### Police Notification
- Mandatory for suspected criminal incidents (assault, sexual misconduct, theft, unlawful restraint)
- Care Partner/Manager must confirm: police contacted, date/time, officer/station, reference number
- Documented in IMS; flagged for separate governance committee review

### Continuous Improvement
- Monthly governance review of all SIRS, priority classifications, police-notifiable matters
- Trend analysis for systemic risks
- Quarterly summary report to Board/Executive
