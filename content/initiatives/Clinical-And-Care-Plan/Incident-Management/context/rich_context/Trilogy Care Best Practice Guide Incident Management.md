# Client Incident & Deterioration Guide — First Edition

**Author**: Head of Clinical Governance (Marianne)
**Date**: October 2025
**Source**: `Trilogy Care Best Practice Guide Incident Management - draft.docx`

## Summary

Best practice guide for incident identification and response across Trilogy Care's home care services. Establishes guiding principles for a consumer-centred, rights-based approach to incident management including self-managed care models.

## Guiding Principles

### 1. Consumer-Centred and Rights-Based Approach
- Prioritise dignity, autonomy, and personal preferences
- Uphold Charter of Aged Care Rights
- Culturally safe, trauma-informed, inclusive communication
- Special attention to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, CALD backgrounds, LGBTI consumers

### 2. Timely Identification and Responsive Action
- All incidents and signs of clinical deterioration identified and addressed promptly
- Response proportionate to risk/harm level
- Escalation through established communication pathways

### 3. Shared Accountability in Self-Managed Models
- Clients who self-manage may select own workers
- Trilogy Care remains legally and regulatorily responsible as registered provider
- Service agreements must define incident reporting and deterioration monitoring obligations

### 4. Transparency and Open Disclosure
- Open disclosure for all incidents including care plan impacts
- Consumers/representatives informed of: event nature, actions taken, care plan modifications

### 5. Integration of Clinical Oversight
- Clinical review and documentation of condition changes
- Registered nurses or care partners assess whether changes require:
  - Care plan amendment
  - Specialist assessment referral
  - Escalation as reportable incident if harm occurred

### 6. Consistency in Recording and Classification
- Integrated Management System (IMS) records all incidents, deterioration, outcomes, follow-up
- Accurate classification for governance, quality improvement, and audit readiness

### 7. Preventative Learning and Continuous Improvement
- Regular analysis of incident and deterioration data
- Identify systemic risks (including from third-party arrangements)
- Drive staff training, service redesign, and resource allocation

### 8. Safe, Inclusive Environments
- Protect older people from harm, abuse, neglect, or discrimination
- Applies to both provider staff and third-party workers
- Allow anonymous reporting if required
