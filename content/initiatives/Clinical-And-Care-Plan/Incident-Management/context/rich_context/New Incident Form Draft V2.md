# New Incident Form — Draft V2

**Author**: Head of Clinical Governance (Marianne)
**Date**: Proposed from November 2025 onwards
**Source**: `New incident form draft V2.docx`

## Summary

Revised incident reporting form designed for use by care recipients, family members, support workers, care coordinators, and healthcare professionals. Uses plain language with low health literacy in mind.

## Form Sections

### 1. Client Identification (complete all 3)
- Full Name
- Date of Birth
- Support at Home ID or Address

### 2. Incident Details
- Date of Incident
- Time (approx)
- Location (client's home, community, car)
- Did this event occur while care was being provided? (Yes/No)
- Has this happened before? If yes, provide details (dates, number of times)
- Part of a trend? (Yes/No) — if yes, provide details

### 3. Who Is Reporting?
- Name, Contact number, Email
- Relationship: Care Recipient / Family member / Support Worker / Care Coordinator / Healthcare Professional / Other

### 4. What Happened?
- Free text: describe what you saw, heard, or were told. Plain language.

### 5. What Type of Incident Is It? (Harm Classification)

| Tick | Category | Timeframes | Examples |
|------|----------|------------|----------|
| 🔴 | **1. Severe Harm** | Call 000 & notify immediately; act within 24h | Heart attack, stroke, fall with head injury, ICU |
| 🟠 | **2. Moderate Harm** | Notify within 1 business day; act within 2 days | Ambulance/emergency hospital, police involvement |
| 🟡 | **3. Low Harm** | Notify within 2 business days; review within 5 days | Minor injury, first aid, GP follow-up |
| ⚪ | **4. Clinical Notification Only** | Notify within 5 business days; record in care plan | No harm, new diagnosis, planned hospital admission |

### 6. Outcome for Client (tick all that apply)

| Outcome | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Mental health crisis or serious psychological distress | Suicidal thoughts, panic attack, aggressive behaviour |
| Hospital admission (unplanned) | Sudden illness or emergency |
| Hospital admission (planned or elective) | Booked surgery, cancer treatment |
| Ambulance called but not transported | Assessed on site |
| Police involvement | Welfare check, investigation |
| First aid only | Cleaned wound, bandage |
| GP, specialist or allied health follow-up | Doctor or physio later |
| New diagnosis | Cancer, diabetes, dementia |
| Behaviour change noted | Confusion, agitation, distress |
| No injury or harm occurred | Near-miss, observation |

### 7. Actions Taken by Reporter (tick all that apply)

| Action | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Provided first aid | Cleaned wound, cold compress |
| Called 000 | Ambulance, fire, police |
| Contacted Registered Supporter | Person listed to manage care |
| Contacted Care Partner / SAH provider | |
| Reassured or comforted the person | Stayed calm, talked gently |
| Removed hazard / made area safe | Cleaned spill, moved furniture |
| Made a note in care diary / communication book | |

### 8. Escalation Details
- Who was contacted?
- Date/Time
- Outcome of escalation

### 9. Follow-up Plan
- GP/specialist appointment scheduled
- Care plan update required? (Yes/No + details)
- Monitoring requirements (wound check, mental health follow-up)

### 10. What Next?
- Was client/rep informed? (Yes/No/Not yet)
- Date and method of informing
- Feedback from client/representative
