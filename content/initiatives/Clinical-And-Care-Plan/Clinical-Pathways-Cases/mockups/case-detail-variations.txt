## Case Detail View — 5 Variations

Context: Right-slide Inertia modal showing full case info + review history.
Opened by clicking a case in the list. Read-heavy view.
Must show: case type, status, clinical concern, review history timeline,
linked incidents, linked risks, assigned coordinator, audit info.
Design principle: "Transparency" — dense data is a feature.

Tabs:
  [Overview] = Clinical concern + details (shown above)
  [Reviews]  = Timeline of all reviews
  [Links]    = Linked risks + incidents

Pros: Header card pins key info (status, next review, assignee). Tabs
      manage content density — review history separate from overview.
      Review count badge on tab.
Cons: Tabs inside a modal-inside-a-tab feels nested. Clinical leads may
      want to see reviews alongside the concern (context switching).
      Violates "Transparency" — hides info behind tabs.

      cards are scannable and visually distinct.
Cons: Sticky header in a modal may behave unexpectedly on some browsers.
      Slightly more complex to implement than simple scroll.

Pros: Most recent assessment/review at top — clinical leads see "where are
      we now?" immediately. Next action (review button) prominently placed.
      Follows APSO pattern from healthcare UX research. History at bottom.
Cons: Pushes original clinical concern below the fold if last review notes
      are long. Different from other TC Portal detail modals (custom pattern).
      May confuse coordinators expecting to see the concern first.
