# RIN Panel — Loom Walkthrough Script (V2)

> **Duration target**: 5–6 minutes
> **Presenter**: Ed
> **Screen**: `sidebar-playground.html` open in browser, Intelligence panel closed initially
> **Context**: This is the second walkthrough. V1 introduced the concept and got positive feedback. V2 shows the refinements from research and prototype iteration. Also seeds the idea that v3 will reframe the initiative more toward client experience.
> **Caveat**: Still exploration — not a spec, not a commitment.

---

## 1. Quick Recap — Where We Left Off (30 sec)

**[Screen: Overview tab, panel closed — normal portal view]**

> "Last time I walked through the first version of the Relationship Intelligence panel — the idea of a persistent, floating sidebar that gives coordinators everything they need to know about a client before they pick up the phone."
>
> "The feedback was really positive. So I took that, sat down with it again, and made some changes based on what we heard. That's what I want to walk through today."

---

## 2. A Shift in Thinking (30 sec)

**[Screen: Still on Overview tab — no panel yet, just talking]**

> "One thing I want to flag upfront. The original brief — and honestly the first version of this exploration — was very much framed around the coordinator experience. Making their lives easier, giving them tools, reducing prep time."
>
> "That's still true. But as we've been talking to the business, the lens is shifting. This is really a *client experience* initiative. The coordinator is the vehicle, but the outcome we're chasing is: does Margaret feel known? Does she feel like a person, not a ticket number? That's the north star."
>
> "We're not changing anything in v2 because of that — the tools still serve the coordinator. But as we move toward v3, expect us to start rebasing more explicitly around client outcomes. Just wanted to plant that seed."

---

## 3. What Changed From V1 (20 sec)

**[Screen: Still on Overview tab]**

> "So what's actually different? I restructured the panel based on what felt right after using it and what the research told us. Let me show you."

---

## 4. Quick Essentials + Timezone (30 sec)

**[Click the Relationship Intelligence icon — panel opens]**

> "First thing — Quick Essentials. This hasn't changed much, but there's a new addition: 'Their time.' It shows the client's timezone and their current local time — right now it says AEST, 10:14 am."
>
> "This came from a real problem. Partners sometimes call WA clients at 6am Brisbane time, not realising it's 4am in Perth. Two-hour difference. Small thing, but it avoids a really bad first impression."
>
> "Everything here is still editable — click the pencil, change the timezone, it updates the live clock."

---

## 5. Operational Context (20 sec)

**[Scroll to or point at the Alerts section]**

> "Operational alerts are still right below essentials — bills on hold, overdue management plan, active complaint. These are clickable — they jump you straight to the relevant tab. Nothing new here, it works well."

---

## 6. Personal Context — Now Open by Default (20 sec)

**[Point at the Personal Context section — Interests, Family, Dates all visible]**

> "Personal Context used to be collapsed behind a toggle. Now it's open by default — interests, family, important dates, all visible immediately. We also removed the Preferences section because preferred name, call time, and contact method already live in Quick Essentials. No need to say it twice."

---

## 7. Conversation Guide — The Big Change (50 sec)

**[Scroll to Conversation Guide section]**

> "This is the biggest structural change. In v1 we had 'Conversation Starters' — a single set of mad-lib cards. It worked, but the research told us coordinators need more than just an opener. They need a running order for the whole call."

**[Click through the Opening carousel — show 2-3 cards]**

> "So now it's a three-phase guide. First: Opening the call. Card one is a generic professional opener — 'Good morning Maggie, it's Sarah from Trilogy Care, how are you doing today?' Cards two and three are personalised — pulled from her interests and family. Click the underlined words to cycle through alternatives."

**[Click to Steering to business carousel]**

> "Second: Steering to business. These are transition phrases — natural ways to pivot from the personal chat to the reason you're actually calling. 'Now Maggie, the reason I'm reaching out today is…'"

**[Click Handling Conflict button to expand, show the carousel]**

> "And third — new in v2 — Handling Conflict. This is collapsed by default because hopefully you don't need it. But if the call goes sideways, you've got a four-step framework: Acknowledge, Empathise, Solve, Buy breathing room. Each step has two or three variants you can click through. It gives you something to reach for when you're under pressure."

---

## 8. Operational Agenda (20 sec)

**[Scroll to Talking Points section]**

> "Talking points used to be conversation starters. Now they're an operational agenda — the business items you need to cover. Bills and holds, active complaints, management plan check-in, overdue touchpoint. Think of it as your call checklist. Each one links to the relevant tab."

## 11. Wrapping Up — The Client Lens (30 sec)

**[Panel open, showing full view]**

> "So to recap v2: Quick Essentials now include timezone. The Conversation Guide is a structured three-phase call arc — not just openers. Handling Conflict gives coordinators a framework for difficult calls. And Talking Points are an operational agenda, not conversation starters."
>
> "The tools serve the coordinator. But the outcome we're measuring is: does the client feel known? That's where v3 is heading — reframing this more explicitly around client experience outcomes. For now, the panel gives coordinators everything they need to make that happen."

## Notes for Recording

- **Start with panel closed** — same as v1, show the "before" state
- **Don't skip the "shift in thinking" section** — this is important framing for stakeholders. Plant the seed that v3 reframes around client experience, but v2 doesn't change because of it
- **Timezone is the new money shot** — pause on the WA example, let the 2-hour difference land
- **Conversation Guide walkthrough** — click through all three phases, don't rush. This is the biggest structural change
- **Handling Conflict** — frame as "hopefully you don't need it" not "when clients are angry". It's a safety net, not an expectation
- **Auth rep timezone** — show the contrast between AEST and AWST when you switch
- **Keep the client lens warm** — mention "does Margaret feel known" at least twice
- **Caveat** — still "exploring", "refining", "iterating". Not "building" or "shipping"
- **No names** — don't name the research participant, the brief author, or specific coordinators
- **v3 framing** — keep it light. "Expect us to rebase" not "we're pivoting". The work so far is validated, the lens is just widening
- **Energy** — conversational, iterative. This is "here's what I've been refining" not "here's the answer"
