# RIN Panel — Loom Walkthrough Script

> **Duration target**: 4–5 minutes
> **Presenter**: Ed
> **Screen**: `sidebar-playground.html` open in browser, Intelligence panel closed initially
> **Caveat**: This is an early exploration — plenty of my own thoughts mixed in. Not a final design or commitment.

---

## 1. Where This Started (30–40 sec)

**[Screen: Overview tab, panel closed — normal portal view with sidebar open]**

> "So this started with an idea brief about Relationship Intelligence. The core problem: our coordinators have no structured way to capture personal context about clients. Rapport-building relies entirely on individual memory. Client data is fragmented across multiple screens. And we have a regulatory requirement for monthly touchpoints that we can't reliably track."
>
> "It's a solid brief. But I wanted to pressure-test it with someone actually on the phones before we started designing anything."

---

## 2. What Research Told Us (40–50 sec)

**[Screen: Still on Overview tab — no panel yet, just talking]**

> "I sat down with one of our longest-serving care coordinators — about 8 months in, making around 30 calls a day. A few things really stuck with me."
>
> "First: *'We don't necessarily get personal anymore. We literally just go straight to the information we need and drop them. It's kind of sad.'* There's a real tension between the volume of calls and having time for genuine connection."
>
> "Second — experienced coordinators have built up their own ways of managing this. They know their clients, they know the rhythms. But when someone's newer to the role, they're picking up a large caseload very quickly and there just isn't time to build that depth of knowledge before the calls start. The system doesn't give them a leg up."
>
> "Third: a significant portion of calls actually go to authorised representatives, not the client directly. And the current system doesn't help you distinguish who you're calling or what context to bring."
>
> "So the research validated the original brief but also sharpened it. This isn't just about relationship building — it's about giving every coordinator the context to have informed conversations while staying on top of the operational stuff."

---

## 3. Caveat — This Is Exploration (10 sec)

> "Quick caveat: what I'm about to show is an interactive exploration — plenty of my own thinking in here. It's not a spec, it's not final design. It's a conversation starter to see if the direction feels right."

---

## 4. The Concept — First Look (30 sec)

**[Click the Relationship Intelligence icon on the right tool strip — panel opens]**

> "So I've been exploring the idea of a floating panel — think Adobe InDesign's palettes. It opens from a tool strip on the right and sits alongside whatever you're working on."
>
> "First thing you see: Quick Essentials. Call them 'Maggie', she speaks English, she's hard of hearing — speak clearly and slowly. You know how to approach the call before you even dial."

---

## 5. Conversation Starters (30 sec)

**[Scroll to Conversation Starters section, click a blank to cycle through suggestions]**

> "The research flagged that talking points would be really useful — especially for people earlier in their journey who haven't had time to get to know every client yet."
>
> "They work like mad libs. The underlined words are clickable — click to cycle through suggestions pulled from the client's actual notes, interests, and hobbies. Three phases: open warmly, steer to business, and naturally gather personal insights you can bank for next time."

---

## 6. Persistent Across Tabs (20 sec)

**[Click between Overview, Needs, Budget, Notes tabs — panel stays open]**

> "The key thing: the panel persists across every tab. Whether I'm checking Needs, looking at the Budget, reading Notes — the Intelligence panel stays right there. No more losing context when you navigate."

---

## 7. Context-Aware — Auth Rep Switching (30 sec)

**[Click "John (Auth Rep)" button — essentials swap to rep card]**

> "Remember — a big chunk of calls go to reps, not clients. Watch what happens when I switch to the auth rep. The essentials swap: 'Calling about Maggie,' the rep's details, their preferred name, best time to reach them, contact preferences. Conversation starters hide because you don't need small talk prompts for a rep call. This came directly from that research gap."

**[Click back to "Margaret (Client)" to restore]**

---

## 8. Draggable, Dockable, Reorderable (20 sec)

**[Drag the panel by its header, then drag a section to reorder]**

> "The panel is draggable — move it anywhere. Dock it to the edge. Every section inside can be reordered. Operational-first people drag Alerts to the top. Relationship-first people put Personal Context there instead. It adapts to how you work."

---

## 9. Quick Notes + Sidebar Collapse (15 sec)

**[Open Quick Notes panel, then collapse the package sidebar]**

> "There's also a lightweight Quick Notes panel — type a note, pick a category, save. It syncs to the Notes tab and prompts you to go there to tag teams or add attachments. And if you need more screen real estate, the sidebar collapses."

---

## 10. Future Vision — Call Bridge (30 sec)

**[Check the Future toggle in the control bar, open Call Bridge, click Dial]**

> "One more thing I've been thinking about — and this is very much future-state dreaming. What if we brought the phone into the panel too? No more switching to Aircall. Dial from here, the call connects, and a live transcript flows in real time."

**[Let transcript run for a few seconds, then hang up to show auto-note]**

> "When you hang up, it generates a structured note from the transcript — key points, action items, category already set. One click to save. That's the end-state vision: the coordinator never leaves Portal."

---

## 11. Wrapping Up (20 sec)

**[Panel open, showing full view]**

> "So to recap: the idea brief identified the problem, a bit of research validated and sharpened it, and this is an early exploration of what a solution could look like. Persistent, draggable, context-aware, and designed to work the way coordinators actually work — not how we assume they do."
>
> "It's early days — I'd love feedback on the direction before we take it further. Let me know what you think."

---

## Notes for Recording

- **Start with panel closed** so the "before" state is visible
- **Don't rush the research section** — this is where credibility comes from
- **Pause briefly** after opening the panel to let viewers absorb it
- **Click through 2-3 tabs** to really drive home persistence
- **Click blanks 2-3 times** to show the cycling suggestions
- **Auth rep switch** is the money shot — pause to let it land
- **Future state / Call Bridge** — frame as aspirational, not promised
- **Keep energy conversational**, not pitch-y — this is sharing exploration, not selling
- **Caveat repeatedly** — "exploring", "thinking about", "early days", not "we're building"
- **No names** — don't name who wrote the brief or who the research participant was
- **Training sensitivity** — frame as "there isn't time to build depth" not "lack of training"; it's a volume/time constraint, not a people problem
