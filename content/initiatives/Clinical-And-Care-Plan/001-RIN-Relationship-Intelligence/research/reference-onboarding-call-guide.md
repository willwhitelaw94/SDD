# Client Onboarding Call Guide

> **Origin**: This document was created by Levi, a senior care coordinator (~8 months tenure), as a reference for junior and new care partners. It was shared during the RIN user research session on 5 March 2026. During the interview, Levi mentioned she had made a Word document with guidance for new starters — this is that document.

---

## Purpose

Ensure onboarding calls are professional, consistent, and correctly documented.

---

## 1. Prepare Before Calling

- Review referral and client profile
- Confirm approved services and budget
- Check service categories and hours
- Open client record in the portal
- Have note-taking ready
- Be familiar with tagging and Monday task process

---

## 2. Introduce Yourself

**Structure**: Name → Trilogy Care → Role → Purpose

**Example**: "Hi, I'm [Name] from Trilogy Care, your [Role]. I'm calling to complete your onboarding and discuss your services."

- Confirm correct contact
- Set expectations

---

## 3. Provide Contact Details

- Your name
- Phone number
- Email (if applicable)
- Response timeframe

---

## 4. Review Budget & Services

Explain clearly:

- Available funding
- Approved service types
- Estimated hours/frequency
- Any limits

Confirm understanding before moving on.

---

## 5. Confirm Preferences

### Scheduling

- Preferred days and times
- Frequency

### Worker Preferences

- Gender
- Language/cultural preferences
- Personality/support style

### Provider Preferences

- Preferred provider
- Request to continue previous worker

---

## 6. Identify Additional Needs

Ask:

- Specific support requirements?
- Communication preferences?
- Risks or alerts?

**Prompt**: "Is there anything else we should know to provide the right support?"

---

## 7. Confirm Next Steps

Explain:

- What happens next
- Expected timeframes
- Follow-up contact
- Any documents required

Ensure the client feels informed and reassured.

---

## After the Call (Mandatory)

### Apply Tags

- Onboarding call completed
- Budget reviewed
- Preferences collected

### Add Portal Note

Include:

- Person spoken to
- Budget discussed
- Preferences and provider requests
- Summary of discussion
- Next steps

### Add Monday Tasks

- Schedule setup
- Documentation requests
- Service coordination

Ensure correct owner, due date, and priority.

---

## Final Checklist

- [ ] Budget discussed
- [ ] Preferences recorded
- [ ] Contact details provided
- [ ] Portal note completed
- [ ] Tags applied
- [ ] Monday tasks added
- [ ] Next steps explained

---

## Design Relevance

This guide is relevant to RIN because it demonstrates:

1. **Senior staff already create structured call guides** — RIN talking points formalise what's already happening informally via Word docs
2. **The onboarding call is a natural moment to capture personal context** — steps 5 and 6 (preferences, additional needs) are exactly where relationship data would be recorded
3. **Post-call documentation is already expected** — portal notes, tags, and Monday tasks are mandatory, so adding personal context capture to this flow wouldn't feel like extra work
4. **The structure maps to RIN panel sections** — budget/services (operational), preferences (personal context), next steps (talking points for follow-up calls)
