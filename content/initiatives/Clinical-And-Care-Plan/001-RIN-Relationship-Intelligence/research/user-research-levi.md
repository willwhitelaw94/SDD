# User Research: Levi — Senior Care Coordinator

## Profile
- **Role**: Care Coordinator
- **Tenure**: ~8 months — one of the longest-serving care coordinators on the team
- **Caseload**: Not given precisely, but references having many clients and making ~30 calls a day
- **Experience level**: Senior / experienced — newer staff come to her for guidance
- **Tools**: TC Portal (notes), Zoho (actioning — inclusions, meals, amendments, check-ins), Monday.com (personal task tracking)

## Key Quotes

> "We don't necessarily get personal anymore. We literally just go straight to the information that we need, the stuff that we need to address, and it's kind of headquarters. It's kind of sad. They probably don't get... I think a lot of our clients prefer having personal conversations sometimes and having those discussions rather than just ringing and dropping them."

> "I always say you can't get attached to your clients."

> "Sometimes the phone call's not very straightforward — it can be like half an hour because I'm discussing all these different issues. They don't just necessarily want to address the questions you're asking them, they want to address everything else under the sun as well and just have some chit-chat time."

> "If I need to get off a phone call and they're chatting and chatting, I just tell them: I'm really sorry, I have a meeting in 10 minutes, I've only got five more minutes to have a conversation, and then I have to go."

> "I know my clients. I know if they're nice. So I know how to address them depending on who they are."

> "People have like misinterpretations of the job and they don't realise it's actually a very high-need job... sometimes people just think it's going to be really easy because you just talk. It's not like that unfortunately."

> "Not all of us have access to each other's emails."

> "If it was at the top, it would be quite nice — sometimes I'll be on the phone with the client not realising there's a bill on hold, so I haven't addressed it. Then I have to ring them back again because I didn't do it in the first place."

> "I don't know if I would change anything. Just because I've been here so long, I know what I'm doing. I just get on the phone and my experience carries me — I know what I'm going to address already."

> "Talking points would be good. I feel like for newbies especially."

## Themes

### Call Prep and Workflow

**How she prepares for calls:**
- Opens the Portal (for notes) and Zoho simultaneously, plus Monday.com for task tracking
- For existing clients she knows well: minimal prep — she knows what to address from experience
- For unfamiliar clients (covering for someone on leave): reads back through notes to see if anything was outstanding
- Checks the budget to understand what services are in place and whether they're correct
- Checks for bills on hold via the dashboard
- Has to open the package in two browser tabs — one for notes (typing while on the call), one for budget/bills

**Pain points:**
- Portal always opens to the consumer profile (care plan), but she needs the overview/sidebar information
- When covering for another coordinator's client, she can't search for clients outside her team unless she has "organisation admin" access
- No access to other coordinators' emails, so context is lost if they communicated via email rather than notes
- Has to jump between 3+ systems (Portal, Zoho, Monday.com) to do one phone call
- Clicking off a page by accident loses your unsaved work (known bug)
- Can't have the notes tab open while browsing other parts of the package

**Top 5 time sinks:**
1. Finding workers
2. Posting (job ads/shifts)
3. Going back and forth with clients who don't listen
4. Inclusions (especially grab rails — complex forms, need reports from professionals)
5. Overspends — educating clients about their budget limits

**Magic wand wish:** "I wouldn't want to do inclusions anymore."

### Personal Context and Client Knowledge

- She has rapport with some clients — they ask about her family, she knows about theirs
- Finds the "hobbies" section in the overview useful but says it's too small — "like four words"
- Doesn't currently document personal information unless it's significant (e.g. a parent passing away)
- Would find it beneficial to record personal details for handover purposes
- When she gets a new client, she reviews what they were granted through their assessment, then doesn't go back to overview/needs/risks sections
- Never reads the "risks" section — considers that the clinician's domain
- Cultural significance (e.g. Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander status) is important for how to address clients — this info exists but is buried and not always populated

**Key insight:** She acknowledged the value of personal context but said the volume of calls (30/day) and KPI pressure means they "don't necessarily get personal anymore" — they go straight to business. This is a systemic issue, not a personal preference.

### Talking Points / Conversation Starters

- **Positive reaction** to the concept of talking points
- Specifically said it would help newer staff: "Talking points would be good. I feel like for newbies."
- When asked about "mad libs" style sentence starters (e.g. "here's three ways you could start a sentence about the bill that's on hold"), she was receptive
- Would want to be able to **add her own talking points** as well as system-generated ones
- Mentioned she made a Word document with guidance for new starters — offered to share it with Ed
- For herself personally: doesn't feel she needs them because experience carries her through calls

### Touchpoint Compliance

- Monthly check-ins are required for all clients
- Process: Goes into Zoho open activity, lodges a check-in, closes the previous month's activity
- Documents check-in notes in the Portal using four standard questions (are they happy with services, do they need anything else, have contact details changed)
- She has seen check-in reminders in the Portal but says they're buggy — showing as overdue even when the check-in was completed correctly
- No mention of a reliable system that tells her whether she's met all clients for the month

### Technology and The Portal

**What works:**
- Portal is good for note-taking
- The overview/sidebar gives some useful info (preferred name, basic details)
- Having the budget visible is important

**What doesn't work / wishlist:**
- Date of birth not displayed prominently (third person to request this)
- Portal always opens to consumer profile instead of a more useful landing view
- Can't have notes open while browsing other tabs — wants a persistent notepad
- Needs to know who to contact (client vs. authorised representative) without digging into the care circle
- Time zones for clients across Australia — she forgets and calls WA clients too early
- Wants "best time to call" information surfaced
- Wants operational alerts (bills on hold, overdue items) surfaced at the top level
- Would love everything consolidated into the Portal so they can stop using Zoho: "That would be good because we'd like to have everything in one place"
- Check-in reminders exist but are buggy/unreliable

**Zoho vs Portal:**
- Zoho is where they "action everything" — inclusions, amendments, meal requests, cab charge cards, checking in
- Portal is "just for note taking" in terms of calls
- Wants Zoho functionality migrated to Portal piece by piece — suggested check-ins could be first

### The "Magic Panel" Concept

When asked what would be in a panel that gives "everything you need to have a conversation":
1. Cultural significance / how they want to be addressed
2. Anything that needs acting on (bills on hold, overdue items)
3. Budget status / plan utilisation
4. Who to contact (client vs. representative) — don't want to dig through care circle
5. Phone numbers readily accessible
6. Time zone / best time to call
7. Preferred contact method

She was clear that **operational context is more important to her than personal/social context** for this panel.

### Covering for Colleagues (Going in Blind)

- When someone is on leave, their clients get distributed but the covering coordinator has limited access
- Can't search for clients outside your team without organisation admin access
- Can't access the absent coordinator's emails
- Strategy: read back through notes while on the phone, try to piece things together
- If it's about something communicated via email rather than notes, she simply can't help the client — they get frustrated

### Anything Surprising

1. **She doesn't feel she needs call prep help herself** — her experience means she just picks up the phone. The value she sees in RIN features is primarily for newer staff. This is important: the most experienced user doesn't see personal value in the core RIN proposition for herself.

2. **Authorised representatives don't want personal chat** — Levi estimates she speaks to representatives more than clients directly (~60/40 split). Reps are usually working themselves and want calls kept short and transactional. The "magic panel" needs to adapt based on who you're calling.

3. **The 30 calls/day KPI actively works against relationship building** — even though she knows clients would prefer personal conversations, she can't afford the time. This is a structural tension that RIN needs to acknowledge.

4. **She uses Monday.com as a personal task manager** — the Portal/Zoho don't give her adequate task tracking, so she maintains a parallel system for follow-ups from calls.

5. **New staff get "30 clients in the first couple of weeks"** with insufficient training — then expected to learn on the job. This contributes directly to high turnover.

6. **ChatGPT is already being used** — Levi uses it to draft emails and has saved context about working at Trilogy Care. She was also shown the Dia browser's ability to query the Support at Home program document and was very excited about it.

## Validation Summary

| Assumption | Validated? | Evidence |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Staff spend 2-3 min prepping per call | Partially | Experienced staff like Levi barely prep — they "just get on the phone." But she does open Portal + Zoho + Monday.com and check notes/budget. Newer staff likely take longer. |
| Staff will capture personal context if fast (<15s) | Partially | Levi doesn't currently document personal info unless significant. Said it "would be beneficial" for handover but didn't express strong enthusiasm for doing it herself. |
| Talking points would feel helpful, not scripted | Yes | "Talking points would be good. I feel like for newbies." Wanted ability to add her own. No concerns about feeling scripted. |
| No system tracks monthly contact | Yes | Check-ins are tracked via Zoho open activities and Portal notes, but the Portal's check-in reminders are buggy/unreliable. No single reliable view of "have I contacted everyone this month." |
| High staff turnover means knowledge is lost | Yes | Confirmed high turnover. When covering for absent staff, coordinators go in blind — no email access, limited client access, notes may be incomplete. |
| Clients feel like "just a number" | Yes | "We don't necessarily get personal anymore... It's kind of sad. They probably don't get... I think a lot of our clients prefer having personal conversations." |
| Sidebar on the right would feel natural | Cautious | She wants the info but worried about overloading: "I feel like we'll just put so much in a sidebar it gets to a point where there's too much." |

## Design Implications

1. **The panel must be context-aware** — adapt based on whether you're calling the client or their authorised representative. Reps don't want small talk.
2. **Operational alerts should be primary** — bills on hold, overdue check-ins, budget warnings are more immediately useful than personal talking points for experienced staff.
3. **Persistent notes are a top request** — ability to type notes while navigating other parts of the package. This came up independently from multiple angles.
4. **The "who to contact" problem is urgent** — coordinators currently dig through the care circle to find phone numbers and determine whether to call the client or their rep.
5. **New staff are the primary beneficiaries of talking points** — experienced staff have internalised this skill. Design for the newer user, not the power user.
6. **Time zone awareness** is a quick win — clients are across Australia and coordinators forget the offset.
