# User Research: Junior Care Partner

## Profile
- **Role**: Care Partner (newer to the team)
- **Tenure**: Relatively new — Levi noted "she's quite new though, she might not really [have opinions yet]"
- **Caseload**: Referenced having 21 clients (vs Levi's larger caseload); mentioned needing to "ring them all twice"
- **Experience level**: Junior — still building confidence on calls

## Key Quotes

> "I like the... I think it's easy. I mean, I feel like when you get confident on the phone, I just sit there — click, copy, click — while it's ringing, I get up on two pages. Got my notes, my budget."

> "I would try and address business first. Then if they have time, or they tell me something personal, then I would have the conversation in terms of personal aspects."

> "If it's a client, that's great — they would love to chat with you all day, to be honest. But if it's an authorised representative, not always. They don't always want to talk to you."

> "Mine's not... the first point is not necessarily personal for me."

## Where She Differs From Levi

| Topic | Levi (Senior) | Junior CP |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| **Call prep approach** | Barely preps — experience carries her. Opens Portal + Zoho + Monday.com from muscle memory. | Still developing her routine. Opens two tabs (notes + budget) while it's ringing. More methodical about copy-pasting info. |
| **Personal conversation** | Acknowledges clients want it but actively avoids it due to time pressure and KPI targets. Has learned to cut conversations short. | Leads with business first, then allows personal conversation if time permits. Hasn't developed Levi's "hard stop" technique. |
| **Call volume management** | 30 calls/day, knows how to manage flow | 21 clients, still working through volume challenges |
| **Opening a call** | Introduces herself, then either has a brief personal moment or goes straight to business depending on the client | Introduces herself, goes straight to business, personal comes after if at all |
| **Confidence on calls** | Fully confident — "I've got the experience, I know what I'm going to address" | Developing confidence — frames it as "when you get confident on the phone" suggesting she's still getting there |

**The core contrast:** Levi has internalised call skills through 8 months of experience and doesn't feel she needs talking points or prep aids for herself. The junior care partner is still in the phase where structured support would be most valuable — but she didn't explicitly ask for it. Her contributions suggest she's coping by keeping things transactional (business first), which may mean she's avoiding the harder personal conversations rather than navigating them confidently.

## Themes

### Call Prep and Workflow
- Opens two browser tabs while the phone is ringing — notes in one, budget in the other
- Copy-pastes information she needs (phone numbers, etc.) as part of her prep routine
- Less fluid than Levi — more deliberate, step-by-step

### Personal Context and Client Knowledge
- Doesn't lead with personal conversation
- Will engage personally if the client initiates or if time allows after business is done
- Didn't express strong opinions about recording personal context — too new to have formed habits around it

### Contact Preferences
- Agreed with Levi that authorised representatives prefer transactional calls
- Implied her client mix may be different from Levi's (fewer high-needs clients with representatives)

### Information Needs
- When asked what information she'd want at her fingertips before a call: "All the numbers" — phone numbers for the client and contacts
- Less specific than Levi about what a "magic panel" should contain — still forming her mental model of what's important

## Validation Summary

| Assumption | Validated? | Evidence |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Staff spend 2-3 min prepping per call | Unclear | She preps while the phone is ringing (seconds, not minutes), but she has fewer clients and less complexity. |
| Talking points would feel helpful, not scripted | Not tested | She didn't react to the talking points concept directly — Levi spoke for her somewhat. |
| New staff struggle with call confidence | Partially | Her language ("when you get confident") and business-first approach suggest she's still building call skills, consistent with what Levi described about new starters. |

## Design Implications

1. **Junior staff are the real target users for RIN** — Levi said as much, and the junior CP's behaviour confirms it. She's coping by keeping things transactional, but that's exactly the pattern RIN should help break.
2. **Don't assume all users want the same panel** — junior staff may need more structured guidance (talking points, suggested openers) while senior staff want operational shortcuts (bills, budget, alerts).
3. **The copy-paste workflow is real** — she's manually copying phone numbers and other data between tabs. Even basic "click to call" or surfaced contact info would save friction.
