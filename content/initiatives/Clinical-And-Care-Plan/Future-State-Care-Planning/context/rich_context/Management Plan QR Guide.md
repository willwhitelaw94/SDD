# Management Plan QR (Quick Reference) Guide

**Author**: Head of Clinical Governance (Marianne)
**Date**: 2025
**Source**: `management Plan QRGuide.docx`

## Summary

Quick reference guide for care partners handling management plan follow-ups across common clinical scenarios. Provides structured question flows for each condition type to ensure consistent, thorough follow-up and documentation.

## Clinical Scenarios

### Hospitalisation (Infection, Cardiac, Cancer, Unwell)
1. **Discharge status**: Discharged? When? Estimated discharge date?
2. **Discharge summary**: Do they have it? If no → consent to contact GP/hospital
3. **Home supports**: Mobility aids, home care services, medication changes in place?
   - Yes → note in Portal client record
   - No → escalate to Pod Clinician
4. **GP/specialist notification**: Has GP been notified of hospital stay?
   - Yes → note in Portal
   - No → follow-up not required, document in Portal

### Continuity of Care (Non-compliance, Budget Issues, Increased Needs)
1. Currently receiving all funded/approved services?
2. Recent changes in care needs? (mobility, hygiene, cognition)
3. Reasons for non-engagement? (unhappy with provider, can't afford co-contributions, missed appointments)

### Mental Health
1. Recent changes in mood, behaviour, daily functioning?
2. Currently seeing a mental health professional?
3. Aware of support resources? (Lifeline, Beyond Blue, mental health services)

### Cognition
1. Concerns about managing daily tasks? (medications, cooking, finances)
2. Appropriate support for cognitive needs in place?
3. Recent cognition review? If no → suggest nursing/GP/care clinician review

### Wound Care
1. **Location and type**: Skin tear, bruise, surgery site, burns, ulcers, pressure wounds
2. **Wound management plan**: Who is managing? (self, nursing, hospital outpatients, GP)
3. **Nursing care needed**:
   - If yes → arranged by GP/Hospital? How many times per week? (if funding allows)
   - If family doing dressings → document in client record
   - If GP/hospital managing → how many clinic visits for dressing changes?
4. **Wound healing?** (Yes/No)
5. **Supplies funded appropriately?** (through HCP or other supports)

### Falls — Admitted to Hospital
1. **Discharge**: Date? Discharge summary available?
   - If no summary → consent to contact GP/hospital
2. **Falls alarm/watch**: Has one? Working properly? Would they like one? (check funding)
3. **Mobility**: Changed recently? Using mobility aids? Concerns with aids?
4. **Changed care needs**: Home modifications, OT assessment, physiotherapy?
5. **Care plan update**: Sufficient post-discharge services?

### Falls — Not Admitted / Refused Medical Attention
1. **Why not admitted?** (didn't feel necessary, no injury)
2. **GP visit**: Seen GP? Planning to? If refusing → advise but respect and document decision
3. **Witnesses**: Anyone witness or assist? (document in care note)
4. **Mechanism**: How did the fall happen? (document in care note)
   - Consider referral to Care Clinician if can't remember or claims head impact
5. **Injuries**: Where and what?
   - Head hit + headache → refer to Care Clinician
   - Will not talk to clinician → refer back to GP + follow up with AR for suspected head injury
   - Concerns → discuss with Care Clinician (consider 000 call for paramedic assessment)
6. **Falls alarm**: Wearing it? If not → explore why, discuss ways to wear it
7. **Further care needs**: Additional services needed? (if funding allows)
