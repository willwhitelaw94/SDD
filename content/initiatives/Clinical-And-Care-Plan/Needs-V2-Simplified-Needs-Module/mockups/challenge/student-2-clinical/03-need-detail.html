A Assessment — Clinical Rationale

Primary: Nutritional compromise secondary to oropharyngeal dysphagia with unintentional weight loss of 5.6% over 8 weeks. MNA-SF score of 7/14 indicates client is at risk of malnutrition.

Secondary: Inadequate fluid intake (<1.2L/day) with early signs of dehydration. Contributing factors include dysphagia, reduced mobility (limiting kitchen access), mild cognitive changes affecting meal planning, and psychosocial factors (eating alone, reduced motivation).

Classification: Tier 1 (Essential Care / Physiological) — Rated HIGH PRIORITY due to progressive weight loss, malnutrition risk, and potential for hospital admission if not addressed.

P Plan — How Need Will Be Met
  1. Dietitian review: Monthly consults with accredited practising dietitian for nutritional monitoring, meal planning and supplement recommendations (HCP funded)
  2. Modified texture meals: Delivered meals service 5x/week (IDDSI Level 5 compliant) — provider: Meals on Wheels QLD
  3. Meal preparation support: Care worker to assist with meal prep 2x/week focusing on nutrient-dense, dysphagia-safe meals (Carers Plus QLD)
  4. Hydration prompting: Care workers to encourage and document fluid intake at each visit; provide insulated drink bottle with time markers
  5. Weekly weight monitoring: Recorded by care worker each Monday; escalate if >1kg loss in any single week
  6. Speech pathology review: 3-monthly swallowing reassessment to monitor dysphagia progression
  7. GP notification: Dr. Helen Park notified of weight loss trajectory; bloods requested in 4 weeks (albumin, pre-albumin, FBC)
