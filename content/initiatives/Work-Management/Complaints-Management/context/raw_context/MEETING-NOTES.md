## Meeting: Complaints Review

**Date:** January 28, 2026
**Duration:** 65 minutes
**Organizer:** sianh@trilogycare.com.au
**Attendees:** Patrick H, Andy N, Mary Anne H, Sian H, and others (9 total)

### Summary

The team reviewed the existing complaints management process, highlighting manual steps and unclear role responsibilities that slow resolution and tracking. With 200-250 complaints monthly, many remain unresolved past the 28-day target.

### Current State Analysis

#### Complaints Process
- Complaints enter through the **Operations inbox** and are triaged by Ops staff
- Assigned in CRM with POD leaders accountable for resolution
- POD leaders often **do not transition complaint stages** in the system, causing tracking issues
- Care partners have **limited visibility** and often receive complaints informally outside the system
- Andy and others maintain a **spreadsheet** to monitor overdue complaints manually
- **Lack of system notifications** means communication relies on tagging or external messaging

#### Triage System
- Complaints categorized by risk: **high, medium, low**
- **200-250 complaints and remediations monthly** (~10 per day needing closure within 28 days)
- Many complaints remain **unresolved past 28 days** due to unclear closure criteria
- Team aims to break down 28-day period into actionable milestones

#### Communication Issues
- All client updates come from POD leaders or care partners **manually**
- Automated emails at complaint stages **turned off or unused**
- Need standardized email suite for acknowledgements, updates, and delay notices
- Clear communication timelines help reduce client anxiety

### Process & System Limitations

#### CRM Limitations
- Lacks **task notification and owner assignment** at care partner level
- Relies on **tagging** which causes clutter and confusion
- POD leaders have 30-45 minutes daily for complaint management but **lack dashboard visibility**
- Action plans exist but are **not surfaced effectively** on dashboards

#### Cross-Department Complexity
- Complaints involving accounts, compliance, or multiple areas require coordination
- Often handled via tagging and manual follow-up
- Accounts prefer tasks routed back to care partners who may **lack skills** to resolve invoicing issues
- Need to **simplify complaint stages** and clarify responsibilities

### Remediations vs Formal Complaints

#### Distinctions
- **Remediations**: Quick, low-level care partner escalations resolved within shifts or two days
- **Formal complaints**: Require formal processes with full documentation
- Some remediations extend into complaints when resolution not possible in expected timeframes
- Currently, many remediations take longer due to dependencies on other departments

#### Recommendations
- Filter and report on remediations separately from complaints
- Incentivize POD leaders to resolve remediations promptly
- Clear guidelines on when to escalate remediations to formal complaints
- Analyze remediation patterns to determine appropriate interventions

### Quality Assurance & Continuous Improvement

#### Complaint Closure Requirements
- Each closure must identify **process gaps or communication issues**
- Complaints categorized by cause (process failure, communication breakdown)
- Feed into monthly and quarterly trend reports

#### Reporting Framework
- Monthly and quarterly reports showing volume, resolution rates, quality trends
- Data analytics to identify patterns and feed into operational strategy
- Quarterly action plan for training, process updates, and strategic improvements

#### POD Leader Competency
- Formalize complaint handling skills in competency framework
- Link to coaching, mentoring, and performance reviews
- Strong POD leader knowledge reduces bottlenecks
- Competency ranking to recognize effective complaint handlers

### Future State & Next Steps

#### Short-term Focus
- Reduce complaints over 28 days
- Improve visibility through CRM automation and dashboards
- Fran working on automation to improve tagging and notifications
- Standardize triage criteria and escalation points
- Monthly reports prepared early each cycle

#### Long-term Goals
- Embed complaint learnings into training and strategy
- Clarify remediation boundaries
- Enhance client communications
- Marketing collaboration for social media complaint resolution
- Follow-up sessions in ~1 month to assess progress
