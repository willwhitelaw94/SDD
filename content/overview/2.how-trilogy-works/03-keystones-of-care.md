Our company values, applied to product and engineering. These aren't just mission statements—they shape how we build, collaborate, and solve problems every day.

## Our Six Keystones

### Choice

*We honour and respect each care recipient's unique story, placing compassion and choice at the heart of all decisions.*

**In Product/Engineering:** Build systems that empower users to make their own decisions. Default to flexibility over rigidity. Expose options, don't hide them. The user knows their situation better than we do.

### Excellence

*We strive to deliver quality outcomes, consistently exceeding expectations in every aspect of our role.*

**In Product/Engineering:** Ship work you're proud of. Code quality, test coverage, UX polish—these aren't optional. "Good enough" isn't excellence. Exceed expectations, don't just meet them.

### Integrity

*We conduct all aspects of business with uncompromising honesty, transparency, and ethical standards.*

**In Product/Engineering:** Be honest about estimates, risks, and trade-offs. Surface problems early. Don't hide technical debt or cut corners invisibly. Transparent code, transparent communication.

### Innovation

*We champion positive change through our commitment to continuous improvement, fostering a culture of agility and adaptability.*

**In Product/Engineering:** Challenge the status quo. Experiment with new tools and approaches. 20% innovation time exists for a reason. AI is changing everything—adapt or fall behind.

### Ownership

*We take full responsibility for our business and our role in partnerships, communities, and the lives of our care recipients.*

**In Product/Engineering:** Own your work end-to-end. Don't throw code over the wall. If it breaks in production, it's your problem. Care about the outcome, not just the output.

### Wellbeing

*We place physical, mental, and emotional health at the forefront of our mission, ensuring a safe, inclusive, and empowering environment for all.*

**In Product/Engineering:** Sustainable pace over burnout. Psychological safety in teams. Build accessible products. Code reviews are learning opportunities, not criticism. Support each other.

---

## Why These Values Matter

These keystones aren't abstract ideals. They inform every decision we make—from how we design features to how we handle failures, from how we allocate time to how we care for each other.

**When you're unsure about a decision**, ask yourself: Which keystone applies here? The answer often becomes clear.

**When you're building**, keep these values in the room. They prevent drift. They keep us grounded in what matters.

**When you're reviewing** someone else's work (code, design, spec, proposal), measure it against these keystones. Are we honoring choice? Are we shipping excellence? Are we being honest? Are we innovating or coasting? Are we owning the outcome? Are we sustainable?

The teams that thrive at Trilogy Care are the ones that live these values, not just recite them.
