## Cross-Functional Collaboration: The Product Triad

The most effective work at Trilogy happens at the intersection of **Product** (vision & strategy), **Design** (usability), and **Engineering** (execution & architecture).

### How the Triad Works

1. **Product Owners** define what should be built (business outcomes, user needs, strategic fit)
2. **Designers** explore how users will interact with it (UX, accessibility, usability)
3. **Engineers** determine how to build it technically (architecture, performance, quality)

All three perspectives must be present in every decision. Product without Design leads to unintuitive systems. Design without Engineering leads to unfeasible solutions. Engineering without Product wastes effort on the wrong things.

### When You Need to Collaborate

**Cross-functional kickoffs (before spec):**

- Are we solving the right problem?
- What are the hard constraints (technical, design, business)?
- What's the simplest viable approach?

**Design review (before implementation):**

- Do users understand the interface?
- Are we meeting accessibility requirements?
- Is the interaction flow intuitive?

**Pre-launch alignment (before shipping):**

- Does the implementation match the design?
- Are we delivering the intended outcome?
- What does success look like?

## How to Work with Each Department

### Before Starting Work

Identify which departments will be affected:

- **Are they users?** (care coordinators using features)
- **Are they stakeholders?** (finance, quality, clinical reviewing decisions)
- **Are they blockers?** (compliance, legal, security sign-off)

### Getting Alignment

1. **Involve them in the spec phase** - Not in implementation. Get their input upfront on requirements, constraints, and success criteria.
2. **Show them mockups before building** - Designers involved in visualizing the solution, not debating code.
3. **Run showcase demos** - Let teams see what shipped and celebrate progress.

### Communication Cadence

- **Daily Standups** - Within teams
- **Fortnightly Sprint Planning** - Dev / Design teams
- **Pre-sprint Product Catch-ups** - Cross-functional (business, POs, key stakeholders)
- **Quarterly Big Room Planning (BRP)** - All Product & Dev
- **Monthly Demos / Showcases** - Everyone
- **Ad-hoc Retros** - Teams as needed

## The Network Effect

Trilogy's departments exist to serve **care recipients**—the person receiving support at home. But they also serve each other.

- **Sales** brings new customers → **Assessment** creates care plans → **Care Management** delivers services
- **Care Management** identifies problems → **Product** builds solutions → **Engineering** implements them
- **Finance** forecasts costs → **Operations** optimizes delivery → **Quality** ensures compliance
- **Clinical** escalates risks → **Quality** investigates → **Product** prevents future incidents

Each department has a role to play. When departments communicate clearly, align on outcomes, and hold each other accountable, the entire system works better for the people we serve.
