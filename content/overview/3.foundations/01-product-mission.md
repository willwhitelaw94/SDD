### Power Users vs Accessible Users

Not all users are the same. We design differently for different stakeholder types:

| User Type            | Stakeholders                               | Design Approach                           | Primary Platform        |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| **Power Users**      | Internal staff, some coordinators          | Feature-rich, learn-once interfaces       | Web (always)            |
| **Accessible Users** | Recipients, representatives, care partners | Simple, intuitive, minimal learning curve | Mobile-first (shifting) |

**Key insights:**

- **Internal staff** are power users - they learn the system once and use it daily. They need comprehensive tooling, keyboard shortcuts, bulk actions.
- **Recipients** (often sons/daughters managing care) need accessible, intuitive interfaces. They may only log in occasionally. Design for "pick up and use" without training.
- **Coordinators** sit in between - they use computers daily but aren't necessarily tech-savvy. They're shifting toward power user patterns.
- **Mobile is the future** for recipients and care partners. \~40% already access via mobile UI. Internal staff will likely stay web-based.

This segmentation drives UX decisions: don't build a power-user feature for recipients, and don't oversimplify tools that internal staff need.

## What This Mission Covers

Our portal exists to be the primary place where care is coordinated, recorded, billed and improved. Key capabilities include:

- **Assign** providers to budgets and bookings
- **Generate** care plans and budgets; version and track them
- **Capture** case notes, care events and incident reports
- **Maintain** compliance; manage staff, rates and agreements
- **Submit and approve** bills for payment (recipient and supplier flows)
- **Track** expenditure and view statements/transactions with transparency
- **Help** recipients and coordinators find the right providers

## How We Deliver

| Posture                              | Description                                                            |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Portal as the primary front door** | CRM and other systems feed the portal; not the other way around        |
| **Human‑in‑the‑loop AI**             | Automation accelerates work; humans guide judgement and care decisions |
| **API‑first and modular**            | Clean integrations for partners and future roll‑ups                    |
| **Data quality at write**            | Validation and structure so analytics and compliance "just work"       |
| **Time to Impact (TTI)**             | Prioritise work that realises stakeholder benefit fastest              |

## What Success Looks Like

- **Delightful usability** – higher satisfaction across recipients, coordinators and staff
- **Adoption at scale** – the default place to action care, billing and collaboration
- **Better outcomes** – higher budget utilisation, fewer hand‑offs, faster cycle times
- **Regulatory confidence** – audit‑ready records; rapid response to rule changes
