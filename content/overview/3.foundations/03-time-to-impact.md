> **TTI (Time to Impact):** Prioritise work that realises stakeholder benefit fastest.

Time to Impact is one of our core delivery principles. It means focusing on work that delivers real value quickly—not just shipping features, but shipping *the right* features *at the right time*.

---

## The Cost of Building

We're investing heavily in development. Understanding the cost helps everyone make better decisions.

### The Numbers (2026)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Annual Dev Investment** | ~$3.1M |
| **Cost per Sprint per Squad** | ~$30,000 |
| **Squads** | 4 (Duck Duck Deploy, Budgets Reloaded, Invoices & Supplier, Mobile) |
| **Cost per Quarter** | ~$1.08M |

### Why This Matters

**Every feature has a cost.** When we're discussing what to build, everyone—engineers, designers, product, business stakeholders—should think in these terms:

| Size | Duration | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| **Small** | ~2 weeks | ~$30k |
| **Medium** | 4-8 weeks | ~$60k-$120k |
| **Large** | Quarter | ~$250k+ |

**The discipline:** If something slips to the next sprint, that's not just "a delay"—it's $30k additional investment. This framing keeps everyone focused on:

1. **Scope discipline** — Don't over-spec. What's the minimum that solves the problem?
2. **Prioritisation rigour** — Is this $60k feature more valuable than those three $20k features?
3. **Trade-off clarity** — When we choose A over B, we're investing $X in A instead of B

---

## Cost-Benefit Thinking

For every idea brief or feature request, ask:

| Question | What it reveals |
|----------|-----------------|
| What's the **benefit** (revenue, cost saving, risk reduction)? | Is this worth the investment? |
| What's the **effort** (S/M/L → $30k/$60k/$120k+)? | Is the ROI positive? |
| What's the **opportunity cost**? | What are we NOT building? |

**The goal isn't to avoid spending.** We're investing $3M+ because we believe it generates far more value. The goal is to spend wisely—maximising impact per dollar invested.

---

## Applying TTI

### In Prioritisation

- Does this move us toward a strategic goal?
- What's the fastest path to value?
- Can we ship a smaller version first?

### In Estimation

Every [Idea Brief](/ways-of-working/spec-driven-development/02-examples) includes an **Estimated Effort** section that maps S/M/L sizing to dollar amounts. This makes cost-benefit visible at the earliest stage—before we commit to building anything.

### In Daily Work

- Favour quick wins that unblock stakeholders
- Ship increments, not big bangs
- Measure from "shipped" not "started"

---

## TTI vs Velocity

| Metric | What it measures | Why it matters |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **Velocity** | How fast we ship | Output |
| **Time to Impact** | How fast value reaches stakeholders | Outcome |

High velocity with low TTI means we're shipping fast but not delivering value. Low velocity with high TTI is better—we're shipping less, but each shipment matters.
