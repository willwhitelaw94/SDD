## TL;DR

- **Claim submission in Portal** — Bills can now be submitted as claims to Services Australia directly from the portal, replacing a manual external process and closing the loop on the end-to-end billing workflow.
- **NDIS compliance banning list** — Compliance Managers can now upload and manage the NDIS Commission banning list, with automatic ABN matching to organisations and monthly reminder emails to keep the list current.
- **Expanded global search** — Users and Organisation Workers are now searchable in the global search bar, and bill search results now show the bill status badge to reduce unnecessary navigation.
- **Zoho sync failure alerts** — Zoho sync job failures now trigger email notifications to the designated recipient, providing immediate visibility into data synchronisation issues.
- **Billing accuracy improvements** — Unit cost calculation precision increased, ABN/GST dates now displayed during bill processing, coordinator loading fee history accessible from the Bill page, and reimbursement suppliers are now correctly excluded from GST restrictions.

### Claims Submitted Directly from the Portal

A single sentence: Finance and billing staff can now submit claims to Services Australia without leaving the portal.

**Why this matters**

Claim submission was previously a multi-step process requiring external systems. Bringing it in-house reduces turnaround time, eliminates manual data re-entry errors, and gives finance teams a single source of truth for the claim lifecycle — from bill creation through to submission and reconciliation.

**What's changing**

A new Claims section under Services Australia provides a dashboard of existing claims, the ability to create new claims, and a submission workflow for open invoices. Bills go through a sign-off process before being submitted, and submissions can be approved or rejected with notes. The system handles invoice creation, polling, and status tracking automatically.

**Who's affected**

Finance team members and anyone involved in the Services Australia billing and claims process.

**Action required**

No action required — this is available immediately to users with existing Services Australia access.

---

### NDIS Compliance Banning List Management

Compliance teams can now track NDIS Commission enforcement actions directly in the portal.

**Why this matters**

The NDIS Commission publishes compliance actions (banning orders, suspensions, revocations) that must be cross-referenced against the organisation's supplier network. Previously this was tracked in an unstructured format. The new system provides structured data with automatic ABN matching, making compliance checks faster and audit-ready.

**What's changing**

A new Compliance section in the sidebar gives access to a searchable, filterable banning list. Compliance Managers can upload the NDIS Commission CSV export, and the system automatically matches entries to organisations by ABN. Monthly email reminders prompt Compliance Managers to upload the latest list.

**Who's affected**

Compliance Managers and Admin users responsible for NDIS regulatory compliance.

**Action required**

Compliance Managers should upload the current NDIS banning list CSV to populate the new system. Monthly reminders will prompt future uploads.

---

### Expanded Global Search with Users and Bill Status

Staff can now find users, organisation workers, and see bill statuses directly from global search.

**Why this matters**

Previously, finding a specific user or organisation worker required navigating to their respective pages. Adding them to global search reduces navigation time. Showing bill status in search results eliminates the need to click into each bill just to check its current state.

**What's changing**

The global search bar now returns results for Users (by email) and Organisation Workers (by email and name). Bill search results now display a status badge, so staff can quickly identify which bills need attention without opening each one.

**Who's affected**

All portal staff who use the search bar.

**Action required**

No action required — this is available immediately.

---

### Zoho Sync Failure Notifications

Operations teams now receive immediate email alerts when Zoho data synchronisation encounters errors.

**Why this matters**

Zoho sync failures previously went undetected until someone noticed stale or missing data, which could cause downstream billing and reporting issues. Proactive notifications allow teams to investigate and resolve sync issues before they impact operations.

**What's changing**

When any Zoho sync job (bills, care coordinators, consumers, documents, notes, packages, package leave, or suppliers) encounters a failure, an email notification is sent to the designated recipient with details about what failed and why.

**Who's affected**

Operations and data management teams responsible for Zoho-Portal data integrity.

**Action required**

No action required — notifications are sent automatically. Ensure the configured recipient email is correct.

---

### Billing Accuracy and Usability Improvements

Several targeted improvements to billing precision and the bill processing experience.

**Why this matters**

Accurate billing is critical for financial compliance. Rounding mismatches between Portal and MYOB were causing reconciliation overhead. Missing context (like ABN dates and coordinator loading history) during bill processing slowed decision-making.

**What's changing**

Unit cost calculations now use higher decimal precision to eliminate rounding mismatches with MYOB. The bill processing page shows ABN Active From and GST Registered dates for suppliers. Coordinator loading fee history is now accessible directly from the Bill page (previously only available from the budget plan). Bill line items now display service codes instead of reference numbers.

**Who's affected**

Finance and billing staff who process bills.

**Action required**

No action required — these improvements are available immediately.

### Submit Claims to Services Australia from the Portal

You can now create and submit claims to Services Australia without leaving the portal.

**Why we built this**

We heard from finance teams that managing claims across multiple systems was time-consuming and error-prone. This brings the full claim lifecycle into one place.

**What's changed**

There's a new Claims area under Services Australia where you can view existing claims, create new ones, and submit invoices for claiming. You can approve or reject invoices before submission, and track the status of everything from one dashboard.

**Where to find it**

`Services Australia → Claims` for the dashboard, or `Services Australia → Submit Invoices` to manage invoice submissions.

**How to use it**

1. Go to `Services Australia → Claims`
2. Click **New Claim** to start a claim
3. Select the invoices you want to include
4. Review and submit

There's a new Compliance section in the sidebar with a Banning List page. It shows all known NDIS compliance actions, whether they match any of your organisation's suppliers, and their current status. You can search, filter by type and status, and upload new data from the NDIS Commission.

### Claims Submission Workflow

A new end-to-end claims submission workflow for Services Australia has been added to the portal.

**Why this was changed**

Finance teams needed to manage claims across multiple systems. The portal now handles the entire lifecycle — creating claims, submitting invoices, and tracking statuses — in one place.

**What's different**

Users can now access a Claims Dashboard showing all existing claims, create new claims by selecting invoices, and submit them to Services Australia. Invoices can be approved or rejected with notes before submission. The system tracks submission status (Approved, Rejected, Error).

**Where in the platform**

`Services Australia → Claims` and `Services Australia → Submit Invoices`

**How to help users**

Walk users through the new Claims section. The workflow is: view dashboard → create new claim → review and submit invoices. Each step has clear actions.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Where do I submit claims now?"**
> A: Go to `Services Australia → Claims` to view and create claims, or `Services Australia → Submit Invoices` to manage invoice submissions.

> **Q: "What does Approved/Rejected mean on an invoice submission?"**
> A: Approved means the invoice was accepted for submission to Services Australia. Rejected means someone reviewed it and decided not to submit it (a note should explain why).

> **Q: "A claim submission shows 'Error' status — what happened?"**
> A: An error occurred during submission to Services Australia. The user should try again or escalate to the tech team if it persists.

### NDIS Compliance Banning List

A new Compliance section lets authorised users manage the NDIS Commission banning list with automatic organisation matching.

**Why this was changed**

The previous banning list storage was unstructured and difficult to search. The new system provides a proper table with filtering, search, and automatic matching of banning entries to organisations by ABN.

**What's different**

The old banning list data (stored as JSON blobs) has been replaced with a structured table. Compliance Managers can upload CSV files exported from the NDIS Commission website, and the system automatically matches entries to organisations. A monthly email reminder prompts uploads to keep the list current.

**Where in the platform**

`Compliance → Banning List` (requires `view-banning-list` permission)

**How to help users**

Ensure the user has the `view-banning-list` permission (Admin or Compliance Manager role). For uploading, they need `upload-banning-list`.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I can't see the Compliance section in the sidebar?"**
> A: Check if the user has the `view-banning-list` permission. Only Admin and Compliance Manager roles have access.

> **Q: "The CSV upload didn't match any organisations?"**
> A: The matching is by ABN. If the organisations in the CSV don't have matching ABNs in the portal, they'll appear as unmatched entries. This is expected for providers not in your network.

> **Q: "I got a monthly reminder email about uploading the banning list — what do I do?"**
> A: Download the latest CSV from the NDIS Commission website (link is in the email), then upload it at `Compliance → Banning List → Upload CSV`.

### Expanded Global Search (Users, Workers, Bill Status)

The global search now includes Users and Organisation Workers, and bill results show status badges.

**Why this was changed**

Users reported that finding specific people required too many clicks. Bill results didn't show status, causing unnecessary navigation to check bill states.

**What's different**

Searching in the top bar now returns User results (by email) and Organisation Worker results (by email and name). Bill results now show a status badge (e.g., In Review, Approved, Paid) directly in the search results.

**Where in the platform**

Global search bar at the top of every page.

**How to help users**

Tell users they can search for people by email address. If they're looking for a bill and want to check its status, they can now see it in the search results without clicking through.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I searched for a user but nothing came up?"**
> A: The search uses email addresses for users. Try searching by the user's email rather than their name. Organisation Workers can be searched by name.

> **Q: "The bill status badge shows something different from what I expected?"**
> A: The badge shows the current bill stage. If you believe it's incorrect, open the bill to check its full status history.

### Zoho Sync Failure Email Notifications

Zoho sync job failures now trigger automatic email notifications.

**Why this was changed**

Sync failures were previously silent, leading to stale data issues that weren't discovered until someone noticed incorrect information in the portal.

**What's different**

When any Zoho sync job fails (bills, care coordinators, consumers, documents, notes, packages, package leave, or suppliers), an email notification is sent to the configured recipient with failure details.

**Where in the platform**

This is a background system — no user-facing UI. Notifications go to the configured email address.

**How to help users**

If a user reports stale Zoho data, check whether a sync failure notification was sent. The notification includes details about which sync job failed and why.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I'm getting sync failure emails — what should I do?"**
> A: Review the error details in the email. Common causes include Zoho API rate limits or connectivity issues. If the issue persists, escalate to the development team.

### Billing Improvements and Bug Fixes

Several fixes to billing accuracy, supplier validation, and user experience.

**Why this was changed**

Various reported issues: rounding mismatches between Portal and MYOB, missing ABN/GST context during bill processing, incorrect document requirements for non-sole-trader suppliers, and stale bill approval emails.

**What's different**

- **Unit cost precision** — Increased decimal precision in calculations to match MYOB exactly
- **ABN/GST dates** — Supplier detail card on the bill page now shows ABN Active From and GST Registered dates
- **Service codes** — Bill line items show service codes instead of reference numbers
- **Coordinator loading fee history** — Now accessible from the Bill page (was only on budget plans)
- **Reimbursement suppliers** — Internal reimbursement suppliers no longer blocked by GST restrictions
- **Bill approval emails** — No longer sent for bills that are already paid/approved
- **Supplier documents** — Non-sole-trader suppliers no longer required to upload extra documents
- **Address saving** — Handles cases where Google Maps omits certain address fields

**Where in the platform**

Bill pages, supplier details, and address forms throughout the portal.

**How to help users**

Most changes are automatic. For ABN/GST dates, direct users to the supplier detail card on the bill page.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "The bill amounts look different from before?"**
> A: Rounding precision was improved to match MYOB exactly. The amounts should now be more accurate.

> **Q: "I'm still getting approval emails for paid bills?"**
> A: This fix only applies to new emails going forward. If it persists, report it as a bug.

---

### Other Fixes

- **Compliance user 403 after impersonation** — Compliance users no longer get a 403 error when they stop impersonating a supplier. The redirect now correctly returns them to the appropriate page.
- **Nova permissions page** — The permissions page in Nova admin no longer fails to load with a type mapping error.
- **Document scan lock timeout** — Document scanning jobs are now unique per document, preventing lock wait timeouts when multiple scans run simultaneously.
- **MYOB sub-account resync** — A new nightly job (00:15) resyncs package MYOB sub-accounts to keep them up to date.
