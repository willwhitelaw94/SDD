### Budgets Reloaded V2

Budget planning has a new foundation that delivers significantly improved visibility and control over how funds are planned and allocated across service plans.

**Why this matters**

The existing budget interface required multiple navigation steps to understand how funding was distributed across streams and time periods. This redesign gives care teams a complete, at-a-glance picture of funding allocation in real time.

**What's changing**

The budget management screen has been rebuilt from the ground up with an expandable funding panel structure. Budget items can now be viewed and edited inline, and funding streams are visible in both collapsed summary and expanded detail modes. The previous budget proposal workflow has been redirected to the new Budget Plans interface.

**Who's affected**

Care coordinators, care managers, and any staff who manage or review package budgets.

**Action required**

No action required â€” the new Budget V2 interface is accessible via the existing budget navigation. Teams should familiarise themselves with the new layout.

---

### Team Permissions Fixed

A critical bug has been resolved that affected how permissions were enforced for users operating within specific teams.

**Why this matters**

Incorrect permission enforcement creates both a security and compliance risk â€” users may have had access to capabilities they shouldn't, or conversely been blocked from actions they should be able to perform. This has been fully corrected.

**What's changing**

The underlying permission system now correctly enforces team-scoped roles across all parts of the portal, including background jobs. Database-level constraints have been added to prevent duplicate permission entries.

**Who's affected**

All users, particularly those with team-specific roles and responsibilities.

**Action required**

No action required â€” the fix is automatic. Admins should review team role assignments if any access anomalies were observed prior to this release.

---

### Bill On-Hold and Rejection Reasons Now Visible

Care coordinators and suppliers can now see exactly why a bill was placed on hold or rejected, directly on the bill view page.

**Why this matters**

Previously, on-hold or rejected bills displayed only the status â€” staff and suppliers had no visibility into the reason without contacting someone internally. This eliminated a significant source of back-and-forth communication and delayed bill processing.

**What's changing**

The bill status badge now includes the reason text and any associated comment for on-hold and rejected bills. This is visible directly on the bill summary page for both care coordinators and suppliers.

**Who's affected**

Care coordinators, suppliers, and any staff who view bill detail pages.

**Action required**

No action required â€” this information is now visible automatically where applicable.

---

### Fee Exemption Accountability on Bills

Bills now record the name of the staff member who applied a loading fee exemption, providing a full audit trail.

**Why this matters**

Without knowing who applied a fee exemption, finance and compliance teams had no way to attribute or review exemption decisions. This change closes that accountability gap.

**What's changing**

When a loading fee exemption is set on a bill, the system now records which user applied it. The Bills table has new columns and filters to search and sort by exemption status and the exempting staff member.

**Who's affected**

Finance leads, compliance officers, and any staff who review or audit billing.

**Action required**

No action required â€” the tracking is automatic from this release onwards. A backfill of historical exemption records has been applied where activity log data was available.

---

### Support at Home Always Enabled

The Support at Home feature flag has been permanently removed â€” SAH is now always active for all eligible organisations.

**Why this matters**

Operating a feature behind a flag indefinitely creates maintenance overhead and risk. With SAH now fully proven and stable, removing the flag simplifies operations and removes a potential misconfiguration vector.

**What's changing**

No visible change for end users or organisations already using SAH. The `SAH_ENABLED` environment variable is no longer required and can be removed from deployment configurations.

**Who's affected**

Engineering and DevOps teams managing environment configuration. No impact on end users.

**Action required**

DevOps should remove the `SAH_ENABLED` environment variable from all deployment configurations.

---

### Organisation Codes and Colours

Organisations now have auto-generated short codes (e.g. "TCC") and colour identifiers, improving visual identification across the platform.

**Why this matters**

As the number of organisations in the system grows, having quick visual identifiers makes it easier for staff to distinguish and navigate between them.

**What's changing**

A short alphabetic code (3â€“4 characters) and a deterministic hex colour are generated from each organisation's legal trading name. A backfill command has been run to assign codes and colours to all existing verified organisations.

**Who's affected**

Internal staff who navigate between multiple organisations.

**Action required**

No action required â€” codes and colours are assigned automatically.

---

### Compliance Managers Can Add Supplier Documents

Compliance managers and compliance team members can now add missing documents to supplier profiles without requiring admin access.

**Why this matters**

Compliance workflows were bottlenecked by the requirement for admin involvement when supplier documentation was incomplete. This change lets compliance teams manage documents independently.

**What's changing**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been added, assigned to Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles.

**Who's affected**

Compliance managers and compliance team members.

**Action required**

No action required â€” the permission is automatically assigned to the relevant roles.

**What's different**

**Where in the platform**

Platform-wide â€” affects any feature gated by team-scoped permissions.

**How to help users**

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I can no longer see something I used to see?"**
