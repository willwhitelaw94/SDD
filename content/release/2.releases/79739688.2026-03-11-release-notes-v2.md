## TL;DR

- **Team permissions fixed** — A critical flaw in how permissions were scoped to teams has been resolved; users now correctly inherit both their team-specific and global permissions without errors.
- **Budgets Reloaded foundations** — The new budget management interface has been deployed to the portal, including a redesigned header, plan navigator, and quarter selector.
- **Organisation codes & colours** — Verified organisations now receive an auto-generated 3–4 character short code and a deterministic colour for use across the portal's budget and care plan views.
- **Fee exemption audit trail** — The bills table now records who applied a loading fee exemption, with new filter and column options so finance teams can quickly identify and audit exempted bills.
- **Support at Home always active** — The `SAH_ENABLED` environment toggle has been permanently removed; Support at Home functionality is now always on for all applicable organisations.
- **Supplier document improvements** — Several fixes around expired document alerts, verification logic, and portal stage management improve accuracy for compliance workflows.

### Team Permissions Resolved

This release closes a compliance gap where users assigned to teams were not correctly inheriting their permissions, causing access errors and unpredictable behaviour across the portal.

**Why this matters**

Incorrect permission scoping meant some staff could not complete tasks they were authorised to perform, while in edge cases the system may have applied permissions from a different team context. This represents both an operational risk and a compliance concern for organisations using the team feature.

**What's changing**

The underlying permission tables have been restructured to correctly include team context in all role and permission assignments. Global permissions (not scoped to any team) now consistently apply across all users and background processes, including scheduled jobs and data sync operations.

**Who's affected**

All organisations using the Teams feature. Staff with team-scoped roles will now have reliable, correct access to the features they are authorised to use.

**Action required**

No action required — this fix is applied automatically. Administrators should be aware that any reports of incorrect access from the past few weeks may now be resolved and should be tested again.

### Budgets Reloaded — New Budget Management Interface

A major redesign of the budget management experience is being deployed in stages. This release lays the structural foundations for the new interface.

**Why this matters**

The existing budget UI was built for an earlier version of the product and does not support the complexity of modern care plans, multi-quarter budgets, or the level of visibility coordinators need. The redesign addresses these gaps directly.

**What's changing**

The budget tab on service plans now uses a redesigned header with improved plan navigation, quarter selection, and a clearer layout for budget actions. The underlying data structures have been updated to support the full redesign as it rolls out across subsequent releases.

**Who's affected**

Care coordinators and anyone managing service plan budgets. The change is visible on the Budget tab of service plans.

**Action required**

No action required — the interface updates automatically. Team leads may wish to note the new navigation pattern and communicate it to coordinators if needed.

### Organisation Codes & Colours

Verified organisations now have a unique 3–4 character short code and a deterministic colour automatically assigned to them.

**Why this matters**

As the portal expands to support richer budget and care plan views, visual organisation identifiers reduce cognitive load and improve at-a-glance recognition — particularly for coordinators managing plans across multiple organisations.

**What's changing**

All verified organisations will have codes and colours generated automatically. Existing organisations without a code will be backfilled via a scheduled command.

**Who's affected**

Primarily relevant to coordinators and managers who work across multiple organisations. The identifiers will appear in budget and plan views.

**Action required**

No action required. The backfill process runs automatically.

### Loading Fee Exemption Audit Trail

The bills system now records who applied a loading fee exemption, giving finance teams a complete audit trail for all fee-exempt bills.

**Why this matters**

Previously it was not possible to determine which user had exempted loading fees on a bill without consulting activity logs. This creates a compliance and accountability gap for finance sign-off processes.

**What's changing**

The bills table now stores a direct reference to the user who set the exemption. New filter and column options are available in the Bills table so finance staff can immediately see all exempted bills and identify who approved the exemption.

**Who's affected**

Finance team leads and compliance staff involved in billing sign-off and audit processes.

**Action required**

No action required — the tracking is applied automatically going forward. A backfill command is available to populate historical data from the activity log.

### Support at Home — Always Active

The `SAH_ENABLED` environment flag that previously controlled whether Support at Home features were active has been permanently removed.

**Why this matters**

Having a feature toggle at the infrastructure level created maintenance overhead and risk of misconfiguration. With SAH now a standard part of every eligible organisation's workflow, the toggle is no longer appropriate.

**What's changing**

Support at Home functionality (funding allocations, quarter management, loading fees) is now always active for all applicable organisations. No environment variable is needed.

**Who's affected**

Operations and IT staff who previously managed the `SAH_ENABLED` config. There is nothing to configure going forward.

**Action required**

Remove `SAH_ENABLED` from any `.env` files or infrastructure configuration if present. The variable is now ignored.

### Team Permissions Fix

A critical fix for how the portal resolves team and global permissions has been deployed.

**Why this was changed**

Users working within a team context were experiencing access errors — being blocked from actions they should be authorised to perform. The root cause was a mismatch in how team IDs were stored in the permission tables, causing some permission lookups to fail silently.

**What's different**

Previously: Permission lookups used `NULL` for the global team context, which caused mismatches since the database used `0` as the default. This caused some users to have permissions denied incorrectly.

Now: The permission tables have been migrated so all global permissions use `team_id = 0` consistently. The middleware and background jobs now also default to team ID `0`, preventing any context leakage between requests.

**Where in the platform**

Portal-wide — any feature that checks user permissions is affected. Particularly relevant for organisations using the Teams feature.

**How to help users**

If a user reports they cannot access something they should have permission for:
1. Ask them to log out and back in to reset their session
2. Ask their admin to confirm their role and team assignment
3. If the issue persists after this release, escalate — it may be a specific role configuration issue rather than the system bug

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I still can't access [feature] after this update?"**
> A: Ask the user to log out and log back in. If the issue persists, verify their role is correctly assigned in their organisation settings. The fix resolves the underlying system issue, but role assignments themselves are unchanged.

> **Q: "My team members have different access than expected?"**
> A: Confirm the correct roles are assigned to each team member. This fix resolves incorrect permission resolution — it does not change what roles are assigned to users.

> **Q: "Why did this happen?"**
> A: A technical mismatch in how team IDs were stored in the permissions database caused some checks to fail. This has now been corrected with a data migration.

### Fee Exemption Tracking on Bills

Bills now record who applied a loading fee exemption, and the Bills table has new filter/column options.

**Why this was changed**

Finance teams needed to be able to audit fee exemptions. Previously, determining who had exempted fees required searching through activity logs manually.

**What's different**

Previously: No direct record of who exempted fees on a bill.
Now: The `bills` table stores a `loading_fees_exempted_by` field (user ID). The Bills table has new columns (Fee Exempted, Exempted By) and filters.

**Where in the platform**

`Bills table` — via column customisation. `Bills → [Select a Bill]` — the exemption detail is also visible on the bill record.

**How to help users**

If users ask about fee exemption history, direct them to the Bills table and show them how to enable the "Fee Exempted" and "Exempted By" columns.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "How do I find all bills with fee exemptions?"**
> A: In the Bills table, use the column picker to enable "Fee Exempted" and then filter by "Fee Exempted = Yes".

> **Q: "The Exempted By column is empty for old bills?"**
> A: Historical data is being backfilled from the activity log. For very old bills, the exempted-by field may not populate if the original action wasn't captured in the log.
