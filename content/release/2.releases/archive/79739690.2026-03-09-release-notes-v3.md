## TL;DR

- **Team permissions fixed** — A critical permission bug affecting users who belong to teams has been resolved. Users who were being incorrectly blocked from accessing features they should have had access to will now function normally.
- **Fee exemption audit trail** — The Bills table now records who exempted a bill's loading fees, adding accountability and an "Exempted By" column to the Bills table.
- **Billing improvements** — On-hold and rejection reasons are now displayed directly to Care Coordinators and suppliers; bill items linked to transactions are now protected from accidental deletion.
- **Supplier dashboard fixes** — Expired document alerts now display correctly on the supplier dashboard, even when the supplier has no services in their price list.
- **Organisation short codes and colours** — Every verified organisation now gets a short code (e.g. CACO) and a consistent colour, laying the groundwork for visual identification across the platform.
- **Support at Home always enabled** — The legacy SAH_ENABLED feature flag has been removed. Support at Home is now permanently enabled for all organisations.

### Critical Team Permissions Fix

Users who belong to teams were being incorrectly blocked from accessing features their role entitled them to access. This was a permissions infrastructure bug — not a configuration issue — that affected any user with an active team assignment.

**Why this matters**

Permission failures erode confidence in the platform and create unnecessary support load. A user being told they don't have access to something they clearly should have is a compliance and operational risk, especially in care management workflows where time-sensitive actions matter.

**What's changing**

The underlying permissions system (Spatie Permission) has been corrected to properly resolve role-based permissions regardless of whether a user is currently operating within a team context. A database migration is also included to fix primary key definitions on permission tables, preventing duplicate key errors that could arise when assigning roles.

**Who's affected**

All users who are members of one or more teams — including Bill Processing team members, Care Partners operating in Pods, and any other team-scoped roles.

**Action required**

No action required. The fix is applied automatically on deployment. If any staff report persistent permission issues after deployment, escalate as a priority support ticket.

### Fee Exemption Audit Trail

Bills with loading fee exemptions now record which staff member applied the exemption, creating a clear accountability trail for finance and compliance.

**Why this matters**

Fee exemptions affect billing totals and revenue recognition. Knowing who applied an exemption — and being able to filter bills by exemption status — is essential for finance team audits and any investigation into billing discrepancies.

**What's changing**

The Bills table gains two new columns: "Fee Exempted" (yes/no) and "Exempted By" (the staff member who set the exemption). Both are filterable in the standard Bills table view.

**Who's affected**

Finance team leads, billing staff, and compliance reviewers who work with the Bills table.

**Action required**

No action required. The new columns are available but hidden by default — billing team leads may want to configure their table views to show them.

---

### On-Hold & Rejection Reasons Now Visible

Care Coordinators and suppliers can now see the specific on-hold or rejection reason directly on a bill, rather than receiving a generic notification.

**Why this matters**

Suppliers and Care Coordinators were spending time contacting support or billing staff to find out why a bill was on hold or rejected. Surfacing this information directly reduces that friction and speeds up resolution.

**What's changing**

On-hold and rejection reasons are now displayed on the bill view for both Care Coordinators and suppliers. Validation error details are also included in the on-hold comment for CM bills, replacing the previous "see logs" placeholder.

**Who's affected**

Suppliers reviewing their bill status, Care Coordinators managing bills for their clients.

**Action required**

No action required. This takes effect immediately on deployment.

### Organisation Short Codes and Colour Identifiers

All verified organisations will be assigned a short code (3–4 characters derived from their legal trading name) and a consistent colour, enabling richer visual identification across the portal.

**Why this matters**

As the platform scales to more organisations, the ability to visually identify organisations at a glance — in dashboards, reports, and multi-org views — becomes increasingly valuable. Short codes also improve searchability and labelling consistency.

**What's changing**

A new `code` and `colour` field has been added to the organisations table. A one-time backfill will run on deployment to populate existing verified organisations. New organisations will be assigned a code and colour automatically on verification.

**Who's affected**

Internal staff and any future UI components that display organisation identifiers.

**Action required**

No action required. The backfill runs automatically on deployment.

### Support at Home Permanently Enabled

The legacy `SAH_ENABLED` feature flag has been removed. Support at Home features are now permanently active for all organisations.

**Why this matters**

Conditional feature flags add complexity and risk when they no longer serve a purpose. Removing the flag simplifies the codebase and eliminates any residual risk of SAH accidentally being disabled.

**What's changing**

Approximately 1,440 lines of conditional code have been removed. No functionality changes — Support at Home behaves identically to before, just without the toggle.

**Who's affected**

No operational change for any team.

**Action required**

No action required. Environment variables for `SAH_ENABLED` can be removed from infrastructure configurations at the next convenient opportunity.

### Supplier Verification & Dashboard Fixes

Two supplier-related issues have been resolved: expired document alerts on the supplier dashboard now display correctly (even when the supplier has no services in their price list), and the verification logic for confirmed suppliers now correctly accounts for Police Check or NDIS Worker Screening clearances.

**Why this matters**

Incorrect verification status or missing compliance alerts could expose the organisation to regulatory risk. Both fixes ensure accurate compliance monitoring.

**Who's affected**

Compliance team, supplier onboarding staff.

**Action required**

No action required.

### Team Permissions Fix

Users belonging to teams were being denied access to features their role should allow.

**Why this was changed**

The Spatie Permission library (which manages roles and permissions) was not correctly resolving permissions when a user had an active team context set. This caused users to hit "access denied" errors on features they should have been able to use.

**What's different**

The User model's `hasPermissionTo()`, `roles()`, and `permissions()` methods have been overridden to correctly merge team-scoped and global (team_id = 0) permissions. A database migration fixes the permission table primary keys to include `team_id`, resolving potential duplicate key violations when assigning roles.

**Where in the platform**

Platform-wide — any permission-gated action or route could have been affected for team members.

**How to help users**

If a user reports an "access denied" error after this deployment:
1. Confirm what role they have (`Users → [User] → Roles`)
2. Confirm they are in the right team (`Users → [User] → Teams`)
3. If the error persists, escalate — it may be a genuine permission misconfiguration rather than the bug that's now fixed.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I still can't access something after the release?"**
> A: Confirm their role has the required permission assigned. The fix resolves the team context issue — if the permission is genuinely not on their role, that's a separate configuration matter.

> **Q: "My team access changed after the update?"**
> A: The fix restores correct behaviour. If anything changed unexpectedly, gather specifics and escalate for investigation.

### Fee Exemption Audit Trail (Bills)

Bills now track who applied a loading fee exemption.

**Why this was changed**

Finance teams needed accountability for fee exemptions. Previously there was no easy way to see who had waived fees on a bill.

**What's different**

- New `loading_fees_exempted_by` column on the `bills` table stores the user who applied the exemption
- Bills table shows "Fee Exempted" (boolean) and "Exempted By" (user name) columns (hidden by default)
- Both columns are filterable
- A one-time backfill (`app:backfill-loading-fees-exempted-by`) will populate historical data from the activity log

**Where in the platform**

`Bills` table — columns need to be enabled via column settings.

**How to help users**

If a billing user asks about the new columns, guide them to enable "Fee Exempted" and "Exempted By" in their column settings.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Why doesn't the 'Exempted By' column show a name for older bills?"**
> A: The backfill populates historical data from the activity log where possible. Some very old records may not have this data.

> **Q: "Can I filter bills by exemption status?"**
> A: Yes — use the "Fee Exempted" filter in the filter panel (Yes/No toggle).
