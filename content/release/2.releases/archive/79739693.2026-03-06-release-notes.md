## TL;DR

- **Team permissions fix** — Users assigned to teams were silently losing access to their role permissions when switching context; this critical issue has been resolved.
- **Organisation codes & colours** — Every organisation now has a unique 4-character code and a colour identifier, making it easier to distinguish organisations across the platform.
- **Fee exemption tracking** — Bills now record which user approved a fee exemption, and the Bills table has new fee exemption filter and column options for billing oversight.
- **On-hold bill reasons now visible** — Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the reason a bill was placed on hold or rejected, reducing back-and-forth queries.
- **Support at Home permanently enabled** — The SAH feature flag has been removed; Support at Home is now always active for all organisations without any configuration.
- **Compliance team can add supplier documents** — A new permission allows Compliance Managers and Team Members to add missing documents directly to supplier profiles.

### Team Permissions Integrity Restored

Users who belong to teams were experiencing silent permission failures — losing access to features governed by their role whenever team context was active. This has been fully resolved.

**Why this matters**

Permission failures create compliance risk and disrupt staff productivity. Users who could not access records they were entitled to see would either escalate to support or find manual workarounds, both of which introduce operational overhead and potential audit risk.

**What's changing**

The underlying permission system (Spatie Permission) has been corrected to check permissions reliably regardless of whether a user has an active team context. A database schema fix ensures role and permission assignments cannot cause duplicate-key errors when teams are in use. The change is transparent to end users — permissions now work as expected.

**Who's affected**

All staff who belong to one or more teams, particularly Bill Processing teams, Care Partner teams, and any organisation using the team role-switcher. Administrators and Super Admins are unaffected.

**Action required**

No action required — the fix is applied automatically on deployment. If any users previously reported losing access after switching teams, they should now find that access is restored.

---

### Organisation Codes & Colour Identifiers

Each organisation on the platform now carries a unique short code (4 characters) and a colour, auto-generated at creation. These identifiers support clearer visual differentiation across multi-organisation workflows.

**Why this matters**

As the platform grows to support more organisations simultaneously, visual disambiguation becomes important for staff managing multiple clients or switching between contexts. Codes and colours reduce identification errors.

**What's changing**

A short alphanumeric code and a hex colour are now stored against every organisation record. These will surface progressively across the UI as they are adopted in components.

**Who's affected**

Internal staff working across multiple organisations, and any reporting or integration that references organisation identifiers.

**Action required**

No action required — codes and colours are assigned automatically. Existing organisations will be backfilled.

---

### Fee Exemption Tracking on Bills

Bills now record which user approved a TC or CC fee exemption, in addition to the exemption type itself. The Bills table includes new filter and column options for fee exemption status.

**Why this matters**

Audit trails for fee exemptions are essential for billing compliance. Knowing who granted an exemption and when supports internal review processes and reduces disputes over billing decisions.

**What's changing**

When a fee exemption is applied to a bill, the approving user is now captured and stored. Finance staff can filter the Bills table by exemption status and view the relevant columns directly.

**Who's affected**

Finance team leads, billing staff, and anyone involved in bill review or reconciliation.

**Action required**

No action required — the tracking is automatic when exemptions are applied. Existing bills will retain their current exemption status without an approver record (backfill is available via a one-time migration action).

---

### On-Hold Bill Reasons Visible to CC and Suppliers

Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the reason a bill was placed on hold or rejected, rather than receiving a generic notification.

**Why this matters**

Opaque on-hold notifications generated unnecessary support queries and delayed resolution. Surfacing the reason at the point of review allows CCs and suppliers to self-serve and correct issues without escalating to the billing team.

**What's changing**

The on-hold or rejection reason (previously only visible internally) is now displayed to Care Coordinators and suppliers when they view an affected bill. The comment field has also been expanded to support longer validation messages.

**Who's affected**

Care Coordinators, suppliers, and the billing team who handles on-hold queries.

**Action required**

No action required — the reason is shown automatically. Billing staff should continue to include clear, actionable reasons when placing bills on hold.

---

### Support at Home: Feature Flag Permanently Removed

The SAH_ENABLED configuration flag has been removed. Support at Home functionality is now always active for all organisations, with no per-environment toggle required.

**Why this matters**

Feature flags add operational complexity and create risk of misconfiguration. Removing the flag reduces the chance of SAH features being accidentally disabled in any environment and simplifies onboarding for new organisations.

**What's changing**

SAH is now unconditionally available. No configuration change is required by any team.

**Who's affected**

Operations, DevOps, and any team that previously managed the SAH_ENABLED environment variable.

**Action required**

No action required. The `SAH_ENABLED` environment variable can be removed from any `.env` files or secrets managers as a housekeeping step.

### New Compliance Permission: Add Supplier Documents

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members can now add missing documents to supplier profiles directly, without requiring an Admin to perform the action.

**Why this matters**

Compliance teams were previously blocked from completing document reviews autonomously, creating a dependency on Admin users for routine tasks. This permission closes that gap.

**What's changing**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been added to the Compliance Manager and Compliance Team Member roles. These users can now upload missing supplier documents from the supplier profile.

**Who's affected**

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members. Admin users already had this access.

**Action required**

No action required — the permission is automatically assigned to the Compliance Manager and Compliance Team Member roles on deployment.

### Team Permissions Fix

Users on teams were sometimes losing access to role-gated pages and actions when team context was active.

**Why this was changed**

A conflict in the Spatie Permission library's team context handling meant that when a user's team was set, the system would only look for team-scoped permissions — missing role permissions that are stored globally. This caused access denied errors for users who should have had full access.

**What's different**

The permission check logic has been corrected. Users who previously reported losing access when switching to a team should now find that access works as expected.

Old behaviour: Role permissions could fail silently when a team context was active.
New behaviour: Role permissions work correctly regardless of team context.

**Where in the platform**

Platform-wide — any permission-gated page or action, particularly those used by Bill Processing teams, Care Partners, and users with multiple roles.

**How to help users**

If a user reports access issues after switching teams, ask them to refresh the page and try again. If the issue persists post-deployment, escalate to development with the specific permission and team context involved.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I switched to my team and now I can't access the Bills page?"**
> A: This was a known bug — it has been fixed in this release. Ask the user to refresh their browser. If the issue continues after the deployment date, escalate to development.

> **Q: "My colleague can access something I can't, but we have the same role?"**
> A: Check whether one user has an active team context and the other doesn't. After this release, this should no longer matter — but if it does, log it as a bug.

### On-Hold Bill Reasons Now Visible to CC and Suppliers

Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the on-hold or rejection reason directly on the bill.

**Why this was changed**

The billing team was receiving a high volume of queries from CCs and suppliers asking why specific bills were on hold. The reason was only visible internally.

**What's different**

Old behaviour: CCs and suppliers saw that a bill was on hold but not why.
New behaviour: The reason is shown directly on the bill detail for CCs and suppliers.

For feature-flagged changes: This change is live and active immediately — no flag involved.

**Where in the platform**

`Bills → [any on-hold or rejected bill] → bill detail view`

**How to help users**

Walk users to the bill detail page and point out the on-hold reason section. Remind billing staff to write clear, actionable reasons when placing bills on hold.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I can see my bill is on hold but there's no reason shown?"**
> A: Ask what the bill status is. If it was placed on hold before this release, the reason field may be empty — the billing team would have set no reason at the time. The billing team can update the reason if needed.

> **Q: "I can see the reason but I don't know what to do next?"**
> A: Direct the user to their CC or the billing team based on the nature of the reason. The reason should indicate what correction is needed.

### Fee Exemption Tracking on Bills

Bills now record who approved a TC or CC fee exemption. New filter and column options are available in the Bills table.

**Why this was changed**

Billing compliance requires an audit trail for fee exemptions. The previous implementation stored the exemption type but not the approver.

**What's different**

Old behaviour: Fee exemption type was recorded but the approving user was not.
New behaviour: The approving user is captured when a fee exemption is applied. The Bills table has new columns and filters for exemption status.

**Where in the platform**

`Billing → Bills` — new filter/column options available via the column picker.

**How to help users**

If users ask about the new columns, direct them to the column selector in the Bills table. If they cannot see them, check their role — billing staff and admins should have access.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Where can I see who approved a fee exemption?"**
> A: In the Bills table, use the column selector to add the fee exemption columns. The approver is shown there or on the bill detail.

> **Q: "Old bills don't show an approver — is that a bug?"**
> A: No — the approver tracking was not in place before this release. Only exemptions applied after deployment will have an approver recorded.

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members can now add documents to supplier profiles.

**Why this was changed**

Compliance staff needed to upload documents during supplier verification reviews but required Admin intervention to do so, creating workflow delays.

**What's different**

Old behaviour: Only Admins could add documents to supplier profiles.

**Where in the platform**

`Suppliers → [supplier profile] → Documents tab`

### Supplier Verification: Police Check & NDIS Logic Fixed

The logic for determining verified supplier status has been corrected to properly include POLICE_CHECK and NDIS document types when evaluating eligibility.

**Why this was changed**

Suppliers who had submitted POLICE_CHECK or NDIS documents were incorrectly showing as unverified in some configurations.

**What's different**

Old behaviour: Certain document types were not considered when evaluating supplier verification status.
New behaviour: POLICE_CHECK and NDIS documents are correctly included in verification logic.

**Where in the platform**

`Suppliers → supplier profile → verification status`

**How to help users**

If a supplier reports their status appears incorrect despite having submitted the required documents, the fix may take effect after the next scheduled portal stage sync. Advise the user to contact support if the issue persists after 24 hours of deployment.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "My supplier uploaded their NDIS check but we still show them as unverified?"**
> A: This was a bug in the verification logic. After this deployment, the verification status should be recalculated correctly. If the supplier's status doesn't update within a day, please log a support ticket with the supplier ID.
