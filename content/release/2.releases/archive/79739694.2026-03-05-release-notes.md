## TL;DR

- **Team permissions fixed** — A defect in how permissions were stored and checked for multi-team organisations has been resolved, preventing incorrect access denials and inconsistent permission behaviour.
- **Bill rejection reasons now visible** — Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the specific on-hold or rejection reason on bills, removing the need to contact staff for context.
- **Fee exemption tracking on bills** — Bills table now displays fee exemption filters and columns, making it easier to audit and manage TC and CC fee exemptions.
- **Compliance team can upload supplier documents** — A new permission allows Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members to add missing supplier documents directly, without requiring Admin access.
- **Supplier verification logic corrected** — The check for verified suppliers now correctly accepts either a POLICE_CHECK or NDIS verification (not both), resolving an inaccurate eligibility gate.
- **Portal performance improved** — Notification queries, shared page data, and care coordinator checks have been optimised with database indexes and memoisation, reducing server load on every page load.

### Team Permission Reliability

The platform's permission system for organisations that use teams has been corrected. A structural issue in how permissions were stored against team membership was causing unpredictable access behaviour — some users could access things they shouldn't, others were blocked from things they should have.

**Why this matters**

Permission integrity is foundational to compliance and governance. Inconsistent access control creates audit risk, support overhead, and erodes trust in the system. This fix closes a significant reliability gap.

**What's changing**

The underlying permission table structure for multi-team organisations has been corrected. All team-scoped permissions are now stored and evaluated consistently. The fix was applied as a hotfix given the severity of the issue.

**Who's affected**

Any organisation that uses the Teams feature. Admins and staff who manage team membership and role assignments.

**Action required**

No action required — the fix is applied automatically. Organisations that experienced unexpected permission behaviour should test their key workflows to confirm access is now correct.

### Compliance Team: Upload Supplier Documents

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members can now add missing documents to supplier profiles directly from the staff portal.

**Why this matters**

Previously, uploading missing supplier documents required Admin-level access, creating a bottleneck. Compliance staff had to escalate document uploads to Admins, slowing down supplier onboarding and compliance review cycles.

**What's changing**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been added and assigned to the Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles. Compliance staff will see the "Add" button on the missing documents view without needing Admin elevation.

**Who's affected**

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members.

**Action required**

No action required — the permission is automatically applied to the relevant roles. Compliance team leads should inform their staff that they can now upload missing documents directly.

---

### Fee Exemption Visibility on Bills

Finance and billing staff can now filter and view fee exemption status directly on the Bills table.

**Why this matters**

Fee exemptions were previously difficult to audit at scale — staff had to open individual bills to check exemption status. This change brings fee exemption data into the main bills view, enabling bulk review and reporting.

**What's changing**

The Bills table now includes fee exemption filter columns, making TC and CC fee exemptions visible and filterable without opening each bill.

**Who's affected**

Finance team, billing staff, and anyone responsible for reviewing or auditing bills.

**Action required**

No action required — the new columns are immediately available in the Bills table.

---

### Bill Rejection Reasons Visible to Care Coordinators and Suppliers

When a bill is placed on hold or rejected, the specific reason is now displayed directly to care coordinators and suppliers — not just internal staff.

**Why this matters**

Care coordinators and suppliers were previously unaware of why their bills were on hold. This generated support requests to staff and delayed resolution. Showing the reason directly eliminates this friction and accelerates billing resolution.

**What's changing**

The on-hold or rejection reason text on a bill is now visible to the care coordinator and supplier who submitted it. No separate communication is needed.

**Who's affected**

Care coordinators and suppliers who submit bills.

**Action required**

No action required. Staff should be aware that the reason they enter when placing a bill on hold will now be seen by the submitting CC or supplier.

### Team Permissions Fixed

Some users in organisations that use teams were experiencing unexpected permission issues — either being blocked from things they should have access to, or occasionally seeing things they shouldn't. This has been fixed.

We identified a structural issue in how permissions were being stored for team members. This was causing inconsistent behaviour that was difficult to predict and reproduce.

The permission system for team-based access has been corrected. You should now see consistent, predictable access to the areas you're assigned to.

`Settings → Teams` — your team memberships and permissions remain unchanged.

### Team Permissions Fixed

#### Why this was changed

A structural defect was found in how the permission tables stored team-scoped permissions. This caused inconsistent permission behaviour for users in multi-team organisations — some users were denied access they should have had, and in some cases had access they shouldn't.

**What's different**

Before: Team permissions were stored with incorrect primary key structure, causing the permission middleware to evaluate them inconsistently.
After: The permission table structure for teams has been corrected. All permissions are now stored and evaluated correctly.

**Where in the platform**

Affects any page where access is controlled by team-based permissions. Most visible in role-restricted sections like billing, compliance, and client management.

**How to help users**

If users report "I can't access something I should be able to" or "I can see something I shouldn't" in a team-based organisation, this fix should resolve it. Ask them to refresh their session (log out and back in) and test again.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I still can't access [feature] even after the fix?"**
> A: Ask them to log out and log back in to clear their session. If the issue persists, check their team membership and role assignment in Settings → Teams.

> **Q: "Did my permissions change?"**
> A: No deliberate changes were made to who has access to what. The fix only corrects how existing permissions are stored and read. If something changed unexpectedly, escalate for investigation.

### New Permission: Compliance Team Can Upload Supplier Documents

#### Why this was changed

Uploading missing supplier documents was restricted to Admin users only. Compliance staff needed Admin intervention to complete routine compliance tasks.

**What's different**

The new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission is now assigned to Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles. These users will see an **Add** button on the missing documents tab for suppliers.

**Where in the platform**

`Suppliers → [Supplier] → Missing Documents` — the Add button is now visible to Compliance staff.

**How to help users**

If a compliance staff member says they can't see the Add button, check their assigned role in the system. They need to be assigned Compliance Manager or Compliance Team Member.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I'm a Compliance Team Member but I can't see the Add button?"**
> A: Check that their role is correctly assigned as Compliance Manager or Compliance Team Member (not just Compliance). If correct and still not showing, escalate.

> **Q: "Can suppliers see other suppliers' documents?"**
> A: No — this only affects what Compliance staff can do within the staff portal. Suppliers cannot see each other's documents.

### Bill On-Hold Reasons Now Visible to CC and Suppliers

#### Why this was changed

Care coordinators and suppliers had no way to see why their bill was on hold without contacting support. This generated unnecessary support load.

**What's different**

Before: On-hold reason was only visible to internal staff.
After: The rejection or on-hold reason text is now shown on the bill detail page for the care coordinator or supplier who submitted it.

**Where in the platform**

`Billing → Bills → [Bill Name]` — the on-hold reason appears on the bill detail view.

**How to help users**

If a CC or supplier asks why their bill is on hold, direct them to the bill detail page. The reason should now appear there. Remind staff that the reason they type when placing a bill on hold will be seen by the submitter.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "The on-hold reason is blank on my bill?"**
> A: The staff member who placed it on hold may not have entered a reason. Ask the internal team to update the bill with a reason.

> **Q: "Can I see the history of why my bill was previously on hold?"**
> A: Currently only the most recent on-hold reason is shown. Historical reasons are not available in the UI.
