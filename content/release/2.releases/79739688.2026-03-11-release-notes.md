## TL;DR

- **Team permission fix** — A bug causing users to be incorrectly denied access in team-scoped contexts has been resolved, restoring correct permission behaviour across the platform.
- **Bill on-hold and rejection reasons** — Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the reason a bill was placed on hold or rejected directly on the bill, eliminating the need to contact support for clarification.
- **Fee exemption tracking** — When TC or CC loading fees are marked as exempt on a bill, the system now records who made the exemption and when, providing a full audit trail.
- **Compliance manager document permissions** — A new dedicated permission allows Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members to add missing supplier documents without requiring full organisation-management access.
- **Supplier document alert fix** — Suppliers with no active services (zero-price price list) no longer receive false-positive alerts about missing documents, reducing unnecessary support contacts.
- **Organisation short codes and colours** — Every verified organisation is now automatically assigned a unique short code and deterministic brand colour for use in display and reporting contexts.

### Team Permission Reliability Restored

**Why this matters**

A defect in the platform's permission enforcement layer was causing users to be incorrectly denied access to features in team-scoped contexts. This has been fully resolved, including a database migration to fix the underlying data integrity issue with permission table primary keys.

**What's changing**

Permission checks now correctly evaluate against the authenticated user's full permission set rather than just the current role context. The fix also prevents queued background jobs from leaking team context between executions, which was a secondary source of permission inconsistency.

**Who's affected**

All staff and care coordinator users. The fix is especially significant for organisations using team-based access control.

**Action required**

No action required — the fix is applied automatically as part of this deployment.

---

### Bill On-Hold and Rejection Reasons Now Visible

**Why this matters**

When a bill is placed on hold or rejected, the reason was previously only visible to internal staff. Care coordinators and suppliers had to contact support to understand why a bill was held up, creating unnecessary friction and delay in the billing cycle.

**What's changing**

The reason for a hold or rejection is now displayed directly on the bill view for care coordinators and suppliers. This reduces the need for support interactions and speeds up billing resolution.

**Who's affected**

Finance teams managing bills, care coordinators reviewing their bills, and suppliers awaiting payment.

**Action required**

No action required — the information is now visible automatically on bills where a reason has been recorded.

---

### Fee Exemption Audit Trail

**Why this matters**

TC and CC loading fees can be marked as exempt on applicable bills. Previously there was no record of who made the exemption or when, creating a gap in the audit trail for finance and compliance reviews.

**What's changing**

When a fee exemption is applied, the system now records which user made the change. The Bills table includes new filtering options to view exempted bills and identify the exempting user. A backfill has been run to populate historical exemption records from existing activity logs.

**Who's affected**

Finance teams and billing administrators. The Bills table now includes an "Is Fee Exempted" filter and an "Exempted By" column.

**Action required**

No action required — data is captured automatically going forward. Historical data has been backfilled where possible.

---

### Compliance Manager Document Permissions

**Why this matters**

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members previously required the broad "manage organisation" permission to add missing supplier documents, granting more access than their role requires. This was a compliance and security gap.

**What's changing**

A dedicated `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been introduced. Compliance Manager and Compliance Team Member roles have been granted this permission. The broad "manage organisation" requirement has been removed from the document upload workflow.

**Who's affected**

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members. Their access is now correctly scoped.

**Action required**

No action required — roles have been updated automatically.

---

### Supplier Document Alert Accuracy

**Why this matters**

Suppliers whose price lists contained only zero-value entries (no active services) were receiving alerts about missing compliance documents even though those documents weren't relevant to their inactive status. This generated unnecessary support contacts and supplier confusion.

**What's changing**

The platform now checks whether a supplier has active (non-zero) services before determining which compliance documents are required. Suppliers with no active services will no longer receive false-positive document alerts. A warning card is now shown instead, directing them to set up their price list.

**Who's affected**

Supplier organisations and the internal compliance team managing supplier onboarding.

**Action required**

No action required — the logic is updated automatically.

---

### Organisation Short Codes and Brand Colours

**Why this matters**

As the platform grows, a concise, unique identifier for each organisation improves readability in reports, dashboards, and UI contexts where full organisation names are too long.

**What's changing**

Every verified organisation is automatically assigned a 3–4 character short code derived from its name and a deterministic brand colour based on that code. These are generated in a background process and stored on the organisation record.

**Who's affected**

All internal staff who view organisation data. The short codes and colours will progressively appear in reporting and UI contexts.

**Action required**

No action required — codes and colours are generated automatically for all verified organisations.

### Your Bill Now Shows Why It's on Hold or Rejected

**Why we built this**

We heard from care coordinators and suppliers that when a bill was placed on hold or rejected, there was no clear explanation visible in the portal — you had to call or email to find out why. That was slow and frustrating, especially when the fix was straightforward.

**What's changed**

The on-hold or rejection reason is now displayed directly on the bill, alongside the existing status badge. If a comment was recorded when the bill was held or rejected, you'll see it right there without needing to contact anyone.

**Where to find it**

`Bills → [Select a bill] → Bill Status`

The reason appears next to the status badge. On larger screens, it shows as a tooltip. On mobile, the reason text appears inline below the badge.

**How to use it**

No steps needed — the reason appears automatically if one was recorded.

---

### Fee Exemption Now Shows Who Approved It

**Why we built this**

For bills where TC or CC loading fees have been marked as exempt, there was previously no record of who approved the exemption. Finance users need this for audit and reconciliation purposes.

**What's changed**

The Bills table now has an "Is Fee Exempted" filter and an "Exempted By" column, so you can quickly see which bills have exemptions and who approved each one.

**Where to find it**

`Bills → Filters → Is Fee Exempted`

**How to use it**

1. Go to **Bills**
2. Open the **Filters** panel
3. Toggle **Is Fee Exempted** to filter for exempted bills
4. The **Exempted By** column shows who approved each exemption

### Supplier Document Alerts Are More Accurate

**Why we built this**

Some suppliers were seeing alerts about missing compliance documents even though those documents weren't relevant to them — because they hadn't yet set up an active service on their price list.

**What's changed**

The document requirement check now only applies to suppliers who have active services on their price list. If you have no active services, you'll see a clear prompt to set up your price list instead of an irrelevant document alert.

**Where to find it**

`Supplier Dashboard → Document Status`

**How to use it**

No steps needed — the alert now only appears when your documents are genuinely required based on your active services.

**Where to find it**

`Bills → Edit → Bill Items`

**How to use it**

No steps needed — if a bill item can't be deleted, an explanation will appear automatically.

### Team Permission Fix — All Users

**Why this was changed**

A bug in the permission enforcement middleware was causing team-scoped permission checks to evaluate incorrectly. Users could be denied features they should have access to, depending on how their team context was set at the time of the request.

**What's different**

The `CheckPermission` middleware now calls `hasPermissionTo()` directly on the user rather than routing through `currentRole`. The `SetPermissionTeamContext` middleware now explicitly resets the team context to 0 when no team is set, preventing context leakage between requests and queued jobs.

**Where in the platform**

Platform-wide — affects all permission-gated features.

**How to help users**

If users report "access denied" errors that don't match their role, ask them to log out and log back in to clear any cached session state. If the issue persists post-deployment, escalate as a permission configuration query.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I'm still getting access denied after the fix?"**
> A: Ask the user to log out and log back in. If the issue persists, check their role assignment and team context in the admin panel.

> **Q: "Did anyone's access change?"**
> A: The fix restores correct behaviour. Users who were incorrectly blocked will regain access. No user should lose access as a result of this fix.

### Bill On-Hold and Rejection Reasons — Billing

**Why this was changed**

Care coordinators and suppliers were contacting support to ask why their bill was on hold or rejected. The reason field was only visible internally.

**What's different**

The reason (and comment, if recorded) now appear on the bill's status badge for care coordinators and suppliers. Old behaviour: plain status badge with no reason. New behaviour: status badge with attached reason tooltip or inline text.

**Where in the platform**

`Bills → [Any bill with on-hold or rejected status] → Status badge`

**How to help users**

Direct users to open the bill and look at the status badge. The reason appears adjacent to the badge. If no reason is shown, one wasn't recorded when the bill was put on hold — this is a data gap, not a bug.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "My bill says on hold but there's no reason shown?"**
> A: A reason is only shown if one was recorded at the time the bill was placed on hold. If no reason appears, the action was taken without a reason being entered. Ask the coordinator or billing team if they can provide more context.

> **Q: "Where exactly do I find the reason?"**
> A: It appears next to the status badge on the bill page. On desktop it shows as a tooltip; on mobile it shows as text below the badge.

---

### Fee Exemption Tracking — Bills

**Why this was changed**

Finance teams needed an audit trail for fee exemptions. Previously, exemptions were applied with no record of who approved them.

**What's different**

The Bills table now shows "Is Fee Exempted" (yes/no) and "Exempted By" (user name). A backfill has populated historical records where possible from activity logs.

**Where in the platform**

`Bills → Table → Filters → Is Fee Exempted` and Bills table `Exempted By` column.

**How to help users**

If users ask about the new column, direct them to the Bills table filters. If the "Exempted By" field is empty for an older bill, the backfill didn't find a matching activity log entry — this is expected for older records.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Why is 'Exempted By' blank for some older bills?"**
> A: The backfill was run from activity logs, but some older records may not have had detailed logs. This is expected and affects historical data only — all new exemptions will be tracked going forward.

### Compliance Manager Document Access — Suppliers

**Why this was changed**

Compliance Managers needed broad "manage organisation" access to upload missing supplier documents, which granted more permissions than appropriate for the role.

**What's different**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission now controls access to the missing documents upload in the Staff Supplier view. Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles have this permission. "Manage organisation" is no longer required.

**Where in the platform**

`Staff → Suppliers → [Supplier] → Missing Documents`

**How to help users**

If a Compliance Manager reports they can't add documents, check their role assignment. They need the "Compliance Manager" or "Compliance Team Member" role. If an Admin can't add documents, check they haven't had the Admin role removed.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Compliance Manager says they can't see the add document option?"**
> A: Confirm they have the Compliance Manager or Compliance Team Member role assigned. The role grants the `can-add-supplier-documents` permission automatically.

---

### Supplier Document Alerts — Supplier Portal

**Why this was changed**

Suppliers with no active services (zero-price entries on their price list) were seeing incorrect alerts about missing compliance documents.

**What's different**

Old behaviour: document alerts triggered for all suppliers regardless of service status. New behaviour: document alerts only trigger for suppliers who have at least one service with a non-zero price. Suppliers with no active services see a prompt to set up their price list instead.

**Where in the platform**

Supplier Dashboard → Document Status section.

**How to help users**

If a supplier says their document alert has disappeared, ask them to check their price list. If they genuinely have no active services, the alert being gone is correct behaviour. Direct them to set up their price list to re-activate document requirements.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "A supplier says their documents are fine but they still see an alert?"**
> A: Check if the supplier has at least one service with a non-zero price on their price list. If all prices are zero, the alert should not show — escalate if it persists.

> **Q: "A supplier asks why they now see a 'set up your price list' message?"**
> A: This means they have no active services on their price list. Direct them to `Supplier Dashboard → Price List` to add their services and rates.

---

### Bill Item Deletion Protection — Billing

**Why this was changed**

Deleting a bill item that had already been processed through MYOB could silently create reconciliation issues.

**What's different**

Bill items with linked transactions (MYOB or otherwise) now cannot be deleted. A clear error message is shown: "Cannot delete this bill item because it has linked transactions."

**Where in the platform**

`Bills → Edit → Bill Items → Delete`

**How to help users**

If a user reports they can't delete a bill item, explain that it has linked transactions and cannot be deleted for data integrity reasons. If the bill item needs to be removed, they may need to reverse the linked transactions first.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "User can't delete a bill item, getting an error message?"**
> A: This is expected if the bill item has linked MYOB transactions. It's a protection against data corruption. Advise the user to contact the finance team about reversing the transactions if removal is genuinely needed.
