## TL;DR

- **Team permissions fixed** — A critical bug preventing users assigned to teams from accessing the platform has been resolved, restoring correct permission behaviour across all roles and teams.
- **Support at Home permanently enabled** — The legacy `SAH_ENABLED` feature flag has been removed; Support at Home is now always active, eliminating a configuration overhead and simplifying system behaviour.
- **Bill on-hold reasons now visible** — Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the exact reason a bill was placed on hold or rejected, reducing back-and-forth support queries.
- **Fee exemption audit trail** — Loading fee exemptions on bills now record which staff member granted the exemption, giving managers a clear accountability record.
- **Organisation identifiers** — All verified supplier organisations now have auto-generated short codes (e.g. "CACO") and brand colours for visual identification across the platform.
- **Supplier verification accuracy** — The verified supplier logic now correctly considers NDIS Worker Screening and police check documents, fixing a compliance gap.

### Team Permissions Restored

Users assigned to teams can now access the platform correctly after a critical permissions bug was blocking them.

**Why this matters**

Following the rollout of team-based permission management, a data structure issue in the permissions tables caused users who belonged to teams to lose access to areas they are authorised to use. This was an operational disruption for any user with a team assignment and represented an access control risk requiring immediate resolution.

**What's changing**

The underlying permissions database schema has been corrected to include team context in its primary keys, and the permission-checking logic has been updated to correctly resolve permissions across both team-scoped and global contexts. Users on teams now receive the same access they had prior to team management being introduced.

**Who's affected**

Any user who has been assigned to a team within the platform. Team leaders, Bill Processing team members, and other role-specific team assignments are all affected.

**Action required**

No action required — permissions are restored automatically. If any users continue to report access issues after this release, escalate to the technical team.

### Support at Home Permanently Active

Support at Home (SAH) functionality is now always enabled across the platform, removing the final legacy configuration toggle.

**Why this matters**

SAH has been live and in use across all organisations for some time. The legacy `SAH_ENABLED` feature flag was a holdover from the initial rollout that added unnecessary complexity to system configuration, deployment processes, and code maintenance. Removing it reduces technical debt and eliminates the risk of misconfiguration in future deployments.

**What's changing**

The SAH feature flag has been removed. All SAH-related functionality — including funding allocation rules, billing calculations, and Support at Home quarter management — now operates unconditionally. No organisation-level or environment-level toggle exists anymore.

**Who's affected**

Finance teams, billing staff, and anyone involved in SAH care management billing. System behaviour will be identical to current — this is a simplification, not a feature change.

**Action required**

No action required. If any environment-specific `SAH_ENABLED` configuration references exist in your deployment setup, they can be safely removed.

### Bill Management: On-Hold Reasons & Fee Exemption Tracking

Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the reason their bill is on hold, and loading fee exemptions now carry a full audit trail.

**Why this matters**

Bills placed on hold or rejected were previously showing a generic message with no context, requiring support staff to look up the reason manually and relay it. This added friction and delay to billing resolution. Separately, loading fee exemptions had no record of who approved them — a gap in financial accountability.

**What's changing**

- The on-hold or rejection reason is now displayed directly on the bill view for care coordinators and suppliers, so they can self-serve and take corrective action without contacting support.
- Loading fee exemptions now record the staff member who granted the exemption, visible in the bill record for audit purposes.
- Fee exemption filter columns have been added to the Bills table, making it easier to search and report on exempted bills.

**Who's affected**

Finance team members managing billing, care coordinators and suppliers who submit or review bills, and compliance/audit teams who review fee exemptions.

**Action required**

No action required — this is available immediately. Finance managers should note that all future fee exemptions will now show the approving staff member.

### Organisation Short Codes and Brand Colours

All verified supplier organisations now have an auto-generated short code (3–4 characters) and a brand colour, enabling visual identification across the platform.

**Why this matters**

As the platform scales to manage hundreds of organisations, visual differentiation and compact identifiers support faster workflows in dashboards, reports, and UI components. The codes and colours are deterministic — the same name always produces the same code and colour — ensuring consistency.

**What's changing**

Each verified organisation receives a short code derived from their legal trading name (e.g. "Care Connect" → "CACO") and a hex colour generated from that code. All existing verified organisations will be assigned codes automatically as part of the post-deployment process.

**Who's affected**

Platform administrators and internal teams who work with organisation data. End users may see organisation codes appearing in UI components in future releases as they are progressively adopted.

**Action required**

No action required. Platform administrators running the post-deployment Artisan command will trigger bulk code assignment for all existing verified organisations.

### Supplier Verification Accuracy Improved

The logic for determining whether a supplier is "verified" now correctly accounts for NDIS Worker Screening checks and police check documents.

**Why this matters**

Compliance teams rely on the verified status to determine whether a supplier meets required checks before being engaged. Incorrect verification logic was potentially showing some suppliers as unverified when they had met the required conditions, affecting both compliance reporting and the supplier experience.

**What's changing**

The verification logic now correctly includes both POLICE_CHECK and NDIS Worker Screening documents when evaluating a supplier's compliance status. The expired document alert on the supplier dashboard has also been corrected so it only shows when genuinely applicable.

**Who's affected**

Compliance managers, Compliance Team Members, and suppliers who have undergone worker screening or police checks.

**Action required**

No action required — the fix applies automatically. Compliance managers may wish to review supplier records that were recently flagged for re-verification.

### Team Permissions Bug Fixed

Users assigned to teams can now access the platform correctly.

**Why this was changed**

A database schema issue in the Spatie Permission tables — introduced when team-based permissions were enabled — caused users with team assignments to lose access to areas they're authorised for. This was a priority fix affecting any user with a `current_team_id` set.

**What's different**

Previously: Users with a team context set were failing permission checks because the permission table primary keys didn't include `team_id`, causing incorrect permission resolution.

Now: The permission tables have been corrected. Permission checks now correctly resolve both team-scoped and global permissions. Users on teams have full access to their authorised areas.

**Where in the platform**

Platform-wide — any route protected by `permission:` middleware or using `can()` / `authorize()` checks was affected for users on teams.

**How to help users**

If a user reports they can't access something they previously could, and they're on a team:
1. Confirm their team assignment (`Settings → Users → [User] → Teams`)
2. Confirm their role is correct
3. Ask them to log out and back in
4. If the issue persists after this release, raise a technical ticket

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I still can't access the Bills page even after the fix?"**
> A: Check the user's current role. The fix resolves permission resolution for team-context users but doesn't change which roles have access. If their role should grant access, raise a technical ticket.

> **Q: "My team member says their permissions changed after being added to a team?"**
> A: This was the bug. After this release, team membership should not affect existing role-based permission grants. If it does, escalate to the technical team.

> **Q: "Why did this break in the first place?"**
> A: The team permission feature added `team_id` to permission tables but didn't update composite primary keys — this caused permission resolution failures for users in a team context. This release fixes the root cause with a migration and updated permission logic.

When a staff member invites a supplier, the supplier's portal stage is now correctly set to `INVITED`.

**What's different**

`Suppliers → [Invite supplier flow]`

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "A supplier says they received an invite email but can't proceed?"**
