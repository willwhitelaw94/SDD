## TL;DR

- **Team permissions fixed** — A critical infrastructure bug in the team permissions system has been resolved, ensuring role and permission checks behave correctly across all team contexts.
- **Fee exemption tracking** — Bills now record which staff member approved a fee exemption, with new filters in the Bills table to find exempted bills by who approved them.
- **Bill on-hold/rejection reason visible** — Care coordinators and suppliers can now see the reason and comment for why a bill was placed on hold or rejected directly on the bill view.
- **Supplier NDIS worker check accepted** — "Just Me" suppliers can now complete verification with either a Police Check or an NDIS Worker screening document, removing a barrier for eligible suppliers.
- **Compliance team can add missing documents** — A new permission allows Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members to upload missing supplier documents directly, without needing Admin access.
- **Supplier documents always visible** — The supplier documents page now shows all document sections even when no service pricing exists, fixing a display issue that hid required documents.

### Critical: Team Permissions Infrastructure Fixed

The role and permission system that governs what users can see and do across the portal has been repaired for organisations using teams.

**Why this matters**

Incorrect permission behaviour — where users could see pages or actions they should not, or conversely be locked out of things they should access — represents both an operational risk and a compliance concern. This fix ensures the permissions framework behaves as intended.

**What's changing**

A structural defect in how the permissions system stored and checked team-based roles has been corrected. The database records for team membership have been normalised, and the permission checking logic has been made consistent. This affects every user whose access is managed through teams.

**Who's affected**

All users whose roles are assigned within a team context. Organisations using team-based role management will see permissions enforced correctly going forward.

**Action required**

No action required — the fix is applied automatically as part of this release. If you observe any unexpected changes in what users can access, contact support.

### Fee Exemption Tracking on Bills

The portal now records exactly who approved a fee exemption on a bill, providing a clear audit trail.

**Why this matters**

Fee exemptions affect billing revenue. Previously, there was no record of which staff member approved an exemption, making it difficult to audit decisions or investigate discrepancies. This closes an accountability gap.

**What's changing**

When a TC or CC fee is marked as exempt on a bill, the system now stores the name of the staff member who approved the exemption. This information is visible in the Bills table alongside new filters to find all bills where fees were exempted and who approved them.

**Who's affected**

Finance team leads and billing staff who review and approve fee exemptions.

**Action required**

No action required — fee exemption approvals are recorded automatically from this release forward. Existing exemptions without an approver will not be backfilled automatically.

### Bill On-Hold & Rejection Reason Now Visible to Suppliers and Care Coordinators

When a bill is placed on hold or rejected, the reason and supporting comment are now shown directly on the bill view.

**Why this matters**

Previously, suppliers and care coordinators had to contact the finance team to understand why a bill was on hold or rejected, creating unnecessary back-and-forth. This change makes the process more self-service and reduces support load.

**What's changing**

A new status badge on the bill view displays the on-hold or rejection reason, along with the comment, so the affected party can immediately see what action is required.

**Who's affected**

Suppliers and Care Coordinators who receive bills that are placed on hold or rejected.

**Action required**

No action required — this is available immediately. Consider communicating to suppliers and care coordinators that this information is now visible on their bill views.

### Supplier Verification: NDIS Worker Check Now Accepted

"Just Me" suppliers can now complete their verification using either a Police Check or an NDIS Worker Screening document.

**Why this matters**

Some eligible individual suppliers were unable to complete verification because they held an NDIS Worker Screening certificate rather than a Police Check. This was a barrier to onboarding compliant suppliers who had already passed the relevant national check.

**What's changing**

The document verification logic for "Just Me" suppliers has been updated to accept either a Police Check or an NDIS Worker Screening document as satisfying the worker check requirement. Both are equally valid.

**Who's affected**

Compliance team and Operations, as well as individual ("Just Me") suppliers currently in a pending verification state due to this document type mismatch.

**Action required**

Review any "Just Me" suppliers currently stuck in verification. Those with a valid NDIS Worker Screening document will now be eligible to progress to VERIFIED status.

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members.

### Critical: Team Permissions Fixed

The permissions system for team-based role management has been fixed.

**Why this was changed**

A structural bug in how the Spatie Permission package stores team-scoped roles was causing permission checks to behave inconsistently — some users had incorrect access, others were incorrectly denied.

**What's different**

The database records for team membership have been normalised (NULL team_id values converted to 0), and the permission checking logic has been corrected. Behaviour should now be consistent and correct for all team contexts.

**Where in the platform**

Affects any feature that uses role-based permission checks — which is most of the portal.

**How to help users**

If a user reports that they suddenly can or cannot access something after this release, escalate to the dev team as it may indicate a lingering permission issue rather than a user error.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I used to be able to see X and now I can't?"**
> A: This could be a result of the permissions fix correcting previously incorrect access. Check the user's assigned roles and permissions in their profile. If the access was legitimate, the admin can re-assign the appropriate role.

> **Q: "A user has access to something they shouldn't?"**
> A: Same root cause — the fix may have corrected previously granted over-access. Verify the user's role assignment is correct.

### Fee Exemption Tracking on Bills

Bills now record who approved a fee exemption.

**Why this was changed**

There was no audit trail for fee exemption approvals. Finance managers needed a way to see who had approved each exemption.

**What's different**

When a fee is marked as exempt, the system stores the approving user. The Bills table now has two new columns/filters: "Fee Exempted" (yes/no) and "Exempted By" (the user's name).

**Where in the platform**

Staff portal → Bills table. The new columns and filters appear in the Bills list view.

**How to help users**

Walk users through the new filters: in the Bills table, they can filter by "Is Fee Exempted" to find all exempted bills, and by "Exempted By" to find bills exempted by a specific person.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I exempted fees before this update — will those show who did it?"**
> A: No. The approver tracking only applies from this release onwards. Historical exemptions won't have an approver recorded.

> **Q: "I can't see the 'Exempted By' column in Bills?"**
> A: Check that the user has the column enabled in their Bills table view. They may need to add it via the column selector.

### Bill On-Hold / Rejection Reason Now Visible

Suppliers and care coordinators can now see why a bill is on hold or rejected directly on the bill.

**Why this was changed**

Support was receiving many queries from suppliers and CCs asking why their bill was on hold, because the reason was only visible to internal staff.

**What's different**

A new status badge next to the bill status shows the on-hold or rejection reason. Hovering shows the full comment. Previously, only the status badge was shown.

**Where in the platform**

Care Coordinator portal → Bills → Bill detail view. Supplier portal → Bills → Bill detail view.

**How to help users**

Direct users to open the specific bill. The reason badge will be visible next to the status. If a user says they still cannot see a reason, confirm the bill is actually in On Hold or Rejected status.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I can see the bill is on hold but there's no reason shown?"**
> A: The reason badge only appears if a reason was recorded when the bill was placed on hold. If none was recorded, the badge won't appear. Check internally what reason was given.

> **Q: "Who can see the on-hold reason?"**
> A: The Care Coordinator or Supplier associated with the bill can see it. Internal staff have always been able to see it.

### Supplier Verification: NDIS Worker Check Accepted

"Just Me" suppliers can now verify using an NDIS Worker Screening document.

**Why this was changed**

Suppliers who had completed their NDIS Worker Screening (instead of a Police Check) were getting stuck in the verification process because only Police Check documents were recognised.

**What's different**

Old behaviour: JUST ME suppliers required a Police Check document to pass the worker check verification step.
New behaviour: Either a Police Check OR an NDIS Worker Screening document is accepted.

**Where in the platform**

Staff portal → Suppliers → Supplier detail → Documents & Verification.

**How to help users**

If a JUST ME supplier says they're stuck in verification and they have an NDIS Worker Screening document, ask them to upload it in the Documents section. Once verified by the compliance team, they should progress to VERIFIED.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "A supplier has an NDIS Worker Screening certificate — why are they still unverified?"**
> A: The document needs to be uploaded to the portal and then verified/approved by the compliance team. Check that the document has been uploaded and is in the right category.

> **Q: "Can both Police Check AND NDIS Worker Screening be uploaded?"**
> A: Yes — either satisfies the requirement. Both can be uploaded if the supplier chooses.

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members can now add missing supplier documents.

Previously this required Admin access, causing delays. The compliance workflow needed to be self-contained.

> A: Confirm the user's role is exactly "Compliance Manager" or "Compliance Team Member". Other roles (e.g., Compliance Viewer) do not have this permission.
