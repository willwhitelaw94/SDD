- **Budgets Reloaded** — The backend and frontend foundations for a major budget management redesign are now live, including visual organisation identifiers (codes and colours) and a new funding streams API.
- **Bill fee exemption tracking** — Fee exemptions on bills now record which staff member applied the exemption, with new filter columns for compliance reporting.

### Budgets Reloaded — Foundation Release

The Trilogy Care portal's budget management system is being redesigned from the ground up to improve clarity, accuracy, and the care planning experience for all stakeholders.

**Why this matters**

Budget management is central to every care package. The current system has grown complex over time, making it difficult for care coordinators and operations staff to quickly understand a client's financial position. The Budgets Reloaded project addresses this by rebuilding the budget interface with clearer data models, better visual design, and improved tooling.

**What's changing**

This release delivers the foundational infrastructure for Budgets Reloaded — new database fields that give each organisation a unique visual identifier (short code and colour), a new funding streams API endpoint, and the backend/frontend scaffolding for the redesigned budget pages. No visible UI changes are expected for most users yet; this release prepares the system for the full rollout in upcoming releases.

**Who's affected**

Operations, finance, and care coordination teams who use budget and service plan features. No workflow changes are required at this stage.

**Action required**

No action required at this stage. Subsequent releases will introduce the new budget UI progressively.

---

### Bill Fee Exemption Tracking

Organisations now have a complete audit trail of who applied loading fee exemptions on bills.

**Why this matters**

Loading fee exemptions affect billing accuracy and revenue. Previously, there was no way to track which staff member applied an exemption, creating a gap in compliance reporting and dispute resolution.

**What's changing**

Bills now record the staff member who applied or cleared a fee exemption. New filter and display columns in the bills table make it easy to search for and audit exempted bills. A backfill process has been run to populate historical exemption data where possible.

**Who's affected**

Finance teams, billing processors, and compliance staff who manage or review bill loading fees.

**Action required**

No action required. The exemption tracking is automatic from this release forward. Finance leads may wish to review the new filter column in the bills table for reporting purposes.

---

### On-Hold and Rejection Visibility for Care Coordinators & Suppliers

Care Coordinators and Suppliers can now see why a bill has been placed on hold or rejected, directly on the bill.

**Why this matters**

Previously, when a bill was placed on hold or rejected, the reason was only visible to internal staff. Care Coordinators and Suppliers had to contact support to understand what action was needed, creating unnecessary back-and-forth and delays in bill resolution.

**What's changing**

On-hold and rejection reason badges and tooltips now appear on bills visible to Care Coordinators and Suppliers, giving them the information they need to take action without escalating to support.

**Who's affected**

Care Coordinators, Suppliers, and support staff who handle billing queries.

**Action required**

No action required. The information is now visible automatically.

---

### Supplier Portal Verification & Document Accuracy

A series of fixes improve the accuracy of supplier verification status, document visibility, and onboarding stage.

**Why this matters**

Inaccurate supplier portal stages and missing document displays were causing confusion during onboarding and compliance reviews, requiring manual intervention from the operations team.

**What's changing**

- Suppliers with police checks or NDIS Worker Screening documents are now correctly progressed to Verified status.
- The expired document alert on the supplier dashboard no longer shows incorrectly when no documents exist.
- Business verification documents now display correctly even when a supplier has no services in their price list.
- Suppliers invited from the staff portal are now correctly set to the Invited stage.

**Who's affected**

Compliance teams, operations staff, and suppliers going through the onboarding process.

**Action required**

No action required. The fixes apply automatically to new and existing supplier records.

---

### Team Permission Reliability (Hotfix)

A critical reliability fix for the permission system in multi-team contexts.

**Why this matters**

The permission system was incorrectly evaluating global permissions when a team context was active, and in some cases creating duplicate key violations when assigning roles. This could result in users being denied access they should have, or errors during role assignment.

**What's changing**

The Spatie Teams permission system has been patched to correctly check global (team-agnostic) role permissions alongside team-specific ones, and to prevent duplicate primary key violations. The default team context in queued jobs and CLI commands is now set correctly to avoid edge cases.

**Who's affected**

All users, particularly those with multi-team or cross-organisational access.

**Action required**

No action required. The fix is applied automatically.

---

### New Compliance Document Permission

Compliance staff can now add missing documents to supplier records via a dedicated, role-controlled permission.

**Why this matters**

Previously, adding missing documents to a supplier profile required Admin access, meaning compliance managers had to either escalate requests or be granted broader access than needed. This creates unnecessary friction in compliance workflows and is inconsistent with the principle of least privilege.

**What's changing**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been created and assigned to Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles. Compliance staff can now upload missing documents directly.

**Who's affected**

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members.

**Action required**

No action required — the permission is automatically assigned to the correct roles.

### Bills: On-Hold and Rejection Reasons Now Visible

### Bills: On-Hold and Rejection Reasons Now Visible to CCs and Suppliers

**Why this was changed**

CCs and Suppliers were frequently contacting support to ask why their bills were on hold or rejected. The information was already in the system but not surfaced to them.

**What's different**

On-hold and rejection reason badges/tooltips now appear directly on bills for CC and Supplier users. Previously, only internal staff could see the reason; now all relevant parties can see it.

**Where in the platform**

`Bills → [On-hold or rejected bill] → Status badge`

**How to help users**

If a user reports they can't see the reason for their bill's status, check their user role — the badge is visible to Care Coordinator and Supplier roles. Staff with billing access also see it.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Why is my bill on hold?"**
> A: Users can now see the reason directly on the bill by hovering over the status badge. If they still can't see it, confirm their role is Care Coordinator or Supplier. If the bill has no reason set, escalate to billing for manual review.

> **Q: "I can see the badge but there's no reason shown?"**
> A: Some older on-hold bills may have been placed on hold before structured reasons were recorded. These will show a generic message. New on-hold actions will always include a reason.

### Supplier Portal: Document and Verification Fixes

**Why this was changed**

Several support tickets related to incorrect expired document alerts, missing documents, and suppliers stuck at the wrong portal stage.

**What's different**

- **Expired document alert**: No longer shows when a supplier has no services in their price list (previously showed incorrectly).
- **Business verification documents**: Now always display, even when the supplier has no services in their price list.
- **Supplier stage after invitation**: Suppliers invited from the staff portal are now correctly set to "Invited" stage rather than remaining at a previous stage.
- **Verified status logic**: Suppliers with POLICE_CHECK or NDIS Worker Screening documents are now correctly progressed to Verified.

**Where in the platform**

`Suppliers → [Supplier Profile] → Documents tab`
`Staff → Suppliers → Invite Supplier`

**How to help users**

For any supplier reporting incorrect document alerts or unexpected portal stage, advise them that these have been fixed automatically. If the issue persists after this release, escalate for investigation.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "My supplier is stuck as 'Unverified' even though they uploaded their police check?"**
> A: This should be fixed. If it persists, check whether the document was approved. The fix applies to newly submitted or re-assessed documents.

> **Q: "Why is there an expired document warning when the supplier has no services listed?"**
> A: This was a known bug — now fixed. If it still appears, please raise it with the dev team.

### Bills: Fee Exemption Tracking

**Why this was changed**

Finance teams and compliance staff needed an audit trail for fee exemptions, including who applied them.

**What's different**

Bills now show a "Fee Exempted By" field when a loading fee has been exempted, showing the name of the staff member who applied it. New filter columns in the bills table allow filtering by exemption status.

**Where in the platform**

`Bills → Bills Table → Fee Exemption columns`

**How to help users**

For users asking about a fee exemption on a bill, direct them to the "Fee Exempted By" column in the bills table. If they can't see it, check their table column visibility settings.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Who exempted the fees on this bill?"**
> A: The "Fee Exempted By" column in the bills table now shows this. The exemption is recorded automatically when a staff member applies it.

### Team Permission Fix (Hotfix)

**Why this was changed**

Some users were experiencing incorrect permission denials or errors when assigning roles, particularly in multi-team organisations.

**What's different**

The Spatie Teams permission system has been patched. Global role permissions now correctly apply even when a team context is active. Role assignment errors (duplicate key violations) have been resolved.

**Where in the platform**

Affects all permission-gated areas of the portal.

**How to help users**

If a user reports sudden permission issues after this release, it may have been an edge case during the fix deployment. Ask them to log out and back in. If issues persist, escalate.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I suddenly can't access a section I had access to before."**
> A: Log out and back in to refresh your session. If the issue persists, raise a ticket — this fix should resolve most permission inconsistencies.

### New Permission: Compliance Staff Can Add Missing Supplier Documents

**Why this was changed**

Compliance Managers and Team Members needed to add missing documents to supplier records but previously required Admin access to do so.

**What's different**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been created and assigned to Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles. These users can now upload missing documents directly.

**Where in the platform**

`Suppliers → [Supplier Profile] → Documents tab → Add Missing Document`

**How to help users**

If a compliance user reports they can't add a document, check their assigned role. They must have one of: Admin, Compliance Manager, Compliance Team Member.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I'm a Compliance Manager but I can't see the add document button."**
> A: Ensure your role is correctly assigned as Compliance Manager or Compliance Team Member. If your role is correct and the button is still missing, raise a support ticket.
