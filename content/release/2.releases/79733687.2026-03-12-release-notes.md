- **Team permissions fixed** — A critical bug in the team-scoped permissions system has been resolved, ensuring users in teams see only what they are authorised to see and that background jobs respect the correct permission context.
- **Bill fee exemption tracking** — The bills table now shows who exempted fees on a bill, with new filter and column options so finance teams can audit and track exemptions at a glance.

### Team Permission System Overhauled

A critical bug in the permissions infrastructure has been resolved, ensuring user roles and team-level access controls work reliably across the platform.

**Why this matters**

Incorrect permission behaviour in a multi-tenancy system creates compliance and governance risk. Users were in some cases either blocked from actions they should have access to, or granted access broader than their team scope. This fix closes that gap.

**What's changing**

The underlying permissions library's team context handling has been overhauled. The system now correctly scopes permissions to the user's current team when a team is active, and defaults to the global context when no team is set. Background jobs and scheduled tasks also now initialise with the correct global scope, eliminating a class of permission-related errors that occurred in automated processing.

**Who's affected**

All users who are members of teams, and any automated billing or data-processing workflows that run in the background.

**Action required**

No action required — this fix is applied automatically. If any users previously reported unexpected "access denied" errors or noticed permissions behaving inconsistently after team switching, this release should resolve those issues.

---

### Bill Fee Exemption Tracking

The ability to track who applied a fee exemption on a bill has been introduced, with filtering and column visibility improvements for the bills table.

**Why this matters**

Fee exemptions on bills represent a financial decision that needs to be auditable. Previously, there was no way to see which staff member approved an exemption after the fact, creating a reconciliation gap.

**What's changing**

When a loading fee exemption is applied to a bill, the system now records which user made that change. The bills table has been updated with new filter options and a column to surface this information, making it possible for finance leads to filter and audit exemptions without manual investigation.

**Who's affected**

Finance team leads, billing coordinators, and anyone involved in bill approval or reconciliation.

**Action required**

No action required — this is available immediately to authorised users.

---

### Bill On-Hold and Rejection Reasons Now Visible

Suppliers and care coordinators can now see the specific reason a bill has been placed on hold or rejected, directly on the bill view.

**Why this matters**

Unclear communication around bill hold and rejection decisions creates back-and-forth between teams and delays resolution. Providing the reason inline reduces support volume and speeds up resubmission.

**What's changing**

The bill stage badge now displays a tooltip or expanded view with the on-hold or rejection reason when one has been recorded. This is visible to both the care coordinator and the supplier on their respective bill views.

**Who's affected**

Suppliers, care coordinators, and anyone who views bill details.

**Action required**

No action required — this appears automatically where a reason has been recorded.

---

### New Compliance Document Upload Permission

Compliance team members and managers can now upload missing documents to supplier profiles directly within the portal.

**Why this matters**

Previously, compliance staff were blocked from uploading documents to suppliers, requiring workarounds or admin escalation. This closes a workflow gap and keeps document management within the right team.

**What's changing**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been added and assigned to the Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles.

**Who's affected**

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members.

**Action required**

No action required — the permission has been granted automatically to the relevant roles.

---

### Organisation Branding Foundation

Verified organisations now automatically receive a unique short code (up to 4 characters) and a colour derived from their legal trading name.

**Why this matters**

A consistent visual identity for each organisation (code + colour) enables better differentiation and identification across the portal — particularly relevant for dashboards, reports, and any future multi-organisation views.

**What's changing**

The `organisations` table now stores a `code` and `colour` value. A background process populates these for all verified organisations. New organisations will have their code and colour assigned automatically upon verification.

**Who's affected**

Internal portal users and any reporting that references organisation identity. No change to existing workflows.

**Action required**

No action required — codes and colours are generated automatically.

---

### Performance and Infrastructure Improvements

Significant backend and infrastructure improvements have been made in this release that will improve portal stability, speed, and deployment reliability.

**Why this matters**

Database performance directly affects page load times and the responsiveness of the portal under load. Infrastructure improvements to the deployment pipeline reduce risk and enable faster, more reliable releases going forward.

**What's changing**

Key database queries on the hot path (user verification lookups, worker relationship queries, notification queries) have been optimised with new composite indexes, reducing load on the database significantly. The application has also been deployed to AWS ECS Fargate via a new CDK-based infrastructure pipeline, improving deployment automation and scalability.

**Who's affected**

All portal users benefit from improved performance. The infrastructure change is transparent to end users.

**Action required**

No action required.

### See Exactly Why Your Bill Is On Hold or Rejected

When a bill is placed on hold or rejected, the reason is now shown directly on the bill page — no more guessing or waiting for a follow-up.

**Why we built this**

We heard from suppliers and care coordinators that finding out *why* a bill was on hold meant contacting another team and waiting for a response. This update puts that information where it's needed most — right on the bill itself.

**What's changed**

The bill status badge at the top of the bill page now expands to show the on-hold or rejection reason and any accompanying comment when one has been provided.

**Where to find it**

`Bills → [open any on-hold or rejected bill] → Bill status badge at the top`

**How to use it**

1. Open a bill from your Bills list
2. Look at the status badge at the top of the page
3. If the bill is on hold or rejected, the reason will be displayed alongside the badge

No steps needed if your bill is in a normal state — the enhanced badge only appears when a reason has been recorded.

### Filter Bills by Fee Exemption

The bills table now includes new filter options and a column showing whether fees have been exempted — and who exempted them.

**Why we built this**

Finance users needed a quick way to identify bills where fee exemptions had been applied without having to open each bill individually.

**What's changed**

New filter and column options for fee exemption status have been added to the bills table, including who applied the exemption.

**Where to find it**

`Billing → Bills → Filter options and table columns`

**How to use it**

2. Use the new fee exemption filter to narrow the list
3. Enable the fee exemption column from the column picker if it's not already visible

### Compliance Team Can Now Upload Missing Supplier Documents

If you're in the Compliance team, you now have direct access to upload missing documents to a supplier's profile.

**Why we built this**

Compliance staff were previously unable to upload documents directly, requiring them to ask an admin every time. This was a common friction point in the compliance workflow.

**What's changed**

Compliance Managers and Compliance Team Members now have a new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission, allowing them to upload documents directly.

**Where to find it**

`Suppliers → [select a supplier] → Documents tab → Add Document`

**How to use it**

1. Navigate to a supplier's profile
2. Go to the **Documents** tab
3. Click **Add Document** or equivalent upload action

You'll see this if you have the Compliance Manager or Compliance Team Member role — chat to your admin if you think you should have access.

### Bill Line Items Can't Be Deleted If They Have Linked Payments

To prevent accidental data loss, bill line items that have been linked to transactions can no longer be deleted.

**Why we built this**

Deleting a bill item that has already been matched to a transaction creates reconciliation issues. This protection ensures that financial data remains consistent.

**What's changed**

If you attempt to delete a bill line item that has linked transactions, you'll now receive a clear error message explaining why it can't be deleted.

**Where to find it**

`Bills → [open a bill] → Line items`

**How to use it**

No steps needed — the protection works automatically. If you see an error when trying to delete a line item, it means that item has linked transactions. Contact the billing team if you need to remove it.

### Bill Stage Now Shows On-Hold and Rejection Reason

**Why this was changed**

Suppliers and care coordinators were frequently contacting support to ask why their bill was on hold or rejected, since no reason was visible on the bill itself. This change puts the reason directly on the bill page.

**What's different**

The bill status badge at the top of bill detail pages now expands to show the on-hold reason, rejection reason, and any comments when present. Previously the badge only showed the status label with no additional context.

**Where in the platform**

`Bills → [any bill] → Status badge at the top` — visible on both the CC bill view (`CareCoordinators/Bills/Show`) and the Supplier bill view (`Suppliers/Bills/Show`).

**How to help users**

If a user asks why their bill is on hold, direct them to open the bill and look at the status badge at the top of the page.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I can't see the rejection reason on my bill?"**
> A: The reason only appears if one has been recorded when the bill was put on hold or rejected. If the reason field was left blank when the action was taken, the badge will show the status only. Ask the billing team to add a comment to the bill.

> **Q: "The on-hold reason is showing as 'see logs' — what does that mean?"**
> A: This was a known issue that has been fixed in this release. For bills put on hold before this release, legacy messages may still appear. For new bills, full error details will be shown.

### Team Permissions System Fixed

**Why this was changed**

Users in teams were experiencing inconsistent permission behaviour — either being blocked from actions they should have, or seeing access that didn't match their team role. A root cause was identified in how the permissions library was initialising team context.

**What's different**

The permissions system now correctly defaults to global context (team_id = 0) when no team is active, and scopes to the user's current team when they switch teams. Background jobs also now initialise with the correct global scope. A database migration fixes the underlying primary key structure on the permissions tables.

**Where in the platform**

Permissions apply platform-wide. Any feature gated by team-level roles.

**How to help users**

If a user reports unexpectedly losing access after switching teams, or seeing access they shouldn't have, a page refresh should resolve it now. If the issue persists, check their team membership and role assignment in the admin area.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "Since switching teams I can't access [feature] anymore?"**
> A: This should be resolved in this release. Ask the user to log out and back in to refresh their session context.

> **Q: "A user seems to have access to something they shouldn't?"**
> A: Check their team membership and the permissions assigned to their role within that team in the admin area.

### New Compliance Document Upload Permission

**Why this was changed**

Compliance team members were blocked from uploading supplier documents, requiring admin escalation for routine compliance tasks.

**What's different**

A new `can-add-supplier-documents` permission has been added and automatically assigned to Admin, Compliance Manager, and Compliance Team Member roles. These users can now upload documents directly to supplier profiles.

**Where in the platform**

`Suppliers → [supplier profile] → Documents tab → Add Document`

**How to help users**

If a compliance team member reports they can't upload documents, confirm their role is Compliance Manager or Compliance Team Member. If it is and they still can't upload, check they're viewing the correct supplier and have the correct organisation context set.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I'm in the compliance team but I still can't add documents?"**
> A: Confirm their role is set to either Compliance Manager or Compliance Team Member. If so, they should now have access — ask them to try refreshing the page or logging out and back in.

### Fee Exemption Tracking on Bills

**Why this was changed**

Finance teams needed an audit trail for fee exemptions — specifically, knowing who applied the exemption and being able to filter for exempted bills.

**What's different**

When a loading fee exemption is applied to a bill, the system now records the user who made the change. The bills table has new filter and column options for fee exemption status, including who applied the exemption.

Old behaviour: Fee exemption was recorded but the exempting user was not tracked.
New behaviour: Fee exemption records the user ID. A new `loading_fees_exempted_by` column in the database stores this.

**Where in the platform**

`Billing → Bills → Filter / Column options`

**How to help users**

If a user can't see the new fee exemption column, they need to enable it via the column picker in the bills table.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "I can't see who exempted fees on older bills?"**
> A: The user tracking was added in this release. Bills where the exemption was applied before this release will not have an exempting user recorded. A one-time backfill will be run for historical records where possible.

> **Q: "Where is the fee exemption filter?"**
> A: In the Bills table, open the filter options and look for the loading fee exemption field.

### Supplier Dashboard and Verification Fixes

**Why this was changed**

Multiple supplier-facing issues were reported:
- Incorrect expired document alerts on the supplier dashboard
- Suppliers with POLICE_CHECK or NDIS Worker Screening not showing as verified
- Business verification documents not appearing when no services are in the price list

**What's different**

All three issues have been corrected. Supplier verification logic, document alert logic, and document visibility have been individually patched.

**Where in the platform**

`Suppliers → Dashboard` (for alerts) and `Suppliers → Documents` (for verification and document visibility).

**How to help users**

If a supplier reports their documents aren't showing correctly or they appear unverified despite uploading valid documents, ask them to reload the page. If the issue persists, escalate as it may require a cache clear on their supplier record.

**Likely Q&A**

> **Q: "The supplier says their documents are approved but they're still showing as unverified?"**
> A: This was a bug that has been fixed. The fix is live — ask them to refresh the page. If they used POLICE_CHECK or NDIS Worker Screening as verification, these are now correctly recognised.
