Strategic intelligence on key players in Australian home care and NDIS markets.

## Market Context

The Australian home care market is undergoing significant transformation:

- **Support at Home** replaced Home Care Packages on 1 November 2025
- Self-managed models are growing rapidly (HomeMade up 95%, Five Good Friends up 50% in FY24)
- Provider consolidation continues (855 home care providers, down 0.7% YoY)
- Not-for-profits still dominate (53.1% of home care market)
- Traditional providers charge 30-40% fees vs self-managed at 13-20%

## Competitor Overview

|---------|-------|---------|-------------|--------|

## Mable / HomeMade

### Company Structure

- **Mable**: Australia's largest care marketplace (founded 2014)
- **HomeMade**: Sister company for self-managed HCP (launched 2021)
- **Leap In!**: NDIS plan management (acquired)
- Combined: ~$839M annual revenue transacted through platform

### Business Model

**Mable (Marketplace)**
- Connects participants directly with independent support workers
- 7.95% client platform fee + 10% worker fee
- NOT a registered NDIS provider (self-managed/plan-managed only)
- 500,000+ hours of support processed monthly

**HomeMade (Self-Managed HCP)**
- Full self-management support for Home Care Packages
- Budget tracking, compliance, clinical oversight
- Entered KPMG top 25 providers at #17 in FY24
- 95% revenue growth ($42M → $83M)

### Strengths

- **Scale**: Largest platform, network effects
- **Brand**: "Mable" is becoming synonymous with self-managed care
- **Full stack**: Marketplace + management + NDIS (Leap In!)
- **Funding**: $100M from General Atlantic (valued near $1B)

### Weaknesses

- **Not registered**: Can't serve agency-managed NDIS participants
- **Worker quality variance**: Marketplace model = inconsistent quality
- **Regulatory risk**: Platform model under scrutiny
- **Acquisition rumours**: Potential distraction, uncertainty

### Strategic Threat Level: HIGH

Mable is the category leader. Their marketplace model and HomeMade's rapid growth make them the primary competitive threat.

## Five Good Friends / The Lookout Way

::callout{icon="i-heroicons-star" color="amber"}
**Featured Competitor** - The Lookout Way is our primary platform competitor. See the [dedicated Lookout Way page](/context/competitors/lookout-way) for video resources and detailed analysis.
::

### Company Structure

- **Five Good Friends**: Care delivery (founded 2016)
- **The Lookout Way**: SaaS platform (spun out 2021)
- TPG acquired majority stake in April 2025
- EQT retains minority stake

### Business Model

**Five Good Friends (Provider)**
- Top 20 home care provider nationally
- 30,000+ care visits per month
- 3 million+ hours of care delivered
- Technology-led care delivery

**The Lookout Way (SaaS)**
- Care management platform for Australian market
- 100+ care providers using the platform
- Features: rostering, family access, automated escalation, compliance
- Sold separately to other providers

### Strengths

- **Dual revenue**: Care delivery + SaaS licensing
- **Technology DNA**: Built platform from ground up
- **Growth trajectory**: 50% revenue growth to $97.5M (FY24)
- **Institutional backing**: TPG brings capital and M&A capability

### Weaknesses

- **Conflict of interest**: Selling platform to competitors they compete with
- **Scale**: Smaller than Mable
- **Focus**: Primarily aged care, less NDIS presence

### Strategic Threat Level: MEDIUM-HIGH

Five Good Friends is a well-funded, technology-forward competitor. The Lookout Way platform poses a platform-level competitive threat.

## PlanCare / CareTaskr

### Company Structure

- **PlanCare**: Self-managed HCP and NDIS (consumer-facing)
- **CareTaskr**: Provider software platform (B2B)
- Operated by CareTaskr Holdings Pty Ltd (Perth HQ)
- NDIS registered until March 2028

### Business Model

**PlanCare (Self-Managed)**
- Self-management for HCP and NDIS participants
- Consumer app for budget tracking, invoices, provider management
- Flat percentage fee (no hidden costs, daily fees)
- Offices: Perth, Melbourne, Adelaide

**CareTaskr (Provider Software)**
- Client management, rostering, invoicing
- Marketed as "Australia's fastest-growing software platform"
- Offered to PlanCare provider community

### Awards & Recognition

- 2025 Future of Ageing Award (Technology – Business)
- 2023 AFR BOSS Most Innovative Companies
- 2022 Best New Business (Australian Disability Services Awards)

### Strengths

- **Award-winning**: Strong innovation credentials
- **Dual-sided**: Consumer platform + provider software
- **NDIS + Aged Care**: Serves both markets
- **Transparent pricing**: Differentiator vs traditional providers

### Weaknesses

- **Scale**: Smaller than Mable and Five Good Friends
- **Geographic**: Perth-based, expanding
- **Funding**: No public institutional backing announced

### Strategic Threat Level: MEDIUM

PlanCare/CareTaskr is a nimble competitor with strong product credentials. Watch for growth and potential acquisition.

## Trilogy Care

### Company Structure

- Trilogy Care Pty Ltd
- National Home Care Package provider
- Note: Different from Trilogy Aged Care (residential)

### Business Model

**Self-Managed HCP Provider**
- Two tiers: Self Managed and Self Managed PLUS
- Flat percentage fee based on HCP budget
- No daily management fees, hidden costs, or surcharges
- Person-centred approach, strong compliance focus

### Services

- Home and community services
- Nursing and transition care
- Personal care and respite
- Assistive technology and home modifications

### Strengths

- **Transparent pricing**: Flat percentage, no hidden fees
- **National reach**: Services across Australia
- **Compliance**: Recent quality assessment (Oct 2024)
- **Established**: Known brand in HCP space

### Weaknesses

- **Technology**: Less platform-focused than competitors
- **Scale**: Smaller than market leaders
- **Focus**: Primarily aged care, limited NDIS

### Strategic Threat Level: LOW-MEDIUM

Trilogy Care is a solid traditional competitor but less of a platform/technology threat.

- Strong digital presence

### Pearl Home Care

- Ranked in top providers

## Strategic Opportunities

### 1. Platform Differentiation

Competitors are converging on similar models. Differentiation opportunities:
- **Clinical integration**: Deeper healthcare integration than marketplace models
- **Outcome tracking**: Beyond compliance to measurable care outcomes
- **AI/automation**: Operational efficiency through intelligent systems

### 2. Provider Software Gap

The Lookout Way sells to competitors. CareTaskr is growing. There's demand for provider-side tools that:
- Don't come from a competing care provider
- Integrate with self-management platforms
- Support Support at Home compliance

### 3. NDIS Underserved

Most competitors focus on aged care. NDIS opportunities:
- Agency-managed participants (can't use Mable)
- Complex needs coordination
- Plan management integration

### 4. Support at Home Transition

The November 2025 transition creates:
- Provider confusion (opportunity for clear positioning)
- System changes (opportunity for better UX)
- New compliance requirements (opportunity for automation)

## Competitive Threats

### 1. Mable's Network Effects

As the largest platform, Mable benefits from:
- More workers → better availability → more clients
- Brand recognition becoming category default
- Potential acquisition by major player

### 2. Institutional Capital

TPG (Five Good Friends), General Atlantic (Mable), and others are pouring capital into the sector:
- Accelerates competitor growth
- Enables aggressive pricing
- Funds marketing and expansion

### 3. Platform Consolidation

The market may consolidate around 2-3 major platforms:
- Winners will have scale, capital, and technology
- Smaller players may need to specialize or exit
- Acquisition pressure on mid-tier providers

### 4. Regulatory Changes

Support at Home and new Aged Care Act 2024 create:
- Compliance burden (favours larger players)
- Uncertainty (delays decisions)
- Opportunity for first-movers on new requirements
