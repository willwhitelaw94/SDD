## Key Predictions

| Prediction | What He Said |
|------------|--------------|
| **Vibe coding becomes invisible** | "The term will be like 'information superhighway'—something we stop saying because it's just how things work" |
| **We're asking the wrong question** | "How fast can I vibe code existing software?" is less interesting than "what new form factors will replace it entirely?" |
| **Agents replace dashboards** | "Rather than forms and fields in a browser, we will delegate tasks to agents that operate autonomously" |
| **Everyone gets their own view** | Not one dashboard for all—every person gets AI-generated insights tailored to their role |
| **Business model shift is harder than tech shift** | Moving to subscription revenue was harder than building for web. Outcomes-based pricing (pay per resolved case) is next. |
| **AI is the new front door** | "ChatGPT is already the front door to the internet for many people" |
| **AI agents are MORE reliable than humans** | Counterintuitive: agents make fewer mistakes than fallible human operations |
| **Consultants pivot to strategy** | Implementation billable hours collapse; change management and strategic advice remain valuable |
| **We're in inning two of nine** | Massive uncertainty remains—and that's exciting, not threatening |

## Topics Covered

1. **Vibe coding** — Will it last or become invisible?
2. **Maintenance vs building** — Most software cost is maintenance
3. **Agents vs dashboards** — Forms and fields are dying
4. **Incumbent dilemma** — Business model transitions harder than tech
5. **AI as front door** — ChatGPT replacing Google as starting point
6. **SEO/SEM disruption** — Marketing optimized for humans enters new world
7. **AI reliability** — Agents more reliable than fallible humans
8. **Sierra case studies** — Cigna, SiriusXM, Rocket Mortgage
9. **OpenAI ads** — Three pillars: subscriptions, API, ads
10. **Not the last invention** — Optimism about unknowable future

## Why This Matters for TC Portal

Taylor's framework maps directly to our situation:

- **"Jobs to be done"**: What job does TC Portal get hired to do? It's not "display fields in a browser"—it's enable better care outcomes.
- **Incumbent risk**: "Incumbents are seeing churn at the edges. AI-native products sit on top of existing systems, pull context across them, and create value the platform doesn't capture."
- **The question**: Will we be the AI-native layer, or will someone else build it on top of us?
