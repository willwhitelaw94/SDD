## The Big Shift

Nick Fox describes what's happening:

> "What AI means for search is probably the biggest shift in search ever. We're talking about a shift from basic information retrieval to intelligence. These models enable a much deeper understanding of information and the ability to transform information."

### Search Will Become Unrecognizable

Fox predicts search will be "unrecognizably different — and far better — in just a few years."

## AI Mode By The Numbers

- **Launch**: March 2025 announcement, May 2025 US rollout
- **Daily users**: 75 million globally (December 2025)
- **Query length**: 2-3x longer than traditional search
- **Multimodal**: Text, voice, and image input supported
- **Global expansion**: India first (mid-2025), then worldwide

---

## What Google Learned About Hallucination

Fox acknowledged early challenges:

> "Very early on if you sort of rewind to those early days, the technology hallucinated quite a bit... Certainly within the context of search, factuality matters a lot."

Reid explained their response:

> "We put a lot of effort into understanding what were the types of failures that were getting surfaced... These sort of false premise questions cropped up in new ways."

Result: Hallucination errors reduced 20-40% through comprehensive grounding systems.

## Why This Matters for TC Portal

Google's shift mirrors what we're seeing across software:

- **Conversational > Forms**: Users want to express needs naturally, not fill out fields
- **Intelligence > Retrieval**: Don't just show data—understand what it means for this user
- **Multimodal > Text-only**: Voice, images, documents all become input
- **Proactive > Reactive**: Anticipate needs before users ask

The question for us: Are we building for the world where search boxes exist, or the world where every interface is conversational?
