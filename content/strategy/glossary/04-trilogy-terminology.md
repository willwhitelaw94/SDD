Terms related to Trilogy Care's organizational structure, team roles, and internal processes.

---

## Organizational Structure

**Pod** — Cross-functional development team (e.g., Pod 1, Pod 2, IM Pod 3, Pod 4) working on specific domains and initiatives.

**Triad** — Leadership group of three senior team members providing guidance and decision-making.

**Product Triad** — Leadership trio comprising Product Manager, Design Lead, and Engineering Lead.

---

## Roles & Responsibilities

**Care Partner** — TC staff responsible for day-to-day recipient care management and budget oversight.

**Care Coordinator** — Staff member coordinating services between recipient and suppliers.

**Supplier** — Service provider delivering care services (personal care, cleaning, meal prep, etc.).

**Recipient** — Client receiving home care services under SAH program.

**Care Worker / DSP** — Disability Support Person; frontline staff delivering personal care services.

**Bill Processor** — Finance team member responsible for validating and processing supplier invoices.

**Clinical Team** — AHPRA-registered nurses and allied health practitioners providing clinical oversight.

**Assessment Partner** — Team member conducting initial needs assessment and service planning.

---

## Processes & Meetings

**BRP** — Business Review Planning
Quarterly strategic meetings reviewing performance, priorities, and planning.

**Standup** — Daily team synchronization meeting discussing progress and blockers.

**Sprint** — Two-week cycle of work with planning at start and retrospective at end.

**Retrospective** — Team meeting reflecting on what went well, what didn't, and improvements.

**Planning Session** — Meeting at start of sprint defining work to be completed.

**Review** — Presentation of completed work to stakeholders for feedback and approval.

**Retro** — Short form of retrospective.

---

## Methodology & Frameworks

**RACI** — Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed
Framework for clarifying roles and decision-making authority in projects and processes.

**Spec-Driven Development** — Philosophy of writing detailed specifications before implementation.

**Ways of Working** — Established processes and workflows for how the team executes.

**Ceremony** — Recurring meeting or ritual (standups, planning, retros).

**Definition of Done** — Shared understanding of when work is ready to ship (tests passing, code reviewed, etc.).

---

## Strategic Planning

**Epic** — Major feature initiative spanning multiple quarters and squads.

**Initiative** — Detailed feature specification derived from an epic, typically scoped to a squad and sprint.

**Feature** — Discrete user-facing capability or enhancement.

**Story** — Individual piece of work with clear acceptance criteria.

**Task** — Non-user-facing work (refactoring, tech debt, infrastructure).

**Roadmap** — Long-term plan of features and priorities by quarter/year.

**Priority** — Ranking of work items by importance and urgency.

---

## Communication & Collaboration

**Slack** — Team messaging platform for asynchronous communication.

**Teams** — Microsoft Teams platform for video calls, chat, and file sharing.

**Confluence** — Documentation wiki for project planning and specifications.

**Jira** — Issue tracking and project management system.

**GitHub** — Version control and code collaboration platform.

**RFC** — Request for Comments; formal proposal for significant decisions.

**ADR** — Architecture Decision Record; documentation of important technical decisions.

**Incident** — Unexpected production issue requiring immediate response.

**Postmortem** — Analysis of incident identifying root cause and preventing recurrence.

---

## Performance & Goals

**OKR** — Objectives and Key Results
Goal-setting framework defining what we want to achieve and how we'll measure success.

**KPI** — Key Performance Indicator
Metric measuring progress toward organizational goals.

**SLA** — Service Level Agreement
Commitment to specific performance levels (e.g., response time, uptime).

**Metric** — Quantifiable measurement of performance or user behavior.

**Baseline** — Starting point measurement for tracking progress.

**Target** — Desired end state or goal for a metric.

---

## Company & Culture

**Values** — Core principles guiding decision-making and behavior.

**Mission** — Organization's purpose and why it exists.

**Vision** — Long-term aspiration for what the organization wants to become.

**Culture** — Shared beliefs, values, and ways of working.

**Inclusion** — Creating environment where all team members feel valued and heard.

**Psychological Safety** — Team environment where people feel safe taking risks and being vulnerable.

---

## Financial & Business

**Revenue** — Income earned through:
- Care coordination fees
- Administrative fees
- Commission-based service delivery

**Cost** — Expenses incurred in operations (staff, infrastructure, etc.).

**Break Even** — Cost recovery threshold; revenue equals operating costs.

**Margin** — Profit remaining after costs (revenue minus cost).

**P&L** — Profit and Loss statement showing financial performance.

**Burn Rate** — Speed at which company spends money (typically for startups).

**ARR** — Annual Recurring Revenue; predictable yearly revenue.

---

## Learning & Development

**Onboarding** — Process of integrating new team members.

**Training** — Structured learning program for skills development.

**Mentorship** — One-on-one guidance from experienced team member.

**Career Path** — Progression opportunities within organization.

**Growth** — Professional development and skill improvement.

**Feedback** — Information about performance and areas for improvement.

**1:1** — One-on-one meeting between manager and team member.

---

## Recognition & Ceremonies

**All Hands** — Company-wide meeting addressing strategy, updates, and recognition.

**Team Lunch** — Team building activity and social gathering.

**Celebration** — Recognition of milestones and achievements.

**Award** — Recognition program honoring exceptional contributions.

**Shoutout** — Public recognition of someone's work or contribution.
