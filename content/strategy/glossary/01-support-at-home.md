Key terms related to the Australian Support at Home (SAH) program and aged care services.

---

## Government Programs & Funding

**SAH** — Support at Home
The Australian government's aged care program launched in 2025, replacing HCP and CHSP. Provides subsidized home and community care services for older Australians.

**HCP** — Home Care Packages
Predecessor program to SAH. Means-tested subsidies for older people needing home care.

**CHSP** — Commonwealth Home Support Programme
Predecessor program to SAH. Subsidized low-level care assistance (cleaning, shopping, social support).

**IPA** — Individual Payment Agreement
The funding entitlement issued by Services Australia specifying approved services and dollar amounts for a recipient.

**NOA** — Notice of Assessment
Government-issued document confirming approved services, funding amounts, and eligibility for a recipient.

**PRODA** — Processor of Digital Accounts
Services Australia's authentication system for accessing government portals and APIs.

## Service Groups & Categories

**SERG** — Service Group
Service categories defined by SAH program. Trilogy Care tracks utilization across these groups:
- **SERG-0001** — Home Support (personal care, domestic assistance)
- **SERG-0002** — Assistive Technology
- **SERG-0003** — Home Modifications
- **SERG-0004** — Care Management
- **SERG-0005** — End of Life Pathway
- **SERG-0006** — Restorative Care Pathway

---

## Care Package Types

**Self-Managed Package** — The recipient or their representative directly engages suppliers and manages their own services. Trilogy Care provides support and tracking.

**Fully Coordinated Package** — Trilogy Care coordinates and manages services on behalf of the recipient. Attracts a Care Coordination fee (typically 20%).

**SM+** (Self-Managed Plus) — Enhanced self-managed model where Trilogy Care provides coordination support while recipient retains management control.

**Consumer Directed Care** — The underlying philosophy giving recipients maximum choice and control over their services.

**Dignity of Risk** — The principle that clients have the right to make informed decisions about their care, even if those decisions involve some level of risk.

## Budget & Funding

**Service Plan** — Document specifying:
- Services approved for the recipient
- Allocation amount for each service category
- Duration and frequency of services
- Assigned suppliers

**Funding Stream** — Source classification for budget allocation:
- **ON** — Ongoing (primary quarterly funding from SAH)
- **RC** — Restorative Care Pathway
- **EL** — End of Life Pathway
- **CU** — Commonwealth Unspent (rolled-over funds from previous periods)
- **HC** — Home Care Account (accumulated surpluses)
- **VC** — Voluntary Contribution (client top-ups beyond government funding)

**Quarterly Budget** — Funding allocation reset each calendar quarter.

**Unspent Funds** — Budget allocated but not utilized within the period.

**Rollover** — Unspent funds automatically carried forward to the next period (default 60 days).

**Wraparound Service** — Services delivered to a recipient using rolled-over or surplus funds beyond the initial service plan period.

## Fees & Charges

**CC Fee** — Care Coordination Fee
Charged on fully coordinated packages (typically 20%) to cover coordination costs.

**TC Fee** — Trilogy Care Fee
Administrative overhead charged to offset operational costs (typically 10%).

**Care Management Fee** — Allowable provider fee (up to 10% per Services Australia rules) for care coordination activities.

**Contribution (VC)** — Voluntary client top-up payment supplementing government funding for additional services.

**ITF Collection** — Incoming Trust Fund collection via direct debit from recipient bank account.

---

## Claims & Billing

**Claim** — Formal request submitted to Services Australia to draw down approved funding for services delivered.

**Bill** — Invoice from a supplier requesting payment for services delivered to a recipient.

**Bill Matching** — Process of matching invoice line items to approved service plan items.

**Planned Service** — Services included in the active service plan with allocated budget.

**Unplanned Service** — Service approved in NOA but not included in the current service plan.

**Out-of-Budget Service** — Planned service where actual utilization exceeds budgeted amount.

**Invoice Line Item** — Individual service entry on a supplier invoice (e.g., "Personal care - 2 hours @ $50/hr").

**Bill Processor** — Finance team member responsible for validating and processing supplier invoices.

**Payment Reconciliation** — Matching payments received from Services Australia to claims submitted and services delivered.

**RCTI** — Revenue Collection Tax Invoice
Formal invoice from coordinator to recipient for coordination fees and contributions.

## Regulatory & Compliance

**Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission** — Australian government regulator overseeing aged care quality and compliance.

**Services Australia** — Government agency administering SAH program, processing claims, and issuing funding.

**Price List** — Required transparency documentation showing service pricing and fee structures.

**Charter of Aged Care Rights** — Government document outlining recipient rights, protections, and obligations.

**AHPRA** — Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency
National regulator of doctors, nurses, and allied health practitioners. Relevant for clinical oversight in end-of-life and restorative care pathways.

**SIRS** — Serious Incident Response Scheme
Mandatory incident reporting framework for aged care providers.

---

## Care & Assessment

**Care Plan** — Document outlining:
- Recipient's assessed needs
- Goals and preferences
- Agreed services and approach
- Risk mitigation strategies

**Utilisation Rate** — Percentage of allocated budget actually spent (e.g., "75% utilization" means 75% of budget was used).

**Unmet Need** — Gap between assessed needs and available/approved services.

**Clinical Risk** — Health-related concern requiring professional nursing or allied health oversight.

**Assessment Partner** — Team member conducting initial needs assessment and service planning.
