Registered providers must meet conditions of registration, obligations and duties under the Aged Care Act 2024.

---

## Overview

The Act establishes a framework linking service delivery to the rights of individuals. Providers must:
- Be registered before delivering funded aged care services
- Meet conditions of registration
- Comply with obligations and duties
- Ensure responsible persons and workers comply with the Code of Conduct

---

## Code of Conduct

### Who It Applies To
- Registered providers
- Responsible persons
- Aged care workers (including volunteers)

### Purpose
The Code:
- Supports person's right to personal choice, dignity and respect
- Promotes kind, honest and respectful behaviour
- Keeps people receiving care safe from harm

### Enforcement
- Commission monitors and enforces compliance
- Non-compliance may result in enforcement action

### Reference
- Section 14 of the Act
- Section 14-1, Chapter 1, Part 5 of the Rules

---

## Serious Incident Response Scheme (SIRS)

### Purpose
Reduce abuse and neglect of older people receiving funded aged care services.

### Provider Responsibilities

| Responsibility | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Prevent** | Prevent incidents focusing on safety and wellbeing |
| **Manage** | Use incident data to drive quality improvement |
| **Report** | Report serious incidents to the Commission |

### Reporting
- Use My Aged Care Service and Support Portal to notify Commission
- Comply with sections 164 and 166 of the Act

### Contact
- Phone: 1800 081 549
- Email: sirs@agedcarequality.gov.au

## Worker Screening

### Requirements (Section 152)

Providers must:
- Comply with worker screening requirements in the Rules
- Ensure aged care workers and responsible persons comply

### Current Requirements

All aged care workers and responsible persons must have:

| Option | Requirements |
|--------|--------------|
| Police certificate | Not older than 3 years, no disqualifying convictions |
| NDIS check | For workers also in NDIS sector |

### Future Screening
- Government working with states/territories to expand NDIS checks to aged care
- Not expected before 2026
- Transitional process will be communicated

## Registration Requirements

### Provider Registration
- Overseen by the Commission
- Must register into one or more registration categories
- Categories group services of similar complexity and risk

### Conditions of Registration
All providers must demonstrate:
- Understanding of Statement of Rights
- Practices in place to act compatibly with these rights

### Registration Categories for Support at Home

| Category | Description | Required For |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Category 4 | Personal care and care support in home/community | All Support at Home providers |
| Category 5 | Nursing and transition care | Providers delivering nursing services |

## Financial Reporting

### Quarterly Financial Report (QFR)
- Introduced July 2022
- Completed via Government Provider Management System (GPMS)
- Improves financial transparency and prudential compliance

### Aged Care Financial Report (ACFR)
- Collects financial information for providers and parent entities
- Completed via Forms Administration Portal

---

## Starting & Ceasing Services

### Starting Services
- Must comply with Rules requirements
- Submit start notification to Services Australia within 56 days
- Have service agreement in place

### Ceasing Services
- Must comply with Rules requirements
- Ensure appropriate continuity of care arrangements
- Provide required notice to participants

---

## Information Provision

### Before/When Commencing Services
Providers must provide and explain:
- Statement of Rights
- Complaints and feedback process
- Code of Conduct
- Protection of personal information
- Contributions and means testing
- Circumstances for ceasing services
- Care plan and monthly statement requirements
- Provider financial position (on request)

### While Delivering Services
Providers must provide:
- Information to help choose best services
- Clear and understandable invoices
- Monthly statements
- Individualised budgets
- Provider financial position (on request, within 7 days)

## Non-Compliance Consequences

If provider fails to meet conditions or obligations:
- Commissioner can undertake regulatory action
- May include suspending or revoking registration
- Civil penalties may apply
