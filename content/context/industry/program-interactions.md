# Program Interactions

Support at Home participants may simultaneously access services through other aged care programs and schemes. This domain covers the rules and interactions between programs.

## CHSP (Commonwealth Home Support Program)

### When Support at Home Participants Can Access CHSP

| Circumstance | Description | Time-Limited |
|--------------|-------------|--------------|
| Pre-existing social support group | Continue attending CHSP social groups after transitioning from CHSP | No |
| Hoarding and Squalor | Specialist H&S services for at-risk participants | Yes (~2 months) |
| Respite services | Community, centre-based, flexible, or cottage respite | No |
| Emergency access | Urgent health/safety needs when budget exhausted or awaiting allocation | Yes (~2 months) |
| Vision advisory services | Specialised support services | No |

### CHSP Rules
- Participant must be assessed via My Aged Care
- CHSP provider must report services in DEX
- Regular progress reviews required
- Client contribution fees apply (cannot use Support at Home budget)

## Transition Care Programme (TCP)

- **Purpose**: Time-limited, goal-oriented therapy after hospital stay
- **Eligibility**: Support at Home participants after hospital stay, if assessed and approved
- **Concurrent Access**: Support at Home services can continue if not duplicative
- **Example**: Continue gardening under Support at Home while receiving TCP therapy
- **Provider Responsibility**: Coordinate with TCP provider to ensure needs met
- **Care Management**: Can claim for coordination/consultation with TCP stakeholders
- **Restriction**: Cannot access Restorative Care Pathway while receiving TCP

## Residential Aged Care

### Temporary Respite
- Support at Home services can continue if not duplicative
- Participant must notify provider of intention to access respite
- Providers coordinate with residential care home
- Care management can be claimed for coordination activities

### Permanent Entry
- Support at Home cannot be accessed simultaneously (except entry/exit day)
- Entry/exit day: Services can continue to support transition
- Provider must discuss and agree cessation date with participant
- For unforeseen moves, providers support appropriate cessation

## Multi-Purpose Service (MPS) Program

- **What It Is**: Integrated health and aged care in rural/remote areas
- **Funding**: Block-funded (different from Support at Home)
- **Concurrent Access**: Allowed if services not accessed same day
- **Example**: Gardening and meals via Support at Home, nursing care via MPS

## Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Services

### NATSIFAC Program
- Culturally safe aged care close to home and community
- Block-funded (no individual budgets)
- Concurrent access allowed if services are different
- **Example**: Gardening via Support at Home, personal care via NATSIFAC

### Elder Care Support (ECS)
- Face-to-face support for navigating aged care
- Delivered by NACCHO partner organisations
- Helps Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people access services

## DSOA (Disability Support for Older Australians)

- **Program Status**: Closed to new clients
- **If Found Eligible for Support at Home**: DSOA funding capped
- **If Commencing Support at Home**: Required to exit DSOA
- **Grandfathered**: Clients with HCP before 1 July 2021 can continue (funding capped)

## Key Principles

1. **No Duplication** - Services must not duplicate across programs
2. **Coordination Required** - Providers must coordinate with other program providers
3. **Notification Responsibility** - Participant must notify provider of other program access
4. **Assessment Required** - Most concurrent access requires My Aged Care assessment
5. **Care Management Claimable** - Coordination activities can be claimed under care management
