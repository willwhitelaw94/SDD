---
title: "Support Plan Reviews"
description: "When and how to request Support Plan Reviews under Support at Home"
---

A Support Plan Review is a mechanism to update a participant's support plan when their needs, goals, or circumstances change.

---

## Who Can Request

- **Participant** - via My Aged Care or Services Australia ACSO
- **Provider** - via My Aged Care Service and Support Portal (with participant consent)

---

## When to Request

### Triggers for Review

Request a Support Plan Review when:

| Trigger | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Needs, goals or circumstances change | Health deterioration, change in living situation |
| Additional services needed | Need for allied health, nursing care |
| Short-term pathway access required | Restorative Care, End-of-Life, AT-HM funding |
| Time-limited service has ended | Restorative care episode completed |
| Higher classification may be needed | Current budget insufficient for care needs |

---

## Possible Outcomes

A Support Plan Review may lead to:

1. **No changes** to the support plan
2. **Updates** to the support plan
3. **New assessment** required (e.g., for Restorative Care Pathway)

---

## Provider Requirements

When requesting a Support Plan Review, providers must attach:
- Supporting documentation about current care arrangements
- Quarterly budget information
- Care plan

### For AT Repairs & Maintenance

Additional requirements:
- Specific item(s), service types, and sub-categories
- Condition details of each item
- Repair/maintenance quote for each item
- Declaration statement

---

## When at Highest Classification

If a participant already receives the highest Support at Home classification but needs additional services, consider:

1. **Review care plan** - identify alternatives and priorities (e.g., reduce weekend services, use informal supports)
2. **Explore informal supports** - family, friends, community, volunteers
3. **Short-term supports** - access HCP Commonwealth unspent funds
4. **Self-funding** - participant purchases additional services from own funds
5. **Other pathways** - CHSP respite, residential respite as short-term complement
6. **Alternative accommodation** - serviced apartment in retirement community
7. **Residential care** - if unable to sustain independent living

---

## Reassessment vs Review

| Support Plan Review | Full Reassessment |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Updates existing support plan | Creates new support plan |
| No new classification required | May result in new classification |
| Provider can request | Required for some pathways |
| Faster process | More comprehensive |

**Note**: Access to the Restorative Care Pathway requires a new assessment, not just a review.
