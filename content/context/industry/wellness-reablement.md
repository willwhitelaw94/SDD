Wellness and reablement are foundational approaches that must underpin all Support at Home service delivery.

---

## Overview

Wellness and reablement are **person-centred, holistic approaches** that:
- Build on people's strengths, capacity and goals
- Promote greater independence and autonomy
- Support older people to remain living at home, connected to community

---

## Wellness Approach

### Philosophy

Wellness informs **how** providers work with participants:
- "Doing with" not "doing for"
- Acknowledging participant abilities
- Building on strengths and skills
- Empowering informed decision-making

### Focus Areas

- Listen to what the participant **wants** to do
- Look at what they **can** do
- Focus on regaining or retaining function
- Enable continued management of day-to-day life

---

## Reablement Approach

### Characteristics

- **Time-limited** interventions
- Focus on achieving **specific goals** or outcomes
- Maintaining or regaining skills
- Improving functional capacity
- Enhancing confidence and capability
- Resuming everyday activities

### Where Applied

- Should be identified as part of ongoing support
- Underpins the **Restorative Care Pathway**
- Aims to keep people as independent as possible

---

## Legislative Basis

### Aged Care Act 2024

Providers must deliver services that:
- Prioritise mental health and wellbeing (s.20(c)(i))
- Use reablement approaches to support improvement of wellbeing, independence, autonomy, physical and cognitive capacity (s.20(c)(v))

### Strengthened Quality Standards

- **Standard 3**: Deliver care that optimises quality of life, reablement and maintenance of function
- **Outcome 3.2**: Care meets needs, goals and preferences while optimising reablement
- **Outcome 5.4**: Clinical care encompasses assessment, prevention, planning, treatment, management and review

---

## Provider Responsibilities

Providers must demonstrate capability and commitment to:

### Service Delivery
- Focus on working **with** participants to achieve goals
- Apply "doing with" approach
- Offer time-limited interventions where appropriate
- Regularly review and monitor changes to needs and goals

### Documentation
- Have an **implementation plan** outlining wellness and reablement approach
- Document in care plans

---

## Key Components

| Component | Provider Action |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Promote independence** | Actively support participant independence and community connection |
| **Identify goals** | Work with participant to plan and achieve their goals |
| **Consider physical & psychosocial** | Address physical, social and emotional wellbeing |
| **Encourage active participation** | Focus on working with, not taking over |
| **Focus on strengths** | Emphasise what participant can do, avoid creating dependencies |
| **Support capabilities** | Help participants maintain and extend activities |
| **Individualised support** | Tailor delivery to goals, capabilities and needs |
| **Regular review** | Reviews at least annually, care management monthly |

---

## Avoiding "Doing For"

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| Completing tasks for participant | Supporting participant to complete tasks themselves |
| Assuming limitations | Assessing actual capabilities |
| Fixed service patterns | Adjusting support as independence improves |
| Passive service delivery | Active engagement in goal achievement |
