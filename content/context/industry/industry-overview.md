## Provider Landscape

### Market Consolidation

The aged care sector continues to consolidate. Provider numbers are shrinking as regulatory complexity and financial pressures push smaller operators to exit or merge.

**Provider breakdown (Home Care):**
- Not-for-profit: 454 providers (53%)
- For-profit: 308 providers (36%)
- Government (state/local): 93 providers (11%)

### Top Providers by Segment

**Home Care (by HCP funding):**
1. **Australian Unity** - Largest after MyHomecare Group acquisition (March 2024)
2. Additional top 25 providers detailed in KPMG report

**Residential Care (by market share):**
1. **Opal** - Largest in Australia, growth via acquisitions and new openings
2. Bolton Clarke, Estia, Arcare, Aegis, Anglicare (Sydney), Mercy Care, Hall & Prior, Thompson Health Care - all grew in FY24

### Consolidation Drivers

- Increasing regulatory requirements
- AN-ACC funding model complexity
- Workforce challenges
- Financial viability concerns for smaller providers
- Desire to acquire new capabilities and services

## Funding & Financial Performance

### Government Investment

| Investment Area | Amount | Timeframe |
|-----------------|--------|-----------|
| Aged care workforce wages | $2.5 billion | 5 years |
| Aged care reforms | $291.6 million | 5 years |
| HCP funding increase | +29.2% | FY24 |

### Financial Recovery

The sector has seen significant financial improvement:

- **65.9% of residential providers now profitable** (up from previous years)
- Driven by:
  - Market consolidation (economies of scale)
  - AN-ACC funding model introduction
  - Increased government funding

### Revenue Drivers

1. **AN-ACC (Australian National Aged Care Classification)** - New residential funding model
2. **HCP growth** - Demand continues to outstrip supply
3. **Support at Home** - Major reform launching July 2025

## Demand Trends

### Supply vs Demand Gap

Demand continues to outstrip supply across the sector:

| Segment | Demand Growth | Supply Response |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Home Care | +6.6% recipients | -0.7% providers |
| Residential | +2.6% residents | -3.0% providers |

### Implications

- Larger providers absorbing market share
- Opportunity for efficient, scaled operators
- Technology and automation becoming critical differentiators
- Care management platforms essential for scale

## Major Reforms

### Support at Home (Launching July 1, 2025)

The largest reform to Australian aged care in decades:

**Replaces:**
- Home Care Packages (HCP)
- Short-Term Restorative Care (STRC)
- Commonwealth Home Support Programme (CHSP) - later phase

**Key Changes:**
- Simplified funding model
- Improved self-management options
- New provider registration requirements
- Services Australia integration
- Quarterly budget allocations

**See:** [Support at Home Context](/context/industry/sah/support-at-home-context/) for detailed regulatory requirements.

### Provider Registration Model

New flexible registration requirements expected to:
- Enable more providers to enter the market
- Support self-management options for recipients
- Increase competition in service delivery

## Future Outlook

### KPMG Predictions

1. **Continued consolidation** - Regulatory and financial pressures will drive further M&A activity

2. **Provider entry** - New registration model may attract new home care providers seeking flexible, self-management focused service delivery

3. **Capability acquisition** - Larger providers acquiring for:
   - Technology capabilities
   - New service offerings
   - Geographic expansion
   - Workforce capacity

4. **Technology investment** - Platforms that can:
   - Manage regulatory complexity
   - Scale operations efficiently
   - Improve care coordination
   - Enable data-driven decisions

### Risk Factors

- Workforce shortages persist
- Regulatory compliance costs increasing
- Transition risks for SAH go-live
- Pricing pressure from government caps
