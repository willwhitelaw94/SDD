---
title: "Registered Supporters"
description: "Legal framework for registered supporters under the Aged Care Act 2024"
---

Registered supporters help older people make and communicate their own decisions about aged care services.

---

## Key Principle

> Registered supporters **do not** have decision-making authority. Their role is to **support** the older person to make their own decisions.

Every older person is presumed to have the ability to make decisions.

---

## What Registered Supporters Can Do

- Help older person understand aged care options
- Support attendance at assessments and reviews
- Access information about the older person's care
- Communicate with providers on behalf of (not instead of) the older person
- Request and receive information about the person they support

---

## What Registered Supporters Cannot Do

- Make decisions for the older person (unless also an appointed decision-maker)
- Override the older person's choices
- Exclude the older person from their own decision-making

---

## Registration Process

A person can request to register a supporter by:
- Contacting My Aged Care
- Contacting an aged care assessor
- Contacting an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO)
- Online registration form
- Printed copy
- My Aged Care Online Account

### Consent Requirements

- Older person must consent to registering a supporter
- **Exception**: Appointed decision-makers with active legal authority don't need separate consent

---

## Appointed Decision-Makers

Some registered supporters also hold legal authority:

| Authority Type | Scope |
|----------------|-------|
| Guardianship | Under Commonwealth/state/territory law |
| Court/tribunal appointment | Decision-making power granted |
| Enduring Power of Attorney | Granted by the older person |

Appointed decision-makers can make decisions **on behalf of** the older person within their legal authority.

**Note**: Being an appointed decision-maker doesn't require registration as a supporter, but registration helps recognition across the aged care system.

---

## Provider Obligations

Providers must:
- Continue engaging **directly with the older person**
- Recognise who registered supporters are
- Understand the supporter's role (support vs decision-making)
- Respect that capacity may fluctuate day-to-day
- Include supporters in discussions **when requested by the participant**

---

## Multiple Supporters

- An older person can have **more than one** registered supporter
- Different supporters may assist with different aspects of care

---

## Changing Capacity

- Older person's ability to make decisions may change day-to-day or over time
- Providers must understand this fluctuation
- Having a registered supporter doesn't prevent the older person from acting independently

---

## Transition Arrangements

Prior to 1 November 2025:
- Regular and authorised representatives in My Aged Care became registered supporters automatically
- Ensures continuity of decision-making support

---

## No Supporter Required

- Not every older person wants or needs a registered supporter
- Some feel adequately supported by carers without formal registration
- Absence of a registered supporter is **no grounds for exclusion** from services
