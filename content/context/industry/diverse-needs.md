Support at Home is designed as a person-centred system that treats older people as unique individuals, valuing and supporting their identity, culture, spirituality and diversity.

---

## Legislative Basis

Under the Aged Care Act 2024, older people have a right to:
- Quality and safe aged care services
- Individual identity, culture, spirituality and diversity valued and supported

The aged care system must offer accessible, **culturally safe**, **culturally appropriate**, **trauma-aware** and **healing-informed** services.

---

## Diverse Populations

Services must accommodate individuals who are:

### Cultural & Linguistic Diversity
- Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander peoples (including Stolen Generations)
- Culturally, ethnically and linguistically diverse backgrounds

### Identity & Orientation
- LGBTIQ+ (lesbian, gay, bisexual, trans/transgender, intersex, other sexual orientations, gender diverse, bodily diverse)

### Life Experience
- Veterans or war widows
- Parents and children separated by forced adoption
- Adult survivors of institutional child sexual abuse
- Care-leavers (Forgotten Australians, former child migrants)

### Circumstance
- Financially or socially disadvantaged
- Experiencing homelessness or at risk of homelessness
- Living with disability or mental ill-health
- Neurodivergent, deaf, deafblind, vision impaired, hard of hearing
- Living in rural, remote or very remote areas

---

## Provider Requirements

### Strengthened Quality Standards

Providers must demonstrate they:
- Understand and value individuals' identity, culture, ability, diversity, beliefs and life experiences
- Recognise and embrace each person's diversity holistically
- Let diversity drive how they engage and deliver services

### Service Delivery

Providers must deliver services:
- **With** and **tailored to** individuals
- Taking into account needs, goals and preferences
- Free from all forms of discrimination, abuse and neglect
- Treating individuals with dignity and respect
- Respecting personal privacy

## Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander Supports

### Age Eligibility
- **50+ years** (vs 65+ for general population)

### Dedicated Programs

| Program | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Elder Care Support** | Assists with navigation, assessment, provider selection |
| **Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander Assessment Organisations** | Culturally safe assessments |
| **NATSIFAC Program** | Culturally appropriate services close to home/community |

### Assessment Preference
During screening, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people can indicate preference for assessment by an Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander assessment organisation.

### Care Management Supplement
Providers receive additional 12 hours/year of care management funding for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander participants.

---

## Culturally & Linguistically Diverse (CALD)

### Provider Actions
- Use interpreters
- Culturally appropriate communication
- Care planning that respects family and cultural values
- Engage with culturally specific organisations
- Incorporate cultural protocols and preferences

### Resources
- **Partners in Culturally Appropriate Care (PICAC)**: [mac.org.au/picac/](https://mac.org.au/picac/)

---

## Rural, Remote & Very Remote

### Challenges
- Limited provider availability
- Workforce shortages
- Travel distances

### Programs
- Multi-Purpose Services (MPS) Program - integrated health and aged care
- Service Development Assistance Panel support

---

## Vulnerable Populations

### Homelessness
- Eligible from **50+ years**
- Care management supplement available
- Care finder program support

### Care-Leavers
- Forgotten Australians
- Former child migrants
- Care management supplement available

### Trauma-Informed Care
Services must be:
- Trauma-aware
- Healing-informed
- Recognise impact of past experiences
