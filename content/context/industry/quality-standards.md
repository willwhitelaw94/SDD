The strengthened Quality Standards explain what safe, quality care should look like and support providers to deliver the funded aged care services that older people need and expect.

---

## Overview

- Established under **Section 15** of the Aged Care Act 2024
- Developed as a suite of **interrelated standards**
- **Standard 1** underpins all others (person-centred care)
- Providers must meet standards that apply to their registration category

## Standard 1: The Individual

**Purpose**: Requires providers and aged care workers to deliver person-centred care.

### Key Outcomes
- **Outcome 1.1**: Understand and value individuals
- **Outcome 1.2**: Support autonomy and independence
- **Outcome 1.3**: Respect dignity and rights
- **Outcome 1.4**: Support informed decision-making

### Relevance to Support at Home
- Underpins all care management activities
- Requires supporting dignity of risk
- Enables participant choice and control

---

## Standard 2: The Organisation

**Purpose**: Organisational governance and accountability.

### Key Outcomes
- Effective governance systems
- Risk management
- Continuous improvement
- Information management

---

## Standard 3: Care and Services

**Purpose**: Care that meets needs, goals and preferences while optimising quality of life.

### Key Outcomes
- **Outcome 3.2**: Care optimises reablement and maintenance of function
- Assessment and planning
- Delivery of services
- Review and evaluation

### Relevance to Support at Home
- Care planning requirements
- Wellness and reablement approaches
- Regular review obligations

---

## Standard 4: The Environment

**Purpose**: Safe, clean, well-maintained environment.

### Key Outcomes
- Safe environment
- Comfortable environment
- Enabling environment

### Relevance to Support at Home
- Home safety assessments
- Home modifications
- Assistive technology

---

## Standard 5: Clinical Care

**Purpose**: Safe, effective clinical care that minimises harm.

### Key Outcomes
- **Outcome 5.1**: Clinical governance
- Clinical assessment and planning
- Medication management
- **Outcome 5.4**: Clinical care encompasses assessment, prevention, planning, treatment, management and review
- **Outcome 5.7**: Specific clinical care requirements

### Relevance to Support at Home
- Care management must meet Outcome 5.1
- Allied health and nursing services
- Clinical risk management

---

## Standard 6: Food and Nutrition

**Purpose**: Nutritious, safe food and fluids.

### Key Outcomes
- Food that meets needs and preferences
- Safe food handling
- Mealtime experience

### Relevance to Support at Home
- Meal services
- Nutritional support

---

## Standard 7: The Workforce

**Purpose**: Sufficient, skilled workforce.

### Key Outcomes
- Workforce planning
- Recruitment and screening
- Training and supervision
- Workplace culture

### Relevance to Support at Home
- Care partner qualifications
- Worker screening requirements
- Training obligations

---

## Application to Registration Categories

| Category | Standards That Apply |
|----------|---------------------|
| Category 4 (Personal care) | Standards applicable to services delivered |
| Category 5 (Nursing) | Standards applicable to services delivered |
| Category 6 | Standards applicable to services delivered |

Standards only apply to services in those registration categories, not other services a provider may deliver.

---

## Monitoring & Compliance

The **Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission**:
- Monitors and regulates providers
- Audits compliance with all provider obligations
- Can take enforcement action for non-compliance
