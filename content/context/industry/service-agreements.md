---
title: "Service Agreements"
description: "Regulatory requirements for Support at Home service agreements"
---

A service agreement is the legal contract between provider and participant that must be in place before services can commence.

---

## Overview

It is a **condition of registration** that providers have a service agreement in place for each participant. The agreement outlines rights, responsibilities, services to be provided, and prices charged.

---

## Required Contents

### Parties & Dates
- Name, address, contact details of provider
- Name, address, contact details of participant
- Date agreement entered into / start date
- Participant start date (when services commence)
- Service agreement review date
- For short-term pathways: end date

### Documents to Include
- Copy of participant's **Notice of Decision**
- Copy of participant's **Support Plan**

### Services & Pricing
For each service:
- Service name (as per aged care service list)
- Service group and type
- Whether delivered by third party
- Price charged to participant

If price differs from published prices, must note:
- That the price is higher
- Reason why it's higher

For regular price increases, include clause with:
- Date of increase
- Amount and method of increase
- Reason for increase

### Other Requirements
- How participant will be involved in care decisions
- How/when agreement may be terminated
- Statement that participant agrees to pay applicable contributions
- Cooling-off period information

---

## Cooling-Off Period

Participants may withdraw from a service agreement:
- Within **14 days** of entering the agreement
- **Before** services commence
- By notifying provider verbally or in writing

---

## Accessibility Requirements

Service agreements must be:
- Readily accessible
- Written in **plain English**
- Avoid legal jargon, unusual words, idioms
- Not include terms treating participant less favourably than Australian law allows

---

## Variations

### When Variation Required
- Participant has a change in classification
- Participant enters a short-term pathway
- Participant approved for new services via Support Plan Review

### Variation Rules
- Must be by **mutual consent**
- Requires adequate consultation
- Participant must provide consent

**Exception**: GST changes can be varied without mutual consent (with reasonable written notice)

### If Participant Doesn't Agree
Provider must:
1. Negotiate to reach agreement
2. Provide detailed rationale in understandable format
3. Encourage participant to seek independent advice (advocates, registered supporters, family, legal advisers)

---

## Annual Review

Service agreements must be reviewed:
- Every **12 months**
- When requested by the participant

Providers must give participants opportunity to:
- Participate in reviews
- Consider whether updates are needed
- Vary the agreement if necessary

---

## Who Can Sign

### Participant Directly
Most participants sign for themselves.

### Appointed Decision-Maker
Can sign on behalf of participant if they have:
- Guardianship under Commonwealth/state/territory law
- Court/tribunal appointment with decision-making power
- Enduring Power of Attorney (active)

If participant cannot sign, providers should keep detailed records:
- Copy of service agreement offered
- File note of discussion (including date)

---

## Unfair Contract Terms

Providers have obligations under:
- Competition and Consumer Act 2010
- ACCC guidelines for home care services

Standard form contracts must not contain unfair terms, especially where participants have little opportunity to negotiate.

---

## Prohibited Charges

Providers **cannot charge** for:
- Entry to the service
- Exit from the service
