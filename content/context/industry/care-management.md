Care management is mandatory for all Support at Home participants and is funded through a pooled model.

---

## Overview

Care management is a set of activities that contribute to the overall safety, wellbeing, health and quality of life of participants. It ensures delivery of funded aged care services reflects individual needs, preferences and culturally appropriate practices.

---

## Funding Model

### 10% Deduction

For participants with **ongoing classifications**:
- **10% is deducted** from their quarterly budget for care management
- This funding is **pooled** at the service delivery branch level
- Providers claim against the pooled care management account

### Pooled Funding Benefits

The pooled model allows providers to:
- Flex care management up/down based on changing participant needs
- Respond promptly to emerging needs requiring urgent support
- Allocate more time to complex participants without penalising simpler cases

### Care Management Supplement

Additional 12 hours/year ($120/hr) for participants who are:
- Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander
- Homeless or at risk of homelessness
- Care leavers (Forgotten Australians, former child migrants)
- Veterans approved for the Veteran's Supplement
- Referred from the care finder program

---

## Care Partners

Care management activities are delivered by staff known as **care partners**.

### Role Requirements

Care partners must:
- Deliver relationship-based, person-centred, coordinated care
- Monitor and respond to changes in health, circumstances and wellbeing
- Promote independence through wellness and reablement approaches
- Support navigation of aged care systems and services
- Ensure culturally safe service delivery
- Document care accurately

### Qualifications

No mandatory qualifications, but valuable credentials include:
- Certificate III/IV in Individual Support, Aged Care, Disability, Community Services
- Diploma of Nursing, Community Services, Ageing Studies
- Bachelor degrees in Social Work, Physiotherapy, Nursing (for clinically qualified care partners)

---

## Minimum Requirements

| Requirement | Frequency |
|-------------|-----------|
| Direct care management activity | At least monthly |
| Minimum duration per contact | 15 minutes |
| Care plan review | At least every 12 months |

---

## Included Activities

Activities that **can be claimed** from care management:

### Care Planning
- Identifying participant needs, goals, preferences
- Reviewing support plans
- Developing and reviewing care plans and quarterly budgets
- Establishing and reviewing service agreements
- Conducting risk assessments

### Service Planning & Management
- Planning and coordinating service delivery
- Communication with participants, carers, registered supporters
- Communication with aged care workers
- Managing the quarterly budget
- Facilitating transitions between care settings

### Monitoring, Review & Evaluation
- Ongoing care discussions and case conferencing
- Internal case conferencing among care partners
- Regular review of care notes
- Monitoring and responding to changing needs
- Risk identification and management
- Evaluating goals and service quality

### Support & Education
- Supporting informed decision-making
- Organising interpreter services
- Providing health promotion information
- Supporting navigation of aged care systems
- Ensuring participant views and rights are heard

---

## Excluded Activities

Activities that **cannot be claimed** from care management:

- Scheduling services
- Creating staff rosters
- Submitting claims
- Staff travel to/from services
- Staff training and education
- SIRS reporting and complaint resolution
- Financial and operational reporting
- Record keeping by non-care-partners
- Human resources activities
- AT-HM services and activities

---

## Quarterly Allocation

### Timing
- Funding credited on first day of each quarter (July, October, January, April)
- Calculated on last day of previous quarter

### Carryover Rules
- Unspent funds roll over within the financial year (unlimited)
- End of financial year: maximum carryover = Q4 allocation amount
- New providers (started Q3/Q4): no carryover limit in first year

---

## Claiming

- Claims submitted in arrears after services delivered
- Must identify: participant, activity date, duration
- Can claim in 15-minute increments (15, 30, 45 minutes, or full hours)
- Only for ongoing services (not Restorative Care or End-of-Life pathways)
