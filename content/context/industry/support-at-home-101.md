## What is Support at Home?

Support at Home is a new, more flexible aged care program replacing Home Care Packages in Australia.

**Key Benefits:**
- More choice and clearer pricing
- Better tailored support than the old HCP system
- 8 tiers of support (vs 4 HCP levels)
- Extra funding for assistive technology and home modifications

---

## Who is Eligible?

Three groups of Australians are eligible:

| Group | Age Requirement |
|-------|-----------------|
| General population | 65+ years |
| Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples | 50+ years |
| People homeless or at risk of homelessness | 50+ years |

Assessments occur through Australia's **Single Assessment System** using the **Integrated Assessment Tool (IAT)**, which gathers information and delivers tailored service recommendations.

Your assessor will collaborate with you to develop a **support plan** based on your needs and goals. This plan determines the funding and services you can receive.

---

## Prioritisation & Wait Times

The program aims to add **300,000 more places** over the next 10 years. By 2035, approximately **1.4 million Australians** will be in care.

### Priority System

Priority ratings are automatically determined based on assessment results:

| Priority | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **High** | Urgent care needs |
| **Medium** | Moderate care needs |
| **Standard** | Lower-level care needs |

### Interim Allocation

When wait times exceed expectations, participants receive an **interim allocation** of **60%** of their budget while waiting for full funding.

---

## Pricing & Funding

### What Stays the Same
- Government continues to pay for health-related costs
- Funds allocated based on individual care needs

### What Changes
- Seniors with financial means contribute more
- Contributions based on services actually received (not flat fees)
- Contribution rate depends on **service type** and **financial standing**

---

## Contribution Rates by Service Type

| Service Category | Description | Contribution Level |
|------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Clinical Supports** | Nursing care, physiotherapy, allied health | **0%** (fully government funded) |
| **Independence** | Personal care, assistive tech, home mods | **Moderate** (5%-50%) |
| **Everyday Living** | Domestic assistance, gardening, cleaning | **Highest** (17.5%-80%) |

---

## Contribution Rates by Pension Status

| Status | Clinical | Independence | Everyday Living |
|--------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Full Pensioner** | 0% | 5% | 17.5% |
| **Part Pensioner** | 0% | 5%–50%* | 17.5%–80%* |
| **Self-funded Retiree (with CSHC)** | 0% | 5%–50%* | 17.5%–80%* |
| **Self-funded Retiree (no CSHC)** | 0% | 50% | 80% |

*Based on income/assets assessment

### Lifetime Cap

A **$130,000 lifetime cap** ensures no individual pays more than this amount in non-clinical care costs (applies to both in-home and residential care).

## Support Classifications

Eight levels of support replace the four HCP levels:

| Classification | Quarterly Budget | Annual Amount |
|----------------|------------------|---------------|
| 1 | ~$2,750 | ~$11,000 |
| 2 | ~$4,000 | ~$16,000 |
| 3 | ~$5,500 | ~$22,000 |
| 4 | ~$7,500 | ~$30,000 |
| 5 | ~$10,000 | ~$40,000 |
| 6 | ~$12,000 | ~$48,000 |
| 7 | ~$14,500 | ~$58,000 |
| 8 | ~$19,500 | ~$78,000 |

Maximum annual funding: **$78,000** (vs $61,400 under HCP Level 4)

## Short-Term Pathways

### Restorative Care Pathway
- **Duration:** 12 weeks (expanded from 8 weeks under STRC)
- **Funding:** ~$6,000 (up to ~$12,000 when eligible)
- **Purpose:** Maintain and improve independence, delay reliance on additional services

### End-of-Life Pathway
- **Duration:** 12 weeks (last 3 months of life)
- **Funding:** ~$25,500
- **Purpose:** Additional services for Australians who prefer to remain at home

---

## Assistive Technology & Home Modifications (AT-HM)

Separate funding for equipment and home modifications to help people remain safely at home.

| Tier | Funding Cap | Time to Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Low** | $500 | 12 months |
| **Medium** | $2,000 | 12 months |
| **High** | $15,000 | 12 months |

AT-HM funding does not accrue over time—use it within the allocation period.

---

## Service Categories

Three categories of services, each with its own contribution arrangements:

### 1. Clinical Care (0% contribution)
- Nursing care
- Allied health services
- Physiotherapy

### 2. Independence (5%-50% contribution)
- Personal care (help with showering, dressing)
- Assistive technology
- Home modifications
- Social support

### 3. Everyday Living (17.5%-80% contribution)
- Domestic assistance
- Gardening
- House cleaning
- Meal services

---

## Quarterly Budgets

Your annual subsidy is divided into **four equal quarterly budgets** (3 months each).

### Rollover Rules
- Unspent funds can roll over between quarters
- Maximum rollover: **$1,000** or **10%** of quarterly budget (whichever is greater)
- Budget held in an account managed by **Services Australia**

---

## Capped Prices

Starting **1 July 2026**, the government sets **price caps** for each service type:

- Providers cannot charge above the cap
- Price caps include all administration costs
- No separate administration fees allowed
- **IHACPA** (Independent Health and Aged Care Pricing Authority) advises on fair pricing

---

## Transition for Existing HCP Recipients

### "No Worse Off" Principle

If on **12 September 2024** you were:
- Receiving a Home Care Package, OR
- On the National Priority System, OR
- Assessed as eligible for a Home Care Package

...you will make the **same or lower contributions** once you transition to Support at Home.

### Existing Recipients
- Those not paying income-tested fees → continue with no fees
- Those paying income-tested fees → special discounted contribution arrangements
- Current funding level retained until reassessed into new classification
