# Participant

> Older Australians receiving funded aged care services through the Support at Home program.

## Definition

Under the Aged Care Act 2024, a **participant** is an older person who has:
1. Been assessed as eligible for Support at Home
2. Been allocated funding
3. Entered into a service agreement with a registered provider

The term "participant" replaced "client" under the new Support at Home (SaH) program that commenced July 1, 2025.

## Demographics

- Generally 65+ years (50+ for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander persons)
- Living at home (not in residential aged care)
- Range of care needs from low (Classification 1) to high (Classification 8)

## Support at Home Funding

### Funding Allocation

Participants receive funding across multiple streams:

| Code | Name | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| **ON** | Ongoing | Primary home support funding (quarterly) |
| **AT** | Assistive Technology | Equipment and devices |
| **CM** | Care Management | Coordination services |
| **EL** | End of Life | Palliative/EOL support |
| **HM** | Home Modifications | Physical home changes |
| **RC** | Restorative Care | Short-term rehabilitation |
| **CU** | Commonwealth Unspent | Rolled-over govt funds |
| **HC** | Home Care Account | Client private funds |
| **VC** | Voluntary Contribution | Additional client payments |

### Service Categories Available

1. **Home Support (SERG-0001)**: Personal care, domestic assistance, social support, meals, transport, allied health
2. **Assistive Technology (SERG-0002)**: Equipment and devices to support independence
3. **Home Modifications (SERG-0003)**: Ramps, grab rails, bathroom modifications
4. **Care Management (SERG-0004)**: Care coordination and planning
5. **End of Life Pathway (SERG-0005)**: Specialized palliative support
6. **Restorative Care Pathway (SERG-0006)**: Short-term intensive rehabilitation

### Fee Structure

For each service delivered, the total package debit includes:
- **Supplier Cost**: What the service provider charges
- **TC Loading Fee (10%)**: Trilogy Care management fee
- **CC Loading Fee (20%)**: Care Coordinator fee (Self-Managed PLUS model only)

Participants can see this breakdown on their monthly statements.

## Goals

- Remain living at home safely and independently
- Maintain quality of life and connection to community
- Access appropriate care and services
- Exercise choice and control over their care
- Achieve personal wellness and reablement goals

## Pain Points

- Navigating the aged care system complexity
- Understanding funding, budgets and contributions
- Finding suitable providers
- Managing changing health needs
- Technology barriers with portals and digital services
- Communicating needs effectively
- Understanding the transition from HCP to SaH

## Diverse Needs Considerations

Participants may include those who are:
- Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander
- From culturally and linguistically diverse backgrounds
- LGBTIQ+
- Living with disability or cognitive impairment
- In rural, remote or very remote areas
- Financially or socially disadvantaged
- Experiencing or at risk of homelessness
- Veterans or war widows

All services must meet:
- **Aged Care Quality Standards** (8 standards)
- **Charter of Aged Care Rights**
- **Serious Incident Response Scheme (SIRS)** requirements

## Decision-Making Capacity

- Presumed to have decision-making capacity
- May have registered supporters to assist
- May have appointed decision-makers (guardianship, power of attorney)
- Capacity may fluctuate day-to-day

## Funding Journey

1. Register with My Aged Care (Services Australia)
2. Complete screening assessment
3. Undergo comprehensive aged care assessment (IAT)
4. Receive Notice of Decision and Support Plan
5. Enter Support at Home Priority System
6. Allocated funding (full or interim)
7. Choose provider (Trilogy Care) and enter service agreement
8. Receive services
9. Monthly statements showing service delivery and costs

### Quarterly Budget Cycle

- Funding allocated per **13-week quarters** (Q1-Q4)
- Budget reviewed quarterly based on needs
- **Underspend alerts** sent at 33%, 66%, 85% through quarter
- Automatic rollover by Services Australia (60 days grace period)

## Interaction Channels

- **Phone** (primary for many participants)
- **TC Portal** (web-based self-service)
- **Consumer mobile app** (viewing and requests)
- **In-person visits** (assessments, care planning)
- **Written correspondence** (statements, notices)
