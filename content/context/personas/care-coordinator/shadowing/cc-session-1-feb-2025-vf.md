# Shadowing Session: Van, Care Coordinator

## Context

Most of his clients were recently transferred to him, so he's still learning their histories and needs. During the session he was juggling incoming calls, bill processing, and a backlog of emails. He was also cleaning up old tasks he'd forgotten about and handling questions he didn't yet have answers for.

### Daily Structure

- **Morning:** Bills processing – aims to get through as many as possible before lunch.
- **Midday:** Client calls.
- **Afternoon:** Emails and follow-ups.
- **After 4pm:** No new calls (they can run 1–2 hours).
- **Finish:** Leaves at 4:30pm (starts early).

### Tools Used (Van's estimate)

| Tool | % of Time |
|------|-----------|
| Portal | 50% |
| CRM | 30% |
| CV | 10% |
| Monday | 5% |
| Other (Teams, Email) | 5% |

### Daily Tasks

What Van is typically doing across a day:

- Processing and activating budgets.
- Managing bills (including evidence and notes).
- Handling client calls and call outcomes.
- Managing client transfers and new allocations via Monday.
- Writing and attaching notes and evidence (emails, call recordings).
- Supporting recipient portal questions where he can (login, visibility).
- Coordinating coverage when others are away – mostly by reading Damien's morning email.

## Key Insights

### Triple-click copy/paste is a small but constant friction
Selecting key details like date of birth via triple-click behaves inconsistently. Van called this a "quick win" – minor annoyance that adds up over the day.

### Budget activation can fail with inconsistent validation
Intermittent validation errors when activating budgets, especially around certain amounts (e.g. >$1,000). Same input can pass on a different day, which reduces confidence.

### Draft budgets lose context when navigating away
When he drafts a budget then switches to Bills/Notes and comes back, the Portal often shows the wrong quarter or jumps back to the active budget. This leads to multiple drafts from the same base budget and confusion about where changes went.

### Impersonate exists but is poorly understood and underused
The impersonate feature "just appeared" without explanation or training. Van rarely uses it and doesn't know who should support portal login issues.

### Refreshing while impersonating breaks the flow
Refreshing the page while impersonating logs him out of that state. The Stop impersonating control isn't obvious, so the exit path is confusing.

### Note categories are required but not clearly enforced, causing data loss
Notes need a category before they can be saved. If he writes a long note and navigates away without a category, the content is lost. He has lost incident details this way, which increases defensive behaviours.

### Call categories are often chosen at random under time pressure
Outcome categories on calls are perceived as low value and time-consuming. Van often just clicks "whatever one" because he's too busy. He suggested favouriting frequently-used categories (e.g. Coordination).

### Monday task reordering is glitchy
Dragging items between columns or up the list can cause rows to "jump" to the bottom. This is a known annoyance and slows down new client transfer and workflow management.

### Coverage visibility is manual and fragile
Coverage for absent team members is usually communicated via Damien's morning email. For longer absences (e.g. 2 weeks), it's easy for people to forget who is covering whom, leading to uncertainty about who to contact.

### Budget carry-over amounts are invisible to coordinators
For home support packages, 10% or $1,000 (whichever is greater) rolls over. There's no clear indicator in the Portal showing how much actually carried over, which makes it hard to answer client questions on the spot.

### EPOA (Enduring Power of Attorney) is scattered and inconsistently surfaced
EPOA documents typically live as attachments in the CRM, but not everyone attaches them. EPOA isn't clearly surfaced in the Portal care circle, forcing users to jump systems.

### Goals section feels arbitrary and under-utilised
The goals feature is unstructured free text ("maintain independence" etc). It feels generic and doesn't meaningfully support coordination decisions, so it's often ignored.

### Process documentation is outdated and erodes trust
Training and process docs are known to be out of date. This reduces trust in written guidance and pushes people back to ad-hoc workarounds and asking peers.

## Pain Points

### Unreliable budget workflows
- Draft budgets lose context when navigating between tabs.
- Quarter display bugs cause confusion and duplicate drafts.
- Intermittent validation failures on activation (< / > $1,000 rules).

### Data-loss risk in notes and calls
- Mandatory categories without clear guardrails cause silent note loss.
- Long notes can disappear on navigation, leading to rework and frustration.
- Call categories are effectively "noise" because they're chosen at random.

### Impersonation and portal support gap
- Impersonate is poorly surfaced and not explained.
- Refreshing while impersonating breaks the flow and forces a reset.
- Coordinators don't know who owns portal login support for recipients.

### Fragmented view of client authority and context
- EPOA is hidden in CRM attachments and not reliably maintained.
- Goals are generic and unstructured, adding little value at the point of care.

### Limited visibility into financial nuances
- No clear UI for budget carry-over amounts (10% / $1,000 rule).
- Hard to give immediate, confident answers to recipient questions about rollover.

### Everyday UX friction in tools
- Triple-click selection for fields like DOB is inconsistent and irritating.
- Drag-and-drop bug causes row jumping and slows task management.
- Outdated process docs mean coordinators rely more on memory and peers than on the documented source of truth.

## Observed Behaviours & Workarounds

### Over-creation of budget drafts
When draft budgets lose their context or show the wrong quarter, coordinators create new drafts rather than untangling the existing ones.

### Defensive note-taking behaviours
For major items (complaints, terminations, assessments, AR issues), Van attaches evidence directly:
- Drags email files into notes or copy/pastes email content.
- Attaches call recordings for significant conversations.

This is both good practice and a response to fear of losing context.

### Ad-hoc category usage
Call outcome categories are often treated as a checkbox to satisfy the system, not as meaningful data. Categories are clicked quickly or randomly due to workload.

### Minimal use of impersonate
Occasionally used to see invoices or "what the client sees." Not used as a standard workflow because it's poorly understood and brittle on refresh.

### Manual coverage discovery
People scan Damien's morning email to figure out who is away and who is covering. Over time, this becomes unreliable, and people just "ask around."

### Workarounds for external tool quirks
Row jumping leads to extra drag operations and re-sorting. Coordinators accept this as "just how it is" but it adds time and frustration.

## Ideas / Opportunities

### Make copy/paste interactions predictable
Fix or simplify triple-click behaviour on key fields (DOB, IDs) to make selection predictable.

### Stabilise budget experiences
- Preserve draft budget context when switching tabs – always bring the user back to the same draft and quarter.
- Make quarter state obvious and prevent accidental creation of duplicate drafts.
- Resolve intermittent validation errors and clarify rules around large values.

### Protect notes by design
- Auto-save notes as you type (even before category selection).
- Add clear "Unsaved changes" warnings when navigating away.
- Visually flag missing categories before allowing the user to leave.

### Make impersonation and portal help safe and understandable
- Surface a clear, persistent "Stop impersonating" control.
- Provide lightweight guidance on when and how to use impersonate.
- Clarify the support path for client portal login/password issues.

### Improve call and note categorisation
- Allow favouriting of high-value categories (e.g. "Coordination") for quick access.
- Consider reducing or refocusing categories to the few that are genuinely useful downstream.

### Surface authority and carry-over clearly in the Portal
- Bring EPOA into the Portal care circle in a dedicated, reliable field, drawing from CRM where possible.
- Explicitly show budget carry-over amounts on relevant budget screens so coordinators can answer questions in-call.

### Tighten the goals feature
- Move from generic free text to a more structured, actionable goals model (e.g. categories, timeframes, link to plans).
- Make it clear how goals influence coordination decisions.
