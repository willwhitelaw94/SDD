# Care Partner

> Case management and oversight role delivering approvals, budget oversight, and complex case decisions.

## Definition

Care Partners deliver case management functions under Support at Home. They work at a higher level than Care Coordinators, with responsibility for approvals, budget oversight, and complex case decisions.

## Key Distinction

| Aspect | Care Partner | Care Coordinator |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Focus** | Case management | Day-to-day operations |
| **Approvals** | Budget, unplanned services | Task execution |
| **Budget Role** | Creates & approves budgets | Works within approved budgets |
| **Decision Authority** | High-level decisions | Operational execution |
| **Team Scope** | Oversight role | Individual caseload |

## Role Levels

| Level | Responsibilities |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Care Partner** | Case management, approvals, budget oversight |
| **Senior Care Partner** | Oversight of care partners, complex case management, mentoring |

## Role Responsibilities

### Case Management & Oversight
- Approve budget modifications and service changes
- Make complex case decisions
- Oversee care coordinator activities
- Ensure compliance with regulatory requirements
- Participate in Task Management oversight and approval

### Budget Management
- Approve budget allocations across service groups (SERGs)
- Review and approve unplanned service expenditure
- Monitor budget utilisation against classification level (1-8)
- Approve loading fee adjustments
- Ensure total payments do NOT exceed funding stream balance
- Monitor "cash in the bank" across all funding streams (ON, RC, EL, CU, HC, VC)

### Unplanned Service Approvals

**Critical function**: Approving services that are in the Notice of Assessment (NOA) but NOT included in the active service plan/budget.

#### Approval Workflow

```
Invoice → Bill Processor → No Match Found → Flag as Unplanned
                                                   ↓
                                          Check Amount & NOA
                                                   ↓
                 ┌────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────┐
                 ↓                                ↓                                ↓
         Under $300 Threshold        Over $300 Threshold         Not in NOA
         (Minor Unplanned)           (Care Partner Required)     (Reject)
                 ↓                                ↓
         BPO may process              CARE PARTNER REVIEWS
         if funding available
                                      → Verify service approved in NOA
                                      → Confirm service was delivered
                                      → Check funding available
                                      → APPROVE or REJECT
```

#### Decision Points

When approving unplanned services, Care Partners must decide:

1. **Map to Existing Planned Service** - Link to similar service if appropriate
2. **Process as Unplanned** - Deduct from funding stream balance
3. **Reject** - If inappropriate or not approved in NOA

**Business Rules**:
- Unplanned services **over $300 MUST have Care Partner approval** - Bill Processing Officers cannot approve these
- All approvals based on **NOA verification** - Service must be approved in Notice of Assessment
- **Funding stream balance enforcement** - Cannot exceed available funds
- **Documentation required** - All decisions and rationale must be documented

### Budget Reconciliation & Planning

- Resolve unplanned services by mapping or processing appropriately
- Update service plan if pattern of unplanned services emerges
- Monitor active vs. draft budget versions
- Identify significant unplanned amounts accumulating
- Trigger creation of updated service plan when needed
- Ensure service plan stays aligned with actual service delivery

### Care Management Activities

Care Partners oversee compliance in care management activities:

| Metric | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Minimum direct care** | 15 minutes per client per month |
| **Average target** | 2.5 hours monthly, 6 hours quarterly |
| **Activity types** | Time spent directly with/for clients (calls, emails, coordination) |

### Advocacy and Conflict Resolution
In case of disputes or conflicts, Care Partners act as advocates and mediators. They facilitate effective communication, address grievances, and ensure participant rights are protected.

## Goals

- Effective case oversight
- Budget compliance and optimisation
- Complex case resolution
- Care coordinator support
- Timely unplanned service approvals

## Pain Points

- High volume of unplanned service approvals
- Balancing budget flexibility with funding constraints
- Service plans becoming outdated vs. actual delivery
- Coordinating with Bill Processing team on holds
- Supplier invoice quality issues

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| **Unplanned service approval turnaround** | Response time for bills on hold |
| **Budget accuracy** | Planned vs. actual variance |
| **Service plan update frequency** | How often budgets updated |
| **Care management compliance** | Minutes per client per month |

## Qualifications

No mandatory qualifications, though valued credentials include:
- Certificate III/IV in Individual Support, Aged Care, Disability, or Community Services
- Diploma of Nursing, Community Services, or Ageing Studies
- Bachelor degrees in Social Work, Physiotherapy, or Nursing
