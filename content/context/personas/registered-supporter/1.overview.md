# Registered Supporter

> Family members, carers, friends, or advocates who support older people with aged care decisions.

## Definition

Under the Aged Care Act 2024, a **registered supporter** is a person formally registered through My Aged Care to support an older person. They help the older person make and communicate their own decisions about aged care services.

## Key Principle

Registered supporters **do not** have decision-making authority. Their role is to **support** the older person to make their own decisions, not to make decisions for them.

## Types of Registered Supporters

### Support-Only
- Registered to help with communication and information
- No legal decision-making authority
- Most common type

### With Legal Authority
Some registered supporters also hold:
- **Guardianship** under state/territory law
- **Enduring Power of Attorney**
- Other court-appointed decision-making authority

These people can make decisions on behalf of the older person within their legal authority.

## Role Responsibilities

- Help older person understand aged care options
- Assist with communicating preferences and needs
- Support attendance at assessments and reviews
- Access information about the older person's care
- Communicate with providers on behalf of (not instead of) the older person
- Help navigate My Aged Care and provider systems

## Goals

- Ensure their loved one receives quality care
- Stay informed about care and services
- Be involved in important decisions
- Advocate for the older person's wishes
- Reduce stress and complexity for the participant

## Pain Points

- Unclear boundaries of their role
- Not receiving timely information
- Difficulty reaching coordinators
- Understanding complex funding/budgets
- Balancing involvement with participant autonomy
- Technology barriers (especially for older supporters)
- Geographic distance from participant

## Needs from TC Portal

### Information Access
- View participant's care plan
- See upcoming services and appointments
- Access budget and spending information
- Receive notifications about changes

### Communication
- Easy contact with care coordinators
- Updates on service delivery
- Involvement in reviews (with participant consent)
- Clear escalation paths for concerns

### Transparency
- Understand what services are being provided
- Know who to contact for different issues
- See invoices and statements

## Legal Considerations

### Consent Required
- Older person must consent to registering a supporter
- Exception: appointed decision-makers with active legal authority

### Information Sharing
- Providers must respect the older person's privacy
- Information sharing depends on participant consent
- Registered supporters may be authorised to receive information

### Autonomy Preserved
- Having a registered supporter doesn't remove the older person's rights
- Older person can still make their own decisions
- Provider should always go directly to participant for decisions

## Interaction with Providers

Providers must:
- Continue engaging directly with the older person
- Recognise who registered supporters are
- Understand the supporter's role (support vs decision-making)
- Respect changing capacity day-to-day
- Include supporters in discussions when requested

## Typical Profiles

### Adult Child
- Often lives separately
- May work full-time
- Coordinates care remotely
- Primary contact for emergencies

### Spouse/Partner
- Lives with participant
- Involved in daily care
- May need support themselves
- Often older themselves

### Professional Advocate
- Formal advocacy role
- May work with OPAN
- Focused on rights and compliance
