## Sales & Onboarding (Internal)

Our Sales and Marketing team focuses on truth and transparency in promoting our self-management and enhanced self-management models under Support at Home.

### Customer Acquisition
The Customer Service Representative (CSR) actively engages in prospecting and lead generation to identify potential participants who may benefit from self-managed home care services. This includes:
- Conducting outreach campaigns
- Attending community events
- Networking with healthcare professionals
- Leveraging online platforms

### Collaboration with Care Coordinators
The CSR collaborates with Care Coordinators to ensure a smooth transition from sales to service delivery. They communicate participant needs, preferences, and specific instructions to facilitate seamless coordination and a personalised care experience.

### Relationship Building
The CSR builds strong relationships with participants, establishing trust and rapport. They serve as an initial point of contact, ensuring participants feel supported and valued. This includes regular check-ins, addressing inquiries, and providing ongoing assistance.

### Other Key Functions
- Initial screening of participant suitability for self-management
- Ensuring participants understand the self-management model
- Identification of authorised and nominated representatives (Registered Supporters)
- Explanation of Support at Home financial arrangements, fees and charges (including loading fees and participant contributions)
- Establishment of a Home Care Agreement
- Explanation of the Charter of Aged Care Rights

*Our Intake Team plays an important role in assessing the suitability of prospective participants for self-management. Participants not deemed suitable for self-management are offered our enhanced model of self-management.*

## Assessment Partner (Internal)

### Assessment and Care Planning
The Assessment Partner conducts an initial assessment to understand the individual's needs, preferences, and goals. They work closely with the participant to develop a comprehensive Support Plan that outlines the specific services and supports required.

As well as qualitative goal-setting, the Assessment Partner is involved in setting the initial quarterly budget and allocating available funds across service groups (SERGs).

*Our Assessment Team works closely with our Clinical Team to ensure prospective participants are suitable for self-management. They consist of suitably qualified clinical and non-clinical assessors.*

## Care Partner (Internal)

Care Partners deliver case management and oversight functions under Support at Home. They work at a higher level than Care Coordinators, with responsibility for approvals, budget oversight, and complex case decisions.

### Role Levels

| Level | Responsibilities |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Care Partner** | Case management, approvals, budget oversight, complex decisions |
| **Senior Care Partner** | Oversight of care partners, complex case management, mentoring |

### Care Management Activities

Under Support at Home, care management is funded through a pooled model (10% of participant quarterly budgets). Care Partners deliver these activities:

**Case Management & Oversight**
- Approve budget modifications and service changes
- Make complex case decisions
- Oversee care coordinator activities
- Ensure compliance with regulatory requirements

**Budget Management**
- Approve budget allocations across service groups (SERGs)
- Review and approve unplanned service expenditure
- Monitor budget utilisation against classification level (1-8)
- Approve loading fee adjustments

**Advocacy and Conflict Resolution**
In case of disputes or conflicts between the participant and caregivers, the Care Partner acts as an advocate and mediator. They facilitate effective communication, address grievances, and ensure participant rights and well-being are protected.

**Evaluation and Continuous Improvement**
Care Partners evaluate the effectiveness of self-managed home care. They gather feedback from participants, caregivers, and other stakeholders to identify areas for improvement.

### Qualifications

No mandatory qualifications, though valued credentials include:
- Certificate III/IV in Individual Support, Aged Care, Disability, or Community Services
- Diploma of Nursing, Community Services, or Ageing Studies
- Bachelor degrees in Social Work, Physiotherapy, or Nursing (for clinically qualified care partners)

## Care Coordinator (Internal)

Care Coordinators handle day-to-day participant interactions and frontline coordination activities. They work under the oversight of Care Partners, focusing on regular participant contact and operational tasks.

### Key Responsibilities

**Daily Operations**
- Manage participant caseload
- Conduct regular participant calls
- Log notes and update participant records
- Complete assigned tasks within SLAs

**Ongoing Support and Monitoring**
Care Coordinators maintain regular communication with participants to address concerns, provide guidance, and offer ongoing support. They conduct periodic reviews to:
- Assess the effectiveness of the Support Plan
- Ensure participant well-being
- Monitor quality and safety of services provided by suppliers
- Address any emerging needs or challenges

*Regulatory requirement: Minimum 15 minutes direct care contact per month with each participant.*

**Service Coordination**
- Help participants navigate available resources and services
- Provide information on caregiver recruitment, training programs, and financial management tools
- Coordinate with suppliers for service delivery
- Escalate complex issues to Care Partners

**Financial Guidance**
Care Coordinators help participants understand their Support at Home finances:
- Explain quarterly funding allocation across service groups
- Assist with budget planning aligned with classification level
- Explain loading fees (10% Trilogy Care, 20% participant contribution where applicable)

*Participant self-assessment is a key aspect of our approach to service planning in our self-management model; it enables participants to self-assess their needs and specify their goals, while identifying risks and issues for which support may be required.*

*The Care Coordination Team has a high level of knowledge about Support at Home inclusions and exclusions across service groups (SERGs) to support participants in self-management while remaining within the regulatory framework.*

*The Care Coordination Team works closely with the Clinical Team, referring participants identified as having clinical risk factors and/or complex clinical care needs.*

## Clinical Nurse (Internal)

Our Clinical Team comprises AHPRA-registered nurses and allied health practitioners focused on working with the Care Coordination Team to support participants in making informed decisions about clinical risks and clinical care.

### Key Responsibilities

**Incident Response**
In the event of a serious incident or adverse event, the nurse follows established protocols for incident response. They assess the situation, initiate appropriate interventions, and report the incident to relevant authorities as required by regulations and the Serious Incident Response Scheme (SIRS).

**Clinical Escalation**
The nurse plays a crucial role in identifying signs of clinical deterioration or serious incidents. If a participant's condition worsens or there is a medical emergency, the nurse initiates appropriate clinical escalation procedures, which may include:
- Contacting emergency services
- Coordinating hospital transfers
- Seeking immediate medical intervention

**Collaboration with Healthcare Professionals**
The nurse maintains regular communication with the participant's primary care physician, specialists, and other healthcare professionals. They provide updates on the participant's condition, discuss concerns or changes, and seek guidance on clinical management.

**Dignity of Risk**
Our clinical risk management ensures participants can make informed decisions about clinical care. Consistent with consumer-directed care and dignity of risk principles, we make recommendations about care but respect each participant's right to choose their home care.

**Infection Control**
Our Clinical Team plays an important role in infection prevention and control, clinical incident management, and risk management, including mandatory reporting under the Serious Incident Response Scheme.

## Bill Processing Officer (Internal)

Bill Processing Officers (BPOs) handle the complete lifecycle of supplier invoices from receipt through payment. They distinguish between planned services (budgeted in active service plans) and unplanned services (approved but not budgeted), routing each through appropriate workflows.

### Key Responsibilities

**Invoice Receipt & Verification**
- Review incoming invoices for completeness
- Verify supplier details and invoice reference
- Check for required documentation
- Detect duplicate invoice submissions

**Bill Item Matching**
- Match invoice line items to service plan items using: supplier, rate, date, service type
- Accept "close enough" matches within policy tolerances (±5% rate variance)
- Utilize AI-powered matching recommendations
- Document matching decisions and rationale

**Planned Service Processing**
- Process bills for planned services within budget directly (delegated authority)
- Verify sufficient funding exists in funding stream
- Ensure service dates fall within planned period
- Confirm rates match published service plan

**Unplanned Service Identification**
- Flag bill items that cannot be matched to planned services
- Check NOA (Notice of Assessment) for service approval
- Process minor unplanned services under threshold ($300) directly if within funding
- Place larger unplanned services on hold for Care Partner approval

**Accuracy & Compliance**
- Verify rates against published service plan
- Check supplier is authorized for the service type
- Ensure funding stream eligibility rules followed
- Use correct workflow for planned vs. unplanned

### Approval Authority

| Can Approve | Requires Care Partner |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Planned services within budget | Unplanned services over threshold |
| Planned services over budget BUT within funding stream | Services not approved in NOA |
| Unplanned services under $300 within funding | Questionable matches or discrepancies |

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Bills processed daily | 90 bills/person (5 min each) |
| Processing time | 5 minutes per bill |
| Matching accuracy | High (monitored) |
| Error/rework rate | Low (monitored) |

### Portal Access
- Bill processing interface with matching UI
- Service plan item selector
- Funding stream balance visibility
- Hold reason documentation

## Collections Team (Internal)

The Collections Team manages direct debit authorisation, fee payer collection, and accounts receivable for participant voluntary contributions. They work with the Easy Collect platform and coordinate AR invoice integration with MYOB.

### Key Responsibilities

**Direct Debit Management**
- Process DD authority forms from fee payers
- Run 152 validation checks on each DD form
- Submit validated forms to Easy Collect platform
- Manage 15-30 day bank activation period
- Track DD status across fee payer accounts

**Fee Payer Management**
- Identify and set up fee payers for packages
- Verify fee payer details match bank account holder
- Follow up on unreturned DD forms
- Manage fee payer communication

**Collection Processing**
- Schedule and submit direct debit collections
- Handle failed collection retries and escalation
- Credit voluntary contribution (VC) funding streams on successful collection
- Process ITF (Incoming Trust Fund) payments

## Participant / Registered Supporter

### Participant
The participant is the person receiving Support at Home funded services. Under Support at Home, participants are older Australians (65+, or 50+ for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples) assessed as eligible for government-funded aged care.

### Registered Supporter
The Registered Supporter (previously "recipient representative" or "authorised representative") is someone the participant nominates to act on their behalf or assist with decision-making. This may include family members, carers, or advocates.

Registered Supporters can have varying levels of access and authority as defined by the participant, from view-only access to full decision-making authority.

## Support Workers / Service Providers (External)

In a self-managed context, support workers play a crucial role in assisting participants with their care needs and providing direct support in the home setting.

### Service Types

**Personal Care**
Support workers assist participants with activities of daily living: bathing, grooming, dressing, toileting, and mobility support. They ensure personal hygiene needs are met and promote independence in self-care.

**Household Support**
Support workers assist with light housekeeping, meal preparation, and grocery shopping, ensuring the living environment is clean, organised, and conducive to well-being. They may also provide support with laundry, pet care, and other household chores.

**Medication Management**
Support workers may help with medication reminders and assist participants in adhering to their prescribed medication regimen. They ensure medications are taken as directed, document administration, and report any concerns to appropriate healthcare professionals.

**Companionship and Social Support**
Support workers provide companionship, engaging in conversation and offering emotional support. They may accompany participants on outings, provide social engagement, and help alleviate feelings of loneliness or isolation.

**Assistance with Mobility and Transfers**
Support workers assist with mobility and transfers, including using mobility aids such as walkers or wheelchairs. They ensure safety during transfers and help maintain mobility and independence.

**Transport and Appointment Assistance**
Support workers may provide transport to medical appointments, grocery shopping, or other necessary outings. They accompany participants to appointments, ensuring comfort and assisting with paperwork or communication with healthcare professionals.

### Key Considerations

**Record-Keeping and Reporting**
Support workers maintain accurate records of care provided, including observations, changes in condition, and incidents or concerns. They communicate these to the participant, their family, and appropriate healthcare professionals.

**Collaboration with the Care Team**
Support workers collaborate with the participant's care team, which may include nurses, Care Coordinators, therapists, and other healthcare professionals. They participate in care team meetings, provide progress updates, and communicate changes or concerns.

**Following Support Plans**
Support workers adhere to the participant's Support Plan, following specific instructions and providing care that respects preferences and choices. They work closely with the participant to understand unique needs and adapt support accordingly.

**Emergency Response**
Support workers are trained in basic first aid and emergency response protocols. They handle emergency situations promptly, ensuring participant safety until further medical assistance arrives.

*The specific responsibilities of support workers vary depending on the participant's needs, the self-managed care arrangement, and applicable regulations. The primary goal is to provide direct care and assistance, promoting well-being, independence, and quality of life.*

## Other Vendors

### Allied Health Providers

**Occupational Therapist**
An occupational therapist (OT) supports participants to maintain independence, improve daily functioning, and enhance well-being in their homes.

- **Comprehensive Assessments:** Evaluates functional abilities, cognitive skills, and environmental factors
- **Identifying Barriers and Goals:** Collaborates with participants to set personalised goals and prioritise interventions
- **Home Safety Assessment:** Assesses the home environment for safety risks and recommends modifications (grab bars, ramps, stairlifts, furniture rearrangement)

**Physiotherapist**
Provides physical therapy services to maintain or improve mobility, strength, and physical function.

**Other Allied Health**
Includes speech pathologists, dietitians, podiatrists, and other specialists as required by the participant's Support Plan.

### Equipment and Supplies

**Equipment Providers**
Supply assistive devices, mobility aids, and medical equipment (wheelchairs, walkers, specialised beds, bathroom safety equipment). They ensure participants have appropriate equipment tailored to specific needs under the Assistive Technology (AT) funding stream.

**Aides Supply Companies**
Provide consumable products, personal care items, and household essentials (incontinence supplies, wound care materials, nutritional supplements, cleaning supplies).

**Pharmacies**
Supply prescribed medications, over-the-counter drugs, and pharmaceutical products. They provide medication counselling, dosage management, and prescription refills.

## External Care Partner - Self-Managed Plus Only

> **Note:** This is distinct from the internal Care Partner role. An External Care Partner is an optional third-party service for Self-Managed Plus participants.

An External Care Partner is an optional stakeholder for participants who have signed up for Trilogy Care's Self-Managed Plus product. They serve as a vital link between participants, their families, and all other stakeholders in the Trilogy Care 'Care Circle'.

### Key Responsibilities

**Coordination of Services**
Responsible for coordinating services and resources to implement the Support Plan, including arranging home healthcare providers, scheduling appointments, coordinating therapy sessions, and ensuring timely delivery of equipment or supplies.

**Communication and Collaboration**
Serves as a central point of contact for participants, families, and the healthcare team. Facilitates effective communication among all parties, ensuring everyone is informed about the Support Plan, progress, and any changes.

**Monitoring and Evaluation**
Continually monitors progress and evaluates Support Plan effectiveness. Conducts regular assessments, reviews documentation, and collects feedback to identify improvements and make necessary adjustments.

**Advocacy and Support**
Advocates for participant needs and rights, ensures access to appropriate and timely care, assists with navigating the healthcare system, and addresses barriers or challenges. Provides emotional support and guidance throughout the care process.

## Finance Team (Internal)

Our Finance Team ensures home care financial matters are managed in accordance with Support at Home legislation, including fees and charges, accounts management, statements, unspent funds, and participant transfers.

### Funding Management

**Quarterly Budget Management**
Manages the government-funded Support at Home budgets allocated to eligible participants by classification level (1-8). Ensures allocated funding is appropriately utilised to meet care needs as outlined in the Support Plan.

**Monitoring Fees and Charges**
Tracks all fees and charges associated with the Support at Home budget, including:
- Loading fees (10% Trilogy Care, 20% participant contribution)
- Service provider rates
- Participant contributions by service category

Reviews invoices and expenditure to verify accuracy and compliance.

**Generating Statements**
Prepares regular statements for participants detailing:
- Services provided
- Associated costs by funding stream (ON, AT, CM, HM, etc.)
- Remaining balance of quarterly funds
- Upcoming budget allocations

**Supporting Participants with Expenditure**
Assists participants in understanding expenditure and budgeting funds effectively. Provides guidance on optimising fund utilisation, discussing service adjustments or alternative providers if necessary.

**Managing Funds for Participant Transfer**
Manages financial aspects of transfers between providers. Coordinates transfer of unspent funds (within the 60-day grace period) ensuring smooth transition and appropriate utilisation for ongoing care.

**Financial Record-Keeping**
Maintains detailed financial records including all transactions, fees, charges, invoices, reimbursements, and transfers. Ensures transparency, accountability, and compliance.

**Financial Reporting**
Prepares financial reports providing overview of Support at Home budget status, including expenditure summaries, budget utilisation by SERG, and financial projections.

### Accounts Receivable

**Participant Contribution Collection**
Manages collection of participant contributions based on means testing and service category. Communicates fee structure and payment requirements, calculates fees based on guidelines, and manages invoicing and collection.

**Payment Arrangements**
Works with participants who require assistance meeting payment obligations. Helps set up suitable payment arrangements and provides information about available financial support options.

### Accounts Payable

**Bill Processing**
Processes and pays bills from service providers and suppliers. Verifies invoice accuracy, matches against services provided, and ensures timely payments.

**Reviewing Workers' Bills**
Reviews bills from support workers providing services. Verifies billed services align with agreed rates, terms, and authorised hours.

**Budget Reconciliation**
Compares received bills against budgeted expenditure by funding stream. Ensures costs are within allocated quarterly budget without unexpected variances.

**Cash Flow Management**
Monitors cash flow to ensure sufficient funds for bill payments. Tracks budget utilisation and projected expenditure to maintain financial stability.
