Home Modifications (HM) are changes to the home environment that make it safer and more accessible for aged care recipients.

---

## What Are Home Modifications?

Physical changes to a person's home that support their ability to live independently and safely. Unlike assistive technology (equipment), home modifications are permanent or semi-permanent structural changes.

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Safety modifications** | Grab rails, non-slip flooring, improved lighting |
| **Access modifications** | Ramps, widened doorways, stair lifts |
| **Bathroom modifications** | Walk-in showers, raised toilets, shower seats |
| **Kitchen modifications** | Lowered benches, accessible storage |

---

## Funding Under Support at Home

Home modifications have their own funding stream under the AT-HM scheme, separate from ongoing care services.

### Funding Tiers

| Tier | Amount | Period | Notes |
|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Low** | Up to $500 | 12 months | Minor modifications (grab rails, non-slip mats) |
| **Medium** | Up to $2,000 | 12 months | Standard modifications |
| **High** | Up to $15,000 | 12 months (extendable to 24) | **$15,000 LIFETIME CAP** |

### Key Rules

- **12-month spending period** — Funds must be SPENT within 12 months
- **Lifetime cap** — High-tier home modifications have a $15,000 lifetime limit
- **24-month extension** — Complex modifications can get a 12-month extension with evidence
- **OT prescription required** — Medium and high-tier modifications need Occupational Therapist assessment

## Common Home Modifications

### Bathroom

- Walk-in/roll-in showers
- Grab rails near toilet and shower
- Non-slip flooring
- Raised toilet seats
- Shower chairs/benches

### Access & Mobility

- Entrance ramps
- Handrails on stairs
- Widened doorways
- Stair lifts
- Threshold ramps

### General Safety

- Improved lighting
- Non-slip surfaces
- Lever door handles
- Emergency response systems

## The Process

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[Need identified] --> B[OT Assessment]
    B --> C[Report & recommendations]
    C --> D[Quotes obtained]
    D --> E[Added to budget]
    E --> F[Work completed]
    F --> G[Invoice processed]
```

1. **Need identified** — Care Partner or client identifies home safety concern
2. **OT Assessment** — Occupational Therapist assesses home and recommends modifications
3. **Quotes** — Builder quotes obtained for recommended work
4. **Budget** — Modifications added to AT-HM budget allocation
5. **Work completed** — Approved contractor completes modifications
6. **Billing** — Invoice processed against AT-HM funding

---

## Coordination Fee Caps

| Type | Cap |
|------|-----|
| Home Modifications Coordination | 15% of quoted cost OR $1,500 (whichever lower) |

---

## What's NOT Covered

- General home renovations
- Normal maintenance and repairs
- Changes unrelated to care needs
- Cosmetic improvements
